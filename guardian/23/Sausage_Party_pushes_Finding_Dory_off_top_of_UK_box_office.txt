
Sausage Party 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/aug/10/sausage-party-review-seth-rogen>, 
Seth Rogen’s animation about horny, hard-talking supermarket products who 
discover the truth about their fate beyond the store, has topped the UK box 
office on its first week of release.

It took £2.7m, enough to move former No 1 Finding Dory 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/jul/28/finding-dory-review-pixar-nemo-ellen-degeneres>

biggest release of the year (behind Disney’s The Jungle Book).

Noel Clarke’s Brotherhood 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/aug/25/brotherhood-review-silly-gangster-porn-version-of-kids-tv>
 was another new opener to perform strongly, taking nearly £2m, with an 
especially impressive site average of £4,579. MeanwhileCafé Society

<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/01/things-to-come-review-isabelle-huppert-in-exquisite-agony>

The BFG  
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/may/14/the-bfg-review-mark-rylance-steven-spieberg-roald-dahl>

enjoying only minimal falloff in attendance included Mila Kunis comedyBad Moms 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/jul/28/bad-moms-review-mila-kunis-kathryn-hahn-kristen-bell-the-hangover>
, which fell only 5% week-on-week andNine Lives 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/aug/05/nine-lives-review-kevin-spacey-christopher-walken>

third week.
