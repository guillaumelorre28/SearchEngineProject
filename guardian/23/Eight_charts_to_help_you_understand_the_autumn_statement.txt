 Government spending deficit 
A shortfall between government spending and income that reached £155bn in the 
aftermath of the banking crash was forecast in March this year to fall to 
£55.5bn in 2016-17. TheOffice for Budget Responsibility 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/office-for-budget-responsibility> said 
its forecast was optimistic and it will now be £68bn.

 General government investment growth 
The allocation of more cash for road maintenance, digital infrastructure, and 
research and development has reversed cuts in government investment spending 
that formed a large part of the previous chancellor George Osborne’s last 
austerity budget.
Departmental spending 
<https://interactive.guim.co.uk/uploader/embed/2016/11/whitehall_spending-zip/giv-15726gnoFs0FEraY4>
 Departmental spending 
After a deep cut in 2014 followed by a boost last year, departments have been 
spared further austerity. But with the NHS under extreme pressure and budget 
reductions for unprotected departments still in place, the squeeze on the state 
will continue well into the next parliament.

 Welfare spending 
A cap on welfare spending was lifted slightly after an easing of universal 
credit cuts. The overall bill is expected to continue rising. However, pensions 
costs, which are outside the cap, are to be kept in check from 2018.
GDP growth 
<https://interactive.guim.co.uk/uploader/embed/2016/11/forecast-gdp-growth-zip/giv-157267wsjrB5G6cya>
 GDP growth 
Britain’s national income this year is expected to be slightly higher than the 
forecast in March predicted, but downgrades for the next two years are expected 
to deny the government vital tax receipts.
Household disposable income 
<https://interactive.guim.co.uk/uploader/embed/2016/11/household-disposable-income-zip/giv-15726W58JUZ0zd9A9/>
 Household disposable income 
This a key measure of household spending power and watched closely by the 
Treasury now the economy is driven largely by consumer outlay. Steadily rising 
inflation is expected to offset wages growth and limit future growth.
Export market share 
<https://interactive.guim.co.uk/uploader/embed/2016/11/uk-export-market-share-zip/giv-157266n1bQ8GQzfGy>
 Export market share 
Exports have fallen as a proportion of GDP for decades. Forecasts show the 
ratio is expected to continue to fall, limiting the country’s ability to buy 
goods from abroad without running up a balance of trade deficit.
Productivity 
<https://interactive.guim.co.uk/uploader/embed/2016/11/productivity-zip/giv-15726L0Z66LGO8fvs>
 Productivity 
For the last six years the government has expected a rise in productivity – 
the value of products made or sold by a worker in an hour of work – to bolster 
profits and workers’ earnings. It hasn’t happened. This year the recovery in 
productivity has been downgraded again.
