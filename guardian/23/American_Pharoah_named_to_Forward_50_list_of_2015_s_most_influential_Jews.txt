
Triple Crown champion. Breeders’ Cup Classic winner. Jewish icon.

American Pharoah has been named to the Jewish Daily Forward’s annual Forward 50
 <http://forward.com/series/forward-50/2015/american-pharoah/> list of notable 
and newsworthy Jews.

The bay colt, who in June became the first thoroughbred in 37 years to win the 
Kentucky Derby, Preakness and Belmont Stakes 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/jun/06/american-pharoah-triple-crown-belmont-stakes>
, was honored by the 118-year-old newspaper in a nod to Egyptian-born owner 
Ahmed Zayat, the Yeshiva University graduate and Orthodox Jew whose largesse 
and philanthropic efforts for Jewish causes is well documented.

The Zayat Stables homebred is in good company on this year’s Forward 50 
<http://forward.com/series/forward-50/2015/>, unveiled on Sunday, whose top 
five names include Democratic presidential candidate Bernie Sanders, comedian 
Amy Schumer, same-sex marriage activist Evan Wolfson, MacArthur genius grant 
winner Marina Rustow and community activist and live kidney donor Mendy Reiner.

The three-year-old son of Pioneerof the Nile and Littleprincessemma became the 
first Triple Crown champion towin the Breeders’ Cup Classic 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/oct/31/american-pharoah-breeders-cup-classic-keeneland-found-golden-horn>
 with a wire-to-wire win on 30 October at Keeneland. The first-place finish, 
his ninth in 11 lifetime starts, lifted his career earnings to $8.65m.

In June, American Pharoah <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/american-pharoah> 
made national headlines in becoming the 12th horse – and first since 1978 – to 
sweep America’s three most celebrated races, joining Sir Barton (1919), Gallant 
Fox (1930), Omaha (1935), War Admiral (1937), Whirlaway (1941), Count Fleet 
(1943), Assault (1946), Citation (1948), Secretariat (1973), Seattle Slew 
(1977) and Affirmed (1978).
