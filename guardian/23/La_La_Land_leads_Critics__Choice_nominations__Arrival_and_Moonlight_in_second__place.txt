
La La Land 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/aug/31/la-la-land-review-ryan-gosling-emma-stone>
, Damien Chazelle’s musical ode to Hollywood starring Emma Stone and Ryan 
Gosling, continues its quickstep towards the Oscars by dominating nominations 
for the Broadcast Film Critics Association Critics’ Choice awards.

The film, about the relationship difficulties of a jazz pianist and aspiring 
movie star, took 12 nods, including best picture, director, actor and actress.

Moonlight review – devastating drama is vital portrait of black gay 
masculinity in America
 Read more  
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/03/moonlight-review-devastating-drama-is-vital-portrait-of-black-gay-masculinity-in-america>
Trailing by two nominations were Arrival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/nov/10/arrival-review-amy-adams-jeremy-renner-science-fiction>
, the Amy Adams-starring story of the linguistic fallout from an alien 
invasion, andMoonlight 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/03/moonlight-review-devastating-drama-is-vital-portrait-of-black-gay-masculinity-in-america>
, Barry Jenkins’s story of a young black gay man at three stages of life.

Also landing best picture nominations were Fences 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/nov/22/fences-review-denzel-washington-viola-davis-august-wilson>
,Hacksaw Ridge 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/04/hacksaw-ridge-review-mel-gibson-finds-a-conscience-in-gruesome-war-story>
,Hell or High Water 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/11/hell-or-high-water-review>, Lion 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/11/lion-review-dev-patel-and-nicole-kidman-hunt-oscars-in-moving-adoption-drama>
,Loving 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/may/16/loving-review-civil-rights-tale-marries-heartfelt-drama-with-too-much-restraint>
,Sully 
<http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/films/reviews/sully-review-tom-hanks-clint-eastwood-captain-sullenberger-miracle-on-the-hudson-a7447401.html>
 andManchester by the Sea 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/jan/24/manchester-by-the-sea-review-kenneth-lonergans-morose-echo-of-margaret>
 – which topped theNational Board of Review’s poll 
<http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-38156617> of the best film of the 
year earlier this week.

There was space for glitzier, more mainstream movies in the 27 categories, 
includingBatman v Superman: Dawn of Justice 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/mar/27/batman-v-superman-dawn-of-justice-observer-review>
 (which landed a nod for Gal Gadot) andSuicide Squad 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/aug/07/suicide-squad-review-bad-film-margot-robbie-jared-leto>
 (whose Margot Robbie was honoured.

The winners will be announced at a ceremony on 11 December.
