
Moonlight 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/03/moonlight-review-devastating-drama-is-vital-portrait-of-black-gay-masculinity-in-america>
, the rapturously received story of a young gay black man at three stages in 
his life, has swept the board at theGotham awards <https://gotham.ifp.org/> in 
New York. The ceremony, which took place on Monday, sawBarry Jenkins 
<https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/oct/15/why-i-love-barry-jenkins-bim-adewunmi>
’s drama pick up best feature, best screenplay and the audience award, as well 
as special jury prize for best ensemble.

Some have interpreted the win in these leading indie awards as lending 
momentum to Moonlight’s Oscars campaign; the last two winners of the Gotham 
feature prize –Birdman 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/feb/23/birdman-wins-big-two-oscars-but-ties-with-grand-budapest-hotel-for-awards-tally>
 andSpotlight 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/feb/29/spotlight-wins-best-picture-oscar>
 – progressed to best picture victory at the Academy Awards.

The current frontrunner this time round is Damien Chazelle’s La La Land 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/sep/08/damien-chazelle-la-la-land-interview-toronto-film-festival-tiff-venice-telluride>
 – but it may face competition from Jenkins’s up-and-comer, as well as from 
Martin Scorsese’sSilence 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/nov/23/martin-scorsese-silence-first-trailer-adam-driver-andrew-garfield-film>
.

Casey Affleck <https://www.theguardian.com/film/casey-affleck> won the best 
actor award for his role as a traumatised janitor in Kenneth Lonergan’s 
comeback film,Manchester by the Sea 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/video/2016/oct/10/manchester-by-the-sea-clip-kenneth-lonergan-casey-affleck-video>
, while Isabelle Huppert was the surprise winner of best actress for her role 
in Paul Verhoeven’s controversial rape comedyElle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/may/21/elle-review-paul-verhoevens-brazen-revenge-comedy-is-a-dangerous-delight>
.

“I’m breathless. I’m speechless,” said Huppert 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/isabelle-huppert> at the podium. “I didn’t 
expect that to happen, I promise. They told me it’s an American award: ‘You’re 
French, and you’ll never get it.’”

Huppert triumphed over favourites such as Natalie Portman (Jackie 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/nov/26/jackie-kennedy-biopic-natalie-portman-jfk>
) and Annette Bening (20th Century Women

<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/nov/29/ethan-hawke-boyhood-before-midnight>
