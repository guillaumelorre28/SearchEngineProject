
A sequel to the hit animated film Shaun the Sheep Movie 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/shaun-the-sheep-movie> has been announced.

Bristol-based producer Aardman Animations 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/aardman> said that work on Shaun the Sheep 
Movie 2 would begin in January 2017, with Richard Starzak back on board as 
director (though Mark Burton, who co-directed the first film, will not return).

David Sproxton, co-founder and executive chairman of Aardman 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2005/jan/01/film.filmnews>, maker of the 
Wallace and Gromit films, said in a statement: “The flock are very excited to 
be embarking on another big screen adventure – a rip-roaring comedy that takes 
them to even greater heights of lunacy.”

Shaun originated as a character in Nick Park’s 1995 Oscar-winning short A 
Close Shave <http://www.aardman.com/work/a-close-shave/>, and then became the 
central figure in a highly successful TV series, Shaun the Sheep, which debuted 
on the CBBC channel in 2007. Shaun the Sheep Movie was released in 2015 andhas 
taken more than $106m 
<http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?id=shaunthesheep.htm> worldwide.
