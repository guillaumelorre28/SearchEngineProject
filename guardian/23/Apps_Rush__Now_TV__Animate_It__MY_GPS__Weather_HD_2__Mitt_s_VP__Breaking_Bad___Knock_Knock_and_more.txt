
A selection of 14 new and notable apps for you today:

Now TV UK <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/now-tv-uk/id512266300?mt=8>

Following its debut on Android <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/android>
, Sky has launched an iOS version of its new Now TV internet TV service. 
Subscribers can register for a Sky Movies Pass for £15 a month to choose from a 
catalogue of films, or "Pay & Play" on the Now TV website for films 
individually, before watching them on the devices.
iPhone / iPad

Animate It <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/animate-it/id544691268?mt=8>

There are a number of stop-motion animation apps for iOS, but this one has 
clout: it's the work ofAardman <https://www.theguardian.com/film/aardman> 
Animations Studios. It aims to be an easy way to create animations using 
features like time lapse and onion skinning, before exporting the results.
iPhone / iPad

My GPS UK & ROI <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/my-gps-uk-roi/id527015993?mt=8>

The team behind navigation app Navfree has a new iPhone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/iphone> app: My GPS. It costs 69p 
(although some features are then offered as in-app purchases), and uses a new 
graphics engine and OpenStreetMap data.
iPhone

Weather HD 2 <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/weather-hd-2/id364193735?mt=8>

The original Weather HD app has more than 5m users according to its developer 
Vimov. A good base for this sequel, which includes animations, 3D weather maps, 
push-notified severe weather alerts and more than 2m locations around the world 
covered.
iPhone / iPad

Mitt's VP <http://www.mittromney.com/app/vp>

Well, this is interesting: US presidential candidate Mitt Romney 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/mittromney> hasn't yet chosen his 
vice-presidential running mate, but when he does, he'll announce it in an app. 
"There's no telling when Mitt will choose his VP. But when he does, be the 
first to find out with Mitt's VP app," explains the blurb for Mitt's VP.
Android / iPhone

Breaking Bad: G.F.Y. 
<http://itunes.apple.com/app/breaking-bad-g.f.y./id541279357>

This US-only app is for fans of TV show Breaking Bad 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/breaking-bad>, based on "the twist 
that no one was expecting" in the final episode of season four. This app 
involves mapping your own face onto the Gus Fring character to re-enact the 
"Face Off" scene from that episode.
iPhone

Knock Knock 1 
<http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/knoc-knoc-1/id536944943?ls=1&mt=8> / Knock 
Knock 2 <http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/knoc-knoc-2/id536961929?mt=8>

Two apps here, both cut from the same cloth. That cloth being voice-activated 
knock knock jokes: a series of cartoon doors that get children to speak to them 
while telling the jokes. They're the work of Disney animator Dan Lund, and a 
"who's who of voice talent" from Hollywood.
iPhone / iPad

London Guide 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.spottedbylocals.London>

Released by Spotted by Locals, this is a travel app for tourists in London 
providing tips from the natives on where to go and what to do. It's free, but 
uses an in-app purchase to upgrade to the full version. And this is just one of
many Spotted by Locals apps 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/developer?id=Spotted+by+Locals> to have 
just gone live on Android, for various cities.
Android

SlotSpot <http://itunes.apple.com/ca/app/slotspot/id515110684?mt=8>

Facebook casino game SlotSpot has been launched for iPhone – the latest social 
game to make such a leap from Facebook to smartphones. It's a slot-machine game 
using in-app purchases for virtual coins, although note, your winnings are 
equally virtual.
iPhone

Live365 Radio for iPad 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/live365-radio-for-ipad/id546800061?mt=8>

Online radio service Live365 has a brand new iPad 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/ipad> app, offering thousands of 
streaming radio stations across more than 250 genres of music, with an 
interface revamped for the larger tablet screen.
iPad

SeatGuru by TripAdvisor 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.seatguru>

TripAdvisor's SeatGuru app has flown onto Android, helping people pick seats 
on planes and search for low airfares. It takes data from nearly 100 airlines, 
and includes more than 45,000 passenger reviews.
Android

iBlast Moki 2 <https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.godzilab.ibm2>

iOS hit game iBlast Moki 2 is the latest to make the jump to Android, offering 
90 levels of physics puzzles, and an editor for players to create their own 
levels too.
Android

Inapub <https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=uk.co.inapub.androidapp>

Inapub is a handy app for finding, yes, pubs. It has more than 30,000 UK 
hostelries in its database, tagged with features like sunday lunch, car park 
and child-friendly. Social features are included, as are vouchers for pubs 
supporting them, and the app uses augmented reality for an "On The Horizon" 
feature to find nearby pubs.
