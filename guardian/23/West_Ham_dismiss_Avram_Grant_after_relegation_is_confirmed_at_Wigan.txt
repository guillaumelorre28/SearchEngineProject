
West Ham United have parted company with their manager, Avram Grant, after 
their relegation was sealed by a3-2 defeat at Wigan Athletic 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2011/may/15/wigan-athletic-west-ham-united-premier-league>
 on Sunday. Kevin Keen will take charge of the team for the remaining match of 
the season.

Shortly after 7pm the club released a statement confirming the Israeli's 
departure following a result that confirmed that West Ham would finish bottom 
of thePremier League <https://www.theguardian.com/football/premierleague>.

The statement read: "The club can confirm that Avram Grant is no longer the 
manager ofWest Ham United <https://www.theguardian.com/football/westhamunited>.

"First-team coach Kevin Keen will take charge of the team for the final home 
match of the season against Sunderland on Sunday 22 May."
