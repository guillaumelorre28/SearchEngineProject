
 A Victorian farmer has died in hospital after being trapped in a hay baler 
for up to 36 hours. 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2016/nov/30/farmer-trapped-in-hay-baler-for-more-than-24-hours-in-critical-condition>

Don Fagg, aged in his 60s, had to be resuscitated at the Mount Duneed 
property, nearGeelong <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/geelong>, before he 
could be transferred to hospital after he was freed from the machine on Tuesday 
night.

He died just before midnight on Wednesday in the Royal Melbourne hospital 
where he had been flown in a critical condition.

“It is believed the man had been there across a number of days and was 
transported to hospital in a critical condition,”Victoria 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/victoria> police said in a 
statement.

It’s understood Fagg was found by neighbours on Tuesday evening with emergency 
services called at 6.45pm.

It then took emergency services crews until 9.20pm to free him.

Worksafe has been notified and police will prepare a report for the coroner.
