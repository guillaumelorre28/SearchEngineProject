
Richard Gere <https://www.theguardian.com/film/richard-gere>, Kristin Scott 
Thomas <https://www.theguardian.com/culture/kristin-scott-thomas> and Penélope 
Cruz <https://www.theguardian.com/film/penelopecruz> will be heading for the 
Potsdamer Platz in February as the Berlin film festival announced its first 
tranche of screenings.

Among a clutch of films receiving their world premiere in the competition 
section of the festival areThe Dinner <http://www.imdb.com/title/tt3203620/>, 
which features Gere alongsideSteve Coogan 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/coogan>, Laura Linney 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/laura-linney> and Rebecca Hall 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/rebecca-hall> in a thriller about two married 
couples who meet to discuss what to do about a crime apparently committed by 
their children; and political fableThe Party 
<http://www.imdb.com/title/tt5814592/?ref_=nv_sr_8>, written and directed by 
Sally Potter <https://www.theguardian.com/film/sally-potter>, whose cast 
includes Scott Thomas,Patricia Clarkson 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/patricia-clarkson>, Emily Mortimer 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/emily-mortimer> and Cillian Murphy 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/cillian-murphy>. Other potential Golden Bear 
winners include films from European auteursAgnieszka Holland 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2016/apr/14/agniesza-holland-cinema-a-boys-club-which-ignores-women>
 andAki Kaurismaki <https://www.theguardian.com/film/aki-kaurismaki>. The 
former has directedPokot <http://www.imdb.com/title/tt5328350/> (AKA Spoor), 
adapted from Olga Tokarczuk’s 2009 “moral thriller” novel Drive Your Plough 
Over the Bones of the Dead, and the latter is bringingThe Other Side of Hope 
<http://www.imdb.com/title/tt5222918/?ref_=nv_sr_1>, his first directorial 
effort sinceLe Havre 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/apr/05/le-havre-review> (2011), which is 
about a Finnish salesman and Syrian refugee in Helsinki.

Cruz’s presence at the Berlinale will come by way of a special screening (ie, 
non-competitive) ofThe Queen of Spain <http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2411144/>, 
her and directorFernando Trueba <http://www.fernandotrueba.com/>’s follow-up to 
The Girl of Your Dreams, the 1998 film about a fictional Spanish screen star 
who attracts the attention of Josef Goebbels. Here the same character is 
shooting a Hollywood film in Spain after the second world war, playing the role 
of Isabella of Castile.

The work of the celebrated German performance artist Joseph Beuys 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/beuys> (who died in 1986) will also 
be acknowledged in Berlin, with the selection of Andres Veiel’s documentary for 
the Golden Bear competition.

The Berlin film festival <https://www.theguardian.com/film/berlinfilmfestival> 
runs from 9-19 February 2017.
