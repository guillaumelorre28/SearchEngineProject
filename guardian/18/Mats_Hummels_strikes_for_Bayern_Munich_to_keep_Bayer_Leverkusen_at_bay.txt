
Bayern Munich recovered from back-to-back defeats to Borussia Dortmund and FC 
Rostov with a 2-1 win overBayer Leverkusen 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/bayerleverkusen>.

Thiago Alcântara put the home side ahead with a close-range header on the 
half-hour mark but Hakan Calhanoglu equalised superbly five minutes later after 
a clever one-two with Julian Brandt had ripped open the home side’s defence.

Mats Hummels powered home a header from Joshua Kimmich’s cross early in the 
second half to restore the champions’ lead but they were made to work hard for 
all three points.

Bayern’s Javi Martínez was at the centre two penalty appeals for the visitors. 
First, he was alleged to have handled the ball in his own penalty area and then 
he bundled into Aleksandar Dragovic in stoppage time.

Goalkeeper Manuel Neuer also saved a late Calhanoglu free-kick to ensure that 
Carlo Ancelotti’s men held on for their first win since the start of November. 
The victory cements second place in the Bundesliga forBayern Munich 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/bayernmunich>, three points behind 
leaders RB Leipzig, while Leverkusen remain in mid-table.
