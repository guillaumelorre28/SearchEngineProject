
A documentary telling the inside story of Donald Trump’s stunning presidential 
election victory has been added to theSundance film festival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/sundancefilmfestival> lineup.

Trumped: Inside the Greatest Political Upset of All Time has been put together 
by the team behind theShowtime network’s election series, The Circus: Inside 
the Greatest Political Show on Earth 
<http://www.laweekly.com/film/showtimes-the-circus-actually-makes-sense-of-this-election-7351616>
, which ran from January to November 2016 and covered the presidential race in 
detail over 26 episodes. No screening date has been confirmed, but Trump’s 
inauguration will take place on 20 January, on the second day of the festival.

Sundance has also added the world premiere of Newness, the new film by 
festival favourite Drake Doremus, whose2011 film Like Crazy 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/jan/28/drake-doremus-like-crazy-sundance>
 won the grand jury prize. Newness stars Nicholas Hoult, Laia Costa and Danny 
Huston, and is described as a contemporary romance set in Los Angeles in which 
“two millennials navigating a social media-driven hookup culture begin a 
relationship that pushes both emotional and physical boundaries”.

The Sundance film festival <https://www.theguardian.com/film/sundance-2017> 
runs from 19-29 January in Park City, Utah.
