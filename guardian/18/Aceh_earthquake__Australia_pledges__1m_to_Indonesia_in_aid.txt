
The government has committed $1m to aid Indonesia’s response to the Aceh 
earthquake that claimed 100 lives and left more than 11,000 without homes.

More than 100 people were killed and 750 injured in the 6.5 magnitude  
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/dec/07/aceh-earthquake-children-among-dead-and-dozens-more-trapped-on-sumatra>
earthquake that struck off the coast of Sumatra, which destroyed more than 
10,500 homes along with schools and a major public hospital on Wednesday.

The full impact of the earthquake is still being assessed.

The foreign affairs minister, Julie Bishop 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/julie-bishop>, said the government 
support will go towards essential household items, shelter, health and first 
aid, clean water and helping families to get back to work.

Australia will also provide 1,500 family kits that include emergency household 
and hygiene items, 2,200 tarpaulins and 100 tents and will help 5,500 people in 
the worst-affected areas.

“The Australian government stands ready to respond to additional requests for 
more assistance from the Indonesian Government,” Bishop said.
