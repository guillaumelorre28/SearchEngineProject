
David Bowie could win a posthumous Brit Award as best British male solo artist 
after being shortlisted for next month’s ceremony. The star – who died in 
January 2016 – will go up against Craig David,Michael Kiwanuka 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/michael-kiwanuka> and Mercury prize-winner 
Skepta for the title on 22 February.

Meanwhile, the battle for best British female solo artist is between Anonhni, 
Ellie Goulding, Emeli Sandé,Lianne La Havas 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/lianne-la-havas> and Nao.

The move of former One Direction member, Zayn Malik 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/zayn-malik>, into a solo career appears to 
be paying off, as he has been nominated for best British single with his track 
Pillowtalk. He goes up against Perrie Edwards’ group Little Mix, with Shout Out 
to My Ex, as well as another X Factor alumnus, James Arthur, with Say You Won’t 
Let Go.

Also in the category are Alan Walker’s Faded Calum Scott track, Dancing On My 
Own,Calvin Harris <https://www.theguardian.com/music/calvin-harris> featuring 
Rihanna on This Is What You Came For, Clean Bandit for Rockabye, Coldplay with 
Hymn for the Weekend, Jonas Blue for Fast Car and Tinie Tempah with Girls Like.

The nominations for the ceremony were announced live on The Brits Are Coming 
on ITV on Saturday night.Craig David 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/craig-david>, Christine and The Queens and 
Calum Scott, as well as Brits Critics’ Choice winner Rag’n’Bone Man, all 
performed during the event, as the contenders for categories including best 
British single, best British group and Mastercard British album of the year 
were revealed.
