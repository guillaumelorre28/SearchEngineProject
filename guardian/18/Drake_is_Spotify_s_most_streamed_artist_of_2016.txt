
Drake 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jul/29/bryan-adams-retains-record-for-longest-time-at-uk-no-1-charts-drake>
 is Spotify’s most streamed artist of 2016. In a year in which the Canadian 
rapper and singer broke records with his trackOne Dance 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/musicblog/2016/jul/15/one-dance-phenomenon-drake-no-1>
, the artist has topped many of the music streaming service’s end of year lists.

Thanks to the success of his album Views, Drake 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/drake> accrued more than 4.7bn streams this 
year, more than doubling his record of 1.8bn streams in 2015.

“With the top album and the top song this year, as well as his successful 
Summer Sixteen tour, Drake continues to engage his fans in a way that only 
Drake can; it’s no surprise he is dominating the music industry,” said 
Spotify’s chief content and chief strategy officer Stefan Blom.

With 2.5bn streams, Rihanna was the most streamed female artist both in the UK 
and globally for second year in a row following the release of her eighth album,
Anti 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/jan/28/rihanna-anti-album-review-brave-bold-and-confused>
.

Justin Bieber, Kanye West, Coldplay and Twenty One Pilots 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2015/jun/16/twenty-one-pilots-blurryface-world-tour-brisbane>
 were the next most streamed male artists after Drake, while Sia, Adele, Ariana 
Grande and Beyoncé featured in the female top five.

David Bowie was named the most popular artist in the alternative category, 
with Glenn Gould the most streamed classical artist, the Beatles – who made
their catalogue available for 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/dec/23/beatles-back-catalogue-music-streaming-spotify-apple-music>
the first time to the service at the end of 2015 – ruling the classic rock 
category, and Bring Me the Horizon topping the metal genre.
