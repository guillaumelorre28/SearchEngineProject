
Craig Hignett has left Hartlepool United by mutual consent after a run of one 
win in nine games. The 47-year-old, who was in his first managerial post at 
Victoria Park, leaves the club 19th inLeague Two 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/leaguetwofootball>.

Hignett replaced Ronnie Moore in February last year and steered Hartlepool 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/hartlepool> away from relegation, 
finishing 16th. They are now only four points above the bottom two after 
Saturday’s 1-0 defeat at Crawley.

“I would like to place on record my thanks to Craig for all of his efforts,” 
the Hartlepool chairman, Gary Coxall, said in a statement on the club’s 
website. “He has given everything during his time in charge and he leaves with 
our very best wishes.

“The search for a new manager will begin immediately and it won’t be a long, 
drawn-out process. We want to get the right man appointed as quickly as 
possible.”

Sam Collins, the club’s under-21 manager, has been placed in temporary charge.
