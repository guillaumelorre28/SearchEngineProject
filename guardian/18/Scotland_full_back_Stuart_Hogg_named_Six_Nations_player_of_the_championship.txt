
Scotland’s Stuart Hogg has been voted the Six Nations 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/sixnations> player of the championship. The 
award was decided by a combination of an online poll, of which Hogg secured 
over 30%, and the Royal Bank and NatWest panel of former rugby players.

Hogg, who made his 43rd appearance during the Championship, scoring tries 
against France anda memorable effort against Ireland on the final weekend 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/mar/20/ireland-scotland-six-nations-regret>
, said: “I’m honoured to receive the RBS player of the championship trophy and 
join the list of the esteemed players that have won in the past. I’m extremely 
grateful for all the supporters who voted for me.”

England’s Billy Vunipola came a close second with 27% of the vote, with third 
place awarded to the tournament’s top-try scorer George North. Ireland’s 
scrum-half Conor Murray was fourth.

The 12-man shortlist, announced on Monday, attracted criticism for overlooking 
the Wales centre Jamie Roberts or either of England’s locks –George Kruis and
