
The former Newcastle United forward Hatem Ben Arfa has revealed up to 18 clubs 
have expressed an interest in signing him next season.

The 29-year-old has enjoyed a career renaissance this season with Nice, 
scoring 18 goals in Ligue 1 andmaking France’s reserve list 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/may/12/france-squad-euro-2016-olivier-giroud-ben-arfa>
 for the European Championship on home soil.

Lyon, his first club, last week revealed they have made him an offer and Paris 
Saint-Germain have also been linked with keeping him inFrance 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/france>, while Nice have not given up 
hope of persuading him to extend his stay on the French Riviera.

Speaking to Canal Plus while attending one of the region’s premier events, the 
Cannes Film Festival, Ben Arfa said: “There are 18 very interesting clubs who 
have put themselves forward. I have picked out three.

“There are plenty of offers, like the one from Lyon. They are a very ambitious 
club with a very ambitious president [Jean-Michel Aulas]. Any club with 
ambition is very interesting.

“It’s a very important decision for me to take this year. I will reflect and 
discuss it with my entourage.”
