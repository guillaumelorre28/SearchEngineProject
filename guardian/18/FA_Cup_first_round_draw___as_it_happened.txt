Westfields celebrated their 50-year birthday this year, having been founded in 
1966 off the back of England’s World Cup triumph. Teenager Andrew Morris, now 
the club’s chief executive, founded a team in Hereford in tribute: “We bought 
West Ham coloured kit - we were in awe of Bobby Moore, Martin Peters and Geoff 
Hurst. I was a goalkeeper because I thought Gordon Banks was the best thing 
sliced bread - all the fields in Westfields had people playing football.”

Westfields last reached the first-round proper 22 years ago, and beat Leiston 
FC in the preliminary round to reach the draw today. They’ve already played 
five matches in this competition already this season, starting on 5 August.

Westfields celebrated their 50-year birthday this year, having been founded in 
1966 off the back of England’s World Cup triumph. Teenager Andrew Morris, now 
the club’s chief executive, founded a team in Hereford in tribute: “We bought 
West Ham coloured kit - we were in awe of Bobby Moore, Martin Peters and Geoff 
Hurst. I was a goalkeeper because I thought Gordon Banks was the best thing 
sliced bread - all the fields in Westfields had people playing football.”

Westfields last reached the first-round proper 22 years ago, and beat Leiston 
FC in the preliminary round to reach the draw today. They’ve already played 
five matches in this competition already this season, starting on 5 August.
