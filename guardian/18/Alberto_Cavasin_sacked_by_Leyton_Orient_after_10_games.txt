Alberto Cavasin after a run of eight defeats in 10 games.

The Italian was only appointed manager on 2 October but the club now languish 
in 22nd place inLeague Two 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/leaguetwofootball>.

Cavasin was the eighth man to take charge of the clubs since the club were 
bought by the Italian businessman Francesco Becchetti in 2014. His departure 
comes following the 1-0 defeat against Exeter City, who went into the game 
bottom of the table.

Andy Edwards has been put in charge, with a deal until the end of the season.

“The club can confirm that Alberto Cavasin has been relieved of his managerial 
duties with immediate effect,” a statement read. “We thank him for his efforts 
during his time with the club and wish him well for the future and he departs 
the club alongside head of fitness Alfredo Vergori and Matteo Festa.”
