There were no goals in the first period but Vicente Iborra scored a 
second-half hat-trick to win it for Jorge Sampaoli’s side. He got his first in 
the 52nd minute with a header from a corner and sealed the game with a second 
from Vitolo’s set-up. He completed his hat-trick from the penalty spot after 
Gustavo Cabral fouled Joaquin Correa in the box.

Athletic Bilbao missed a chance to go fifth as they lost 1-0 at Real Betis, 
with Rubén Castro stroking in the only goal in the 18th minute at the Estadio 
Benito Villamarín.

Espanyol extended their unbeaten run in La Liga to eight matches and climbed 
to ninth in the table with a 2-1 home victory overSporting Gijon. The Ecuador 
forward Felipe Caicedo opened the scoring in the 54th minute with a brave 
header before Léo Baptistão scored a cracker on the break in the final minute.

Duje Cop’s free-kick in stoppage time halved the deficit but it was another 
encouraging result for Quique Sánchez Flores’s men.

The day’s other match between Eibar and Alaves finished goalless.
