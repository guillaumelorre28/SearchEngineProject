Well that was a huge improvement from Rob Howley’s side; the returns for Alun 
Wyn Jones, Sam Warburton and Liam Williams made such an enormous difference. It 
wasn’t perfect but after a run of five straight defeats, and against southern 
hemisphere opponents to boot, they won’t complain too hard.

Argentina were disappointing and the team that lit up the World Cup just a 
year ago could do with showing that they’re not just a flash in the pan after 
only managing one win in the Rugby Championship this year.

That’s it from me for tonight. Cheers for reading, bye!

Wales ring changes and end woeful run with narrow win over Argentina
 Read more  
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/nov/12/wales-argentina-autumn-internationals-match-report> 
Gareth Davies usher it into touch and that’s more like it from Wales.
 Gareth Davies and Ross Moriarty celebrate at the final whistle. Photograph: 
Andrew Boyers/Reuters  
79 min Alun Wyn Jones drops the restart and Argentina have it, working their 
way towards the Welsh 22. A poor pass from Sanches sends them backwards but 
they still have it. Senatore barrels to the 22 but the maul is formed and 
Jones, the man of the match, has won Wales a turnover and a scrum! That should 
do it.
 
10 metres in from the left and about the same distance outside the 22, 
Halfpenny fires Wales into a four-point lead.
 
77 min And Pieretto is in the maul at the side after the lineout. Penalty to 
Wales and Halfpenny should all but secure the win with this.
 
76 min Baldwin’s throw goes loose and Desio seizes it, but then Sanches kicks 
and the men in the ruck are penalised for being in front of the kicker. Biggar 
finds touch with the penalty on the left, just outside the 22.
 
75 min Biggar goes very long and Hernandez returns to touch with interest. 
Halfpenny, looking to catch it on the full, keeps his eye on the ball and trips 
over a ball boy. Iglesias replaces Hernandez. Evidently Argentina don’t think 
they’ll need his boot.
 
A touch surprisingly, they take the points from 30 metres. One point in it...
 
73 min Hernandez finds touch five metres outside the Wales 22 on the left and 
Senatore takes it. Sanches attacks the line and Charteris is a touch 
unfortunate to be penalised for a high tackle, given how low the Pumas No10 
ducked.
 
72 min Wales go left from the lineout midway inside Argentina’s half. It’s too 
quickly through the hand’s though and Jonathan Davies’s poor flapped pass for 
Liam Williams goes to touch. From that lineout Argentina attack at speed and 
get the penalty for a high tackle, I think.
 
70 min Penalty Wales at the scrum after Nicky Smith makes pulp out of Herrera. 
Off goes the Argentinian prop, again replaced by Pieretto.
 
69 min Good defence by Biggar, holding up the No13 Orland and winning his side 
the turnover scrum five metres or so outside his own 22.
 Argentina’s Ramiro Moyano attempts to stop Dan Biggar. Photograph: Ben 
Hoskins/Getty Images  
68 min “This’ll be up in the air,” says Jonathan Davies on commentary. 
Straight down the middle and flat goes Hernandez. “Yep,” says Davies. Argentina 
carry it 20 metres inside the Wales half.
 
67 min Montoya replaces Cap’n Creevy. After an exchange of kicks at the 
restart Biggar finds touch with a gorgeous banana kick on the run. Argentina 
will go from the lineout midway inside their own half but there’s no space for 
them to run at.
 
Notch it he does and Wales have a four-point cushion.
 
65 min Halfpenny should notch three points here once Jones has had some 
treatment to his ear. It’s on the 22, just to the left.
 
64 min North chases Davies’s kick chase beautifully, Isa is wrapped up after 
taking it from Cordero and Wales win the turnover when North rips it clear! 
Swung left and it’s stabbed through in goal by Jon Davies for Liam Williams, 
but we’re going back for a penalty for side entry at a ruck by Sanches.
 
63 min Desio slips through a tackle from the restart and Argentina are flying 
clear. They go up to halfway but then De la Fuente messes up his grubber and 
slices it into touch. For Argentina, Cubelli and Senatore are on for Landajo 
and Matera; for Wales Nicky Smith is on for Jenkins. Tipuric wins the lineout 
on his own 10 metre line.
 
Just when I thought I was out, they pulled me back in.
 
The question is whether or not he had control of the ball and the ruling is 
that he has!
 
61 min Isa comes now and throws a great offload out to Landajo. The scrum-half 
is scragged just short so Argentina go through the phases, inches out. Landajo 
darts and we’re going to the TMO. A few players celebrated and I think he might 
have got this.
 
60 min There is no excuse for keeping the clock running while scrums are reset.
 The Wales and Argentina players clash. Photograph: Andrew Boyers/Reuters  
59 min Tomas Lezana comes on for Petti. Gethin Jenkins might be withdrawn soon 
too, I reckon, as he’s down having treatment before this Argentinian scrum 15 
metres out. I imagine we’ll have a test of Liam Williams’ defensive attributes 
shortly, when Facundo Isa comes hurtling down his channel.
 
58 min Isa barrels into Moriarty and Argentina are up to the Welsh 22, whence 
Matera carries it on another five metres. Solid defence around the fringes from 
Wales though – take note, South Africa – and this is very, very slow going. 
Jenkins wins the turnover but Charteris knocks on.
 
57 min This time it’s a shallower restart and Wales struggle to gather it. The 
ball bounces to the try-scorer Gavin Davies, who then thumps it directly into 
touch from just outside his 22. Good lineout position for Argentina on the left.
 
The ensuing maul crabs towards the posts before going to ground. Gareth Davies 
steps off the back, checks back inside, darts and stretches for the line to 
finish well. You Stole the Sun From my Heart is the celebratory music this time.
 Gareth Davies scores the second try for Wales. Photograph: Paul Harding/PA  
54 min It’s still with Wales but they’ve made precisely zero metres. Gethin 
Jenkins of all people pops a beauty of a chip towards the corner and Cordero, 
covering back, doesn’t have a tight enough turning circle and his heel just 
brushes the touchline. Five metre lineout to Wales...
 
53 min Wales comes off the back of the scrum and probe for holes in midfield.
 
51 min Penalty Argentina from the lineout for obstruction but Hernandez’s kick 
to touch is kept in play by Liam Williams, flying to tap it back in. The Pumas 
must come from deep but a poor pop pass from Botta forces Sanches to drop it 12 
metres inside his own half.
 
50 min Well that came from nowhere but that is sort of Argentina’s thing. From 
the restart, Landajo makes a cracking exit, finding touch around halfway on the 
right. The Pumas are making changes: Herrera returns and Botta replaces Noguera.
 
Penalty Argentina for offside and Landajo goes quickly. He steps from side to 
side and makes 20 metres before slipping a diagonal grubber kick through. 
Hernandez gives chase and outruns Biggar to gather and dive under the posts!
 Argentina’s Juan Martin Hernandez dives over the line to score. Photograph:
