 Photograph: David Sillitoe for the Guardian. 
The satisfaction levels among a hospital's staff are closely linked to the 
quality of healthcare it provides, say a team of doctors fromImperial College 
London <http://www3.imperial.ac.uk/research>. Research shows 
<http://qualitysafety.bmj.com/content/early/2013/02/20/bmjqs-2012-001540.short?g=w_qs_ahead_tab>
 that hospitals in England with lower mortality rates were more likely to have 
members of staff satisfied with the quality of care they provide.

Companies could save millions of pounds if they followed 18 key success 
factors for outsourcing products and processes, according to researchers at
Portsmouth Business School 
<http://www.port.ac.uk/uopnews/2013/02/21/outsourcing-effort-equals-bigger-profits/>
.

Arts and humanities <http://www.guardian.co.uk/education/artsandhumanities>
 Photograph: Manchester University. 
The editors of the world's only surviving copy of a 165-year-old Chartist hymn 
book have been identified. Researcher Mike Sanders from theUniversity of 
Manchester <http://www.manchester.ac.uk/aboutus/news/display/?id=9548> says the 
hymn books were designed in an attempt to produce a standard hymn book for the 
Chartist movement.

An archaeological team at Kingston University 
<http://www.kingston.ac.uk/news/article/797/04-feb-2013-excavation-unearths-secrets-of-londons-victorian-past/>
 has unearthed secrets of London's Victorian past, including a clay smoking 
pipe and Neolithic flint.

Science <http://www.guardian.co.uk/science> and technology 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/technology>
 Photograph: Christopher Charisius/Reuters. 
Scientists from the University of Southampton 
<http://www.southampton.ac.uk/research/latest_research/> have identified the 
molecular system that could help develop potential treatments for conditions 
such as Alzheimer's disease.

The most common genetic cause of Parkinson's is not only responsible for the 
condition's distinctive movement problems but may also affect vision, according 
to researchers at theUniversity of York 
<http://www.york.ac.uk/news-and-events/news/2013/research/parkinsons-research/>.

And finally
 Photograph: Alamy. 
Research by scientists at Anglia Ruskin University 
<http://www.anglia.ac.uk/ruskin/en/home/research/research_at_anglia_ruskin/news/exotic_tress_affect_breeding.html>
 has shown that the presence of exotic, non-native trees and shrubs is having a 
negative impact on blue tit and great tit populations in British parks. Their 
research found that blue tits and great tits have more difficulty raising 
chicks in a man-made environment such as the Botanic Garden.
