
The second series of time travel cop drama Ashes to Ashes 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/ashes-to-ashes> launched with 7 
million viewers last night, Monday 20 April.

BBC1's follow-up to Life on Mars, starring Philip Glenister and Keeley Hawes, 
attracted an average audience share of 29% in the 9pm hour, according to 
unofficial overnight figures.

The first series also of Ashes to Ashes also launched with 7 million viewers 
in February last year, but ended with 5.4 million.

ITV1's cooking reality show Hell's Kitchen drew 4.5 million viewers and a 19% 
share in the 9pm hour.

Channel 4's Henry VIII: Mind of a Tyrant picked up 1.7 million and a 7% share 
in the same slot, with a further 152,000 on digital catchup service Channel 4 
+1.

The concluding part of Channel Five's Extreme Fishing with Robson Green was 
the broadcaster's biggest catch of the day, netting 1.4 million viewers and a 
6% share in the 9pm hour.

BBC2's US drama import Heroes attracted 1.3 million viewers and a 5% share 
between 9pm and 9.45pm.

The return of presenter Julia Bradbury to BBC1's Watchdog was watched by 4 
million viewers between 7.30pm and 8pm, while a repeat of her Wainwright Walks 
on BBC2 between 8pm and 8.30pm drew 1.9 million and 9%.

Sky1's US drama 24 picked up 508,000 viewers and a multichannel share of 2.3% 
in the 9pm hour.
