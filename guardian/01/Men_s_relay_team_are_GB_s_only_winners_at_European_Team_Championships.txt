
The men’s sprint relay quartet secured Great Britain’s only victory on the 
opening day of the European Team Championships in Braunschweig, Germany.Adam 
Gemili <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/adam-gemili> brought the 4x100m team 
home for a convincing win in 38.51sec to leave a developmental squad in fourth 
place after 21 of the 40 events.

The quartet, which also featured Richard Kilty, Harry Aikines-Aryeetey 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/harry-aikines-aryeetey> and James Ellington, 
finished 0.37sec ahead of Germany, despite keeping the change-overs safe to 
ensure they got the baton round. The win moved Britain on to 158.5 points 
overnight, with the leaders, Germany, on 193.5.

Greg Rutherford <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/greg-rutherford>, one of 
the most experienced members of the squad, was beaten into second in the long 
jump by Germany’s Christian Reif, his best effort of 7.99m leaving him 
disappointed. Eilidh Child, in the 400m hurdles, and Danny Talbot, in the 100m, 
also earned second-place finishes.

The world triple jump champion, Teddy Tamgho, has been banned for a year for 
missing three drug tests in an 18-month period, the FrenchAthletics 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/athletics> Federation have announced.
