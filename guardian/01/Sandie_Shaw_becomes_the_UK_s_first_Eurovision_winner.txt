
The barefoot Sandie had been a regular on TV shows such as Ready Steady Go! 
and epitomised the "swinging 60s". But sales of her records were declining by 
1967, and despite her concerns that it wasn't cool, plus the BBC's misgivings 
that she'd been cited as the "other woman" in a divorce, she was invited to 
represent the UK in that year'sEurovision 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/eurovision> Song Contest in Vienna. 
She won, with Puppet On a String, cementing the country's on-off love affair 
with the competition for decades to come.
