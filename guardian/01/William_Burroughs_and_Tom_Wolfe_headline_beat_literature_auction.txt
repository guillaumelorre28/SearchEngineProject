
An empty vial that once contained methadone prescribed to William Burroughs 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/williamburroughs> and a 1968 first edition of
Tom Wolfe's book 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2008/jan/20/fiction.tomwolfe> The Electric 
Kool-Aid Acid Test, signed by 45 stars of the 60s counterculture scene, are 
being auctioned in San Francisco next week as part of the sale of a unique 
collection of beat and avant-garde literature and ephemera.

One of the star items of the sale 
<http://www.pbagalleries.com/view-auctions/catalog/id/137/> is a 1958 first 
edition paperback ofJack Kerouac <https://www.theguardian.com/books/jackkerouac>
's On the Road, inscribed by Neal Cassady – thereal-life inspiration 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/jan/18/beat-poets-cassady-kerouac-ginsberg>
 for Kerouac's drug-fuelled protagonist Dean Moriarty – with the words "Tell it 
John". The inscription is a message to Kerouac, and the book is expected to 
make between $8,000 and $12,000

The list of signatories to the copy of Wolfe's The Electric Kool-Aid Acid 
Test, his new journalism account ofKen Kesey 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/ken-kesey> and the Merry 
Pranksters' bus trip across America in 1964, reads like a who's who of 1960s US 
counterculture. They include Kesey, who wrote One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest, 
the novelist and activistWendell Berry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/wendell-berry>, who worked 
alongside Kesey at Stanford University, acid test participantKen Babbs 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/ken-babbs>, and Dan Healy and 
Phil Lesh ofthe Grateful Dead <https://www.theguardian.com/music/grateful-dead>
. Its sale value is estimated at $7,000 - $10,000.

Counterculture collector and historian Rick Synchef, who is selling off the 
books and other items,told CollectorsWeekly 
<http://www.collectorsweekly.com/articles/extraordinary-collection-of-counterculture-literature-up-for-auction/>
: "These are my idols. I looked up to these people because they brought 
something so new to the country."

The empty vial once belonging to Burroughs has a list price of $300 (£185). 
The 1950s and 1960s paperbacks, which are being sold separately and together in 
small lots, include works byCharles Bukowski 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/charles-bukowski>, Gary Snyder 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/gary-snyder>, Burroughs, Raymond 
Chandler <https://www.theguardian.com/books/raymondchandler> and Allen Ginsberg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/data/author/allen-ginsberg>. Most of the 
books are signed by the author, and many have additional signatures or 
inscriptions from others associated with the work.

The sale takes place at PBA Galleries in San Francisco on 10 October, and will 
be followed by a second auction in January 2014, focusing on books and other 
materials about drugs. A third sale will follow in May 2014.
