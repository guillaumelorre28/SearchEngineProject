
Annette Bening is to join John Lithgow 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/feb/14/john-lithgow-king-lear-shakespeare-in-park>
 as the latest big-name screen actor to tackle Shakespeare's King Lear onstage.

The American Beauty star 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2010/oct/27/annette-bening-david-thomson> and 
four-time Academy award nominee will play Goneril to Lithgow's Lear as part of 
New York's summer theatre season,Shakespeare in the Park 
<http://publictheater.org/en/programs--events/shakespeare-in-the-park/?SiteTheme=Shakespeare>
.

Bening was a stage actor long before her film career took off. As a student at 
the American Conservatory theatre in San Francisco she played Lady Macbeth. 
Nominated for a Tony award for her 1987 Broadway debut, Coastal Disturbances, 
she recently rplayedEuripides' Medea 
<http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/culturemonster/2009/09/theater-review-ucla-lives-medea-at-freud-playhouse.html>
 in Los Angeles.

Oscar-nominated Lithgow is a regular on the New York stage and was inducted 
into the American Theatre Hall of Fame in 2005. His Lear comes as two of his 
peers are winning five-star plaudits for their portrayal of Shakespeare's 
tragic monarch on either side of the Atlantic: Michael Penningonat Brooklyn's 
Polonsky Shakespeare Centre 
<http://www.nytimes.com/2014/03/28/theater/king-lear-with-michael-pennington-opens-in-brooklyn.html?_r=0>
, and Simon Russell Bealeat the National Theatre 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/jan/24/king-lear-olivier-theatre-review>
.

They join a roll call of high-profile actors to have played the part in recent 
years including Frank Langella atthe 2013 Chichester Festival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/nov/10/king-lear-review>, Jonathan 
Pryce atthe Almeida in 2012 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/sep/12/king-lear-almeida-review>, and 
Derek Jacobi inthe 2010 Donmar production 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2010/dec/08/review-king-lear-derek-jacobi>, 
all of whom earned four-star reviews from Guardian theatre critic Michael 
Billington.

Actor Fiona Shaw 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/may/08/the-testament-of-mary-fiona-shaw-barbican-review>
, asked in a recent Guardian webchat whether she would ever take on Lear having 
played a female Richard II, replied:"Aren't there rather a lot of those around?"
 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/may/16/fiona-shaw-live-webchat-post-your-questions-now>

However, King Lear has not been performed at Shakespeare in the Park since 
1973. Established by Joseph Papp,the founder of Manhattan's Public theatre 
<http://www.publictheater.org/>, the annual outdoor theatre season provides 
free productions of Shakespeare and other theatre classics, with more than 150 
plays performed at Central Park's Delacorte amphitheatre since 1962.

TV stars Jessica Collins <http://www.imdb.com/name/nm2193754/> and Breaking 
Bad's Jessica Hecht will join Bening as Lear's younger daughters Cordelia and 
Regan, with Tony-award winner Daniel Sullivan directing.
