
Pink Floyd's eighth studio album dwelt on themes of greed, conflict and mental 
illness: hardly the material of a million daydreams. Nevertheless, it captured 
the public imagination. Through the 70s and 80s, anyone looking at the UK or US 
charts would see it loitering around the lower reaches, refusing to depart. It 
eventually chalked up a record 741 weeks on the Billboard chart, despite only 
holding the top spot for a week.
