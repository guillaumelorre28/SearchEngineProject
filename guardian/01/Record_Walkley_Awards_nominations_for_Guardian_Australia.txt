
Guardian Australia has been nominated in five categories including 
international journalism, multimedia storytelling, major news event or issue, 
short feature writing and commentary.

The Nauru files team (Paul Farrell, Nick Evershed, Helen Davidson, Ri Liu and 
Josh Wall) were nominated for multimedia storytelling for thethe Nauru files 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2016/aug/10/the-nauru-files-2000-leaked-reports-reveal-scale-of-abuse-of-children-in-australian-offshore-detention>
. The team were also recognised in the Walkleys’ international journalism 
category for their coverage, which includedrevealing the despair of asylum 
seeker children stuck on Nauru 
<https://www.theguardian.com/news/2016/aug/10/i-want-death-nauru-files-chronicle-despair-of-asylum-seeker-children>
.

Guardian Australia’s immigration reporter, Ben Doherty’s feature “How the 
Australian immigration detention regime crushed Fazel Chegeni” 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2015/dec/21/how-australias-immigration-detention-regime-crushed-fazel-chegeni>
, was recognised a nomination in the short feature writing category. David Marr 
and Melissa Davey’scoverage of Cardinal George Pell’s appearance before the 
royal commission 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/live/2016/mar/03/george-pell-testifies-to-the-child-sexual-abuse-royal-commission-from-rome-day-four-live>
 was nominated for coverage of a major news event or issue.

Guardian Australia’s economics writer, Greg Jericho, has been nominated for 
his regular columns in the commentary, analysis, opinion and critique category.

Guardian Australia’s data and interactives editor, Nick Evershed has also been 
awarded aWalkleys grant for innovation in journalism 
<http://www.walkleys.com/2016-walkley-grants-for-innovation-in-journalism-winners-announced/>
 to develop a system for automating news stories from data feeds.

Guardian Australia has also been nominated in this year’s UNAA Media Peace 
Awards and Amnesty International Australia Media Awards.
