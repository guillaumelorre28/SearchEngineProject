
Malcolm Starbrook, editor-in-chief of Archant's East London 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk/london> and Essex titles for the last two 
years, is to leave the company at the end of this month.

According to a HoldTheFrontpage report 
<http://www.holdthefrontpage.co.uk/2014/news/editor-in-chief-of-london-weeklies-to-step-down>
, the newspaper veteran has spent 45 years in newspapers, and for 35 of those 
he sat in the editor's chair.

He joined the East London Advertiser in 1969 as a trainee and returned to the 
paper as editor in 2005. He subsequently assumed responsibility for more 
titles, such as the Hackney Gazette, Newham Recorder and the Barking & Dagenham 
Post, during variousArchant <https://www.theguardian.com/media/archant> 
restructures.

During the course of his career, he had spells with Thomson trade titles, West 
Suffolk Newspapers and the Sussex Express series. He spent 10 years as Croydon 
Advertiser editor, edited (and owned) the Milton Keynes Mirror, worked for
Trinity Mirror <https://www.theguardian.com/media/trinity-mirror>, set up a 
management consultancy and briefly took on the sub-editorial role at Press 
Gazette.

Starbrook also spent six years on the Press Complaints Commission 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/pcc>.

Archant's London editorial director, Laura Adams, is quoted as saying: 
"Malcolm has steered the editorial teams through many different changes as we 
have sought to respond to a challenging industry.

"Malcolm will be sorely missed in London and has been a great support to me 
during the last few years, particularly in his strategic role.

"He has been a committed and loyal employee of Archant and we remain grateful 
for his hard work and dedication."
