
In 1966, the Beatles toured the far east. The Japanese started to form bands 
that adopted the same line-up – drums, bass, lead guitar and a John Lennon 
figure doubling up on rhythm guitar and keyboards – all squeezed into tight 
suits singing three-part vocal harmonies. The genre, known as group sounds, 
would inspire countless garage rock curios, with 45s by the Tigers and the 
Tempters now selling for three-figure sums.
