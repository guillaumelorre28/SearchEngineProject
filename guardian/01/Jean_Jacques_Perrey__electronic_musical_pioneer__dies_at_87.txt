
Jean-Jacques Perrey, the French composer and pioneer of electronic music, has 
died at 87.

Perrey died in Lausanne, Switzerland 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/switzerland>, on Friday “from a very quick 
and violent lung cancer,” according to his daughter, Patricia Leroy.

Perrey was known as one of the first artists in Europe to work with electronic 
instruments. In the 1960s, he worked with the American composer Gershon 
Kingsley in the duo Perrey and Kingsley, whose Baroque Hoedown became known to 
millions worldwide as the music for theMain Street Electrical Parade at 
Disneyland <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hWTdd8EKo3Y> and Disney World.

His music appeared in commercials and on TV shows including The Simpsons 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/the-simpsons> and Comedy Central’s 
South Park.
