
Mail Online <https://www.theguardian.com/media/mail-online>, the most popular 
UK newspaper website network, returned to double-digit growth in March.

The Daily Mail & General Trust website network's daily average browsers rose 
16.5% on February's minor slump to 3,661,666 with monthly users up almost 30% 
month on month, according to the latest figures from the Audit Bureau of 
Circulations. UK users accounted for 32.7% of total online traffic.

Guardian.co.uk, which is part of the network that publishes 
MediaGuardian.co.uk, posted a 10.67% rise in daily users last month, to 
2,722,490. Monthly users grew by 24.5% month on month. UK users accounted for 
37% of all online traffic.

Telegraph.co.uk recorded a significant rise in daily average users last month, 
up 7.78% to 1,995,655. UK users accounted for 34% of online traffic.

Mirror Group Digital's network of sites suffered a second consecutive 
month-on-month fall in online traffic in March, with a 2% slide in daily 
average users to 537,355. It has fallen some way from its record high,posted in 
January 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2011/mar/24/february-abce-mail-online>, of 
602,685. Monthly users were up 4.75% month on month. UK users accounted for 56% 
of total online traffic.

Independent.co.uk also recorded a dip in daily users last month, falling 2.56% 
to 605,328, but still remaining ahead of Mirror Group Digital. Monthly users 
were up 7.1%. UK users accounted for 42% of online traffic.

Associated Newspapers' Metro returned to publishing figures – the Monday to 
Friday freesheet began in January and then did not supply figures in February – 
with daily average users at 255,394.

The London Evening Standard's website reported 154,751 daily average users 
last month.
