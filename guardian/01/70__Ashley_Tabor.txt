
Job: founder and executive president, Global Group
Age: 34
Industry: broadcasting
Annual revenue: £201.4m
Staff: 1,100
Salary: £2.5m (total remuneration)
2010 ranking: 72

Ashley Tabor <https://www.theguardian.com/media/ashley-tabor> is the most 
powerful commercial radio executive in the country.

Global Radio <https://www.theguardian.com/media/globalradio> has a 15.9% share 
of all listening in the UK, and his group includes Classic FM, the Heart 
network and London's 95.8 Capital FM.

The rollout of the Capital brand 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/sep/14/global-radio-capital-rebrand?INTCMP=SRCH>
 across the country coincided with the 38-year-old station returning to the
coveted number one commercial spot in London 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2011/may/12/capital-rajars-commercial-radio?INTCMP=SRCH>
.

Bankrolled by his father, the multimillionaire racehorse owner Michael Tabor 
<http://business.timesonline.co.uk/tol/business/industry_sectors/media/article4769451.ece>
, Global was created with the £545m purchase of GCap Media and Chrysalis Radio, 
though tough conditions for radio advertising have made the going hard since. 
The groupcut its pre-tax losses to £31m 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2011/jan/26/global-radio-2010-results?INTCMP=SRCH>
 in the year to March 2010.

Tabor's key lieutenants include ex-Capital boss Richard Park and former 
Associated Newspapers executive Stephen Miron 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2009/jul/10/stephen-miron-mediaguardian-100-2009>
. Global's chairman is former ITV chief executive Charles Allen.

The Global boss has criticised Radio 1 and Radio 2 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/oct/19/bbc-radio-1-radio-festival?INTCMP=SRCH>
 for failing in their public service remit and called on BBC bosses toinvest 
more money in digital radio 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/sep/15/global-radio-bbc-ashley-tabor-digital-radio?INTCMP=SRCH>
.

Tabor left school at 16 to work in local radio before setting up his own 
talent and music publishing business, looking after Corinne Bailey Rae.
