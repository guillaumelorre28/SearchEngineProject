
Regional newspaper group Archant <https://www.theguardian.com/media/archant> 
has appointed Jeff Henry, the former chief of ITV Consumer, as its chief 
executive.

Henry was most recently running FilmFlex, the video-on-demand service owned by 
Sony and Disney, but left followingthe sale of the business to US-based company 
Vubiquity in May 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/may/28/disney-sony-sell-filmflex-video-on-demand>
.

He will join Archant, which owns more than 130 titles including the Eastern 
Daily Press and the Ham & High newspapers, on 1 September.

His appointment follows the departure of Adrian Jeakings, a 12-year Archant 
veteran who had run the company since 2008, who stood down at the end of last 
month.

The group also runs Mustard TV, one of the city TV franchises awarded under 
former culture secretary Jeremy Hunt’s scheme to deliver localised programming 
across the UK.

“With our powerful portfolio of newspapers, magazines, websites, mobile apps, 
events and our newly-launched local TV station, this business has a great 
opportunity to be the most innovative local media business in the UK,” said the 
Archant chairman, Simon Bax.

Bax, the former chief financial officer at Pixar and 20th Century Fox, joined 
as chairman of Archant in April.
