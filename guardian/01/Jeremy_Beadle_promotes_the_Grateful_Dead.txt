
Others might locate the high point of the San Francisco acid rock scene to one 
of Bill Graham's promotions at the Fillmore. But, for sheer oddity, nothing can 
beat future TV pranksterJeremy Beadle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/jeremy-beadle> putting on a rock 
festival in a flood-ridden site outside Wigan, and booking the Grateful Dead to 
headline. So wet was the Bickershaw site – the area at the front of the stage 
was a bog – that the Dead began their 245-minute (yes, 245-minute) set with the 
words: "For all our muddy friends …"
