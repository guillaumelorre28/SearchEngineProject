
When the Federal Communications Commission ruled that broadcasters in major US 
markets could not duplicate programming on both their FM and AM frequencies, it 
probably didn't realise the boost it had given to underground rock. Faced with 
the need to fill hours of airtime cheaply, stations took to filling the hours 
on FM with DJs playing album cuts, creating a new radio outlet for music that 
would otherwise have been unbroadcastable, and birthing "album-oriented rock", 
that staple of the following decade.
