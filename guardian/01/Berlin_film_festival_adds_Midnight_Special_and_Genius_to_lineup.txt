
The Berlin film festival has released details of a clutch of new titles in its 
competition and special sections to join thealready announced opening film 
Hail, Caesar! 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/dec/04/coen-brothers-hail-caesar-to-open-berlin-film-festival>

Among the films having their world premiere on the Potsdamer Platz is Midnight 
Special <http://www.imdb.com/title/tt2649554/>, a thriller by Jeff “Mud” 
Nichols, which sees the director reunited with regular collaborator Michael 
Shannon. Shannon, who played a man haunted by apocalyptic visions in Nichols’ 
2011 filmTake Shelter 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2011/nov/24/take-shelter-review>, here takes 
the role of a father who goes on the run with his son, whose extrasensory 
powers make him a target of government agents and extremists.

Where to Invade Next review – Michael Moore gets happy with a sugar-binge 
idea-stealing session
 Read more  
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/11/where-to-invade-next-film-review-michael-moore-happy-toronto-tiff>
Also debuting in Berlin will be Genius <http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1703957/>, 
the Michael Grandage-directed study of authorThomas Wolfe 
<https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_Wolfe>’s relationship with his editor, 
starring Jude Law and Colin Firth and based on A Scott Berg’s 1978 biography 
Max Perkins: Editor of Genius. Meanwhile, Alone in Berlin, based onHans 
Fallada’s 1947 novel 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/jan/07/hans-fallada-alone-berlin-rereading>
 Every Man Dies Alone about a husband and wife who were executed during the war 
for anti-Nazi activities, is directed by Vincent Perez and stars Brendan 
Gleeson and Emma Thompson.

Berlin has also announced the debut of Zero Days, a new documentary from Going 
Clear director Alex Gibney 
<https://twitter.com/alexgibneyfilm/status/675329813553405952>, about internet 
surveillance and cybercrime –not to be confused 
<https://twitter.com/alexgibneyfilm/status/676361479331831808> with another 
documentary on a similar subject, currently entitled Zero Day, for whichGibney 
is acting as consulting producer 
<https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/1175064326/zero-day-a-film-about-cybercrime-and-threats-to-th/posts/1378191>
.

Three other documentaries have been picked as special sessions at Berlin. A 
study of seminal cultural critic John Berger,The Seasons in Quincy 
<http://www.jarmanlab.org/the-seasons-in-quincy-four-portraits-of-john-berger.html>
, directed by, among others, Colin MacCabe and Tilda Swinton, will have its 
world premiere; 20 Feet from Stardom director Morgan Neville’s record of the
Silk Road project, The Music of Strangers: Yo-Yo Ma and the Silk Road Ensemble 
<http://www.silkroadproject.org/posts/the-music-of-strangers>, will have its 
first European screening; and Michael Moore’s Where to Invade Next, which 
premiered at Toronto, will also be shown.

The Berlin film festival <https://www.theguardian.com/film/berlinfilmfestival> 
runs from 11 to 21 February.
