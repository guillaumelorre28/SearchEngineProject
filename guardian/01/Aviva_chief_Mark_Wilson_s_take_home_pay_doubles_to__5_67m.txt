
Aviva’s chief executive, Mark Wilson, saw his take home pay more than double 
to £5.67m last year after the purchase of Friends Life.


The insurer completed the £5.6bn acquisition last April, creating the UK’s 
largest insurer 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/dec/02/aviva-takeover-friends-life-uk-largest-insurer>
. It posted a 20% rise in operating profit for 2015 on Tuesday.

 Wilson received a bonus of £1.78m, or 182% of his basic salary – the maximum 
possible payout is 200% – due to “strong financial performance” and progress on 
strategic objectives last year, Avivasaid in its annual report 
<http://www.aviva.com/investor-relations/reports/>, published on Tuesday.

 Wilson, who joined Aviva at the start of 2013 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2012/nov/20/aviva-appoints-mark-wilson-chief-executive>
, also received his first payments from a long-term incentive plan. These 
totalled £2.56m based on a three-year performance period to the end of 2015. 
Aviva’s share price rose by 38% over the period.

 Legal & General’s chief executive, Nigel Wilson, earned £4.7m in 2015 and the 
RSA Insurance Group chief executive, Stephen Hester, earned £2.47m.
