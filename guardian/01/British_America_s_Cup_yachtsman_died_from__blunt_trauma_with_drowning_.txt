
The autopsy on the British yachtsman Andrew Simpson 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2013/may/10/andrew-simpson-british-sailing-golden-generation>
 who died during training for the America's Cup races showed he suffered 
extensive trauma to his head and neck, and drowned while trapped underwater for 
about 10 minutes.

The report by the San Francisco medical examiner says there were serious cuts 
and bruises to Simpson's head and neck, and his helmet was crushed. The Olympic 
gold medalist's cause of death was listed as "blunt trauma with drowning".

Simpson died on 9 May after his 72-foot catamaran nosedived and broke into 
pieces while training in San Francisco Bay with Artemis Racing.

The 36-year-old's death prompted America's Cup 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/americas-cup> organisers to implement 37 
safety recommendations, including equipping sailors with body armour.
