
Anthony Rose <https://www.theguardian.com/media/anthony-rose>, the chief 
technology officer of video-on-demand service YouView, is leaving the company 
after barely six months.

Rose, who joined the project to bring VoD to Freeview and Freesat in May from 
the BBC 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/mar/30/bbc-anthony-rose-project-canvas>
, will continue to work with the company in an advisory role on product 
development.

As the BBC controller of vision and online media for three years from 2007, 
Rose oversaw technical innovations including moving the iPlayer to a streaming 
service and plotting a blueprint for an Amazon-esque series of recommendation 
tools.

While Rose is not being directly replaced, Sef Tuma has been appointed head of 
technical delivery. Andrew Burdess, a former BT and NTL executive, is also 
joiningYouView <https://www.theguardian.com/media/youview> as director of 
operations.

YouView, a joint venture between the BBC, ITV, Channel 4, TalkTalk, BT, Arqiva 
and Channel 5, plans to launch by the end of June 2011,although executives have 
admitted that this may slip 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/sep/16/project-canvas-rebrands-as-youview>
.
