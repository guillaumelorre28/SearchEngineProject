<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/aug/29/99-homes-review-andrew-garfield-and-michael-shannon-flog-the-foundations-of-america>
Director: Ramin Bahrani
Running time: 112 minutes
Rating: R
Hardhitting drama about construction worker Andrew Garfield who is forced to 
take a job with the predatory foreclosure specialist who evicts him and his 
family.

October

The Martian
 <https://www.theguardian.com/film/the-martian>

Director: Ridley Scott
Running time: 142 minutes
Rating: PG-13
Matt Damon plays a can-do NASA astronaut who doesn’t let being stranded on 
Mars get in way of cracking wise.

He Named Me Malala 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/04/i-am-malala-review-awed-documentary-captures-extraordinary-subject>

Director: Davis Guggenheim
Running time: 87 minutes
Rating: PG-13
Documentary following Malala Yousafzai, the young woman shot in the head by 
the Taliban for promoting female education, as she becomes a civil rights 
superstar.

Jafar Panahi’s Taxi 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/nov/01/taxi-tehran-review-admirable-jafar-panahi-takes-to-the-streets>

Director: Jafar Panahi
Running time: 82 minutes
Rating: 12A
Handed an edict banning him from making films or leaving Iran, Jafar Panahi 
has gone rogue. His latest, in which he ferries people around Tehran in a taxi 
fitted out with a hidden camera, is another triumphant rebellion.
 Tom Hanks in Steven Spielberg’s Bridge of Spies. Photograph: PR 
Bridge of Spies <https://www.theguardian.com/film/bridge-of-spies>

Director: Steven Spielberg
Running time: 141 minutes
Rating: 12A
Steven Spielberg’s Cold War thriller, starring Tom Hanks as a lawyer tasked 
with mediating a spy swap between the US and Russia.

November

Brooklyn 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/nov/08/brooklyn-observer-film-review-saoirse-ronan>

Director: John Crowley
Running time: 112 minutes
Rating: 12A
Swooning immigrant story in which Saoirse Ronan plays a young Irish woman 
striking out for a new life in 1950s New York.

Spectre <https://www.theguardian.com/film/spectre>

Director: Sam Mendes
Running time: 148 minutes
Rating: 12A
Daniel Craig’s fourth Bond adventure romps from The Day of the Dead to 
Westminster Bridge as 007 chases down the head of a secret organisation that 
has been plotting his demise.


Spotlight 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/03/spotlight-review-catholic-church-child-abuse-film-decently-tells-an-awful-story>

Director: Thomas McCarthy
Running time: 129 minutes
Rating: 15
The true story of the Boston Globe investigative team who discovered that the 
Catholic church had been sheltering up to 90 priests accused of sexually 
abusing children. Starring Michael Keaton, Mark Ruffalo and Rachel McAdams.
