
Mail Online <https://www.theguardian.com/media/mail-online> defied the 
traditionally quiet month of August to hit a new traffic record, according to 
the latest figures from the Audit Bureau of Circulations Electronic published 
today.

Guardian.co.uk, Telegraph.co.uk, Mail Online and Independent.co.uk all showed 
modest traffic rises from July, while Mirror Group Digital's sites saw a slight 
month-on-month drop.

Mail Online remains the most visited UK newspaper site, both in terms of daily 
average browsers and monthly users, reaching an average 2,552,511 users each 
day and 45,539,202 unique users during the month. This was up 2.31% and 2.99% 
from July respectively. Mail Online's traffic is up 51.33% from August last 
year.

Guardian.co.uk remains the second most popular site, recording 1,941,540 
average daily browsers, up 0.41% from July and up 34.59% year on year.

Telegraph.co.uk saw traffic rise 3.56% from July to 1,681,152 daily browsers 
with year-on-year figures up 31.84%.

Mirror Group Digital's daily browsers fell 2.78% to 499,645, but this was up 
13.12% from August 2009. Independent.co.uk was up 3.94% from July to 490,499 
daily browsers. Independent.co.uk's traffic is up 33.69% year on year.
