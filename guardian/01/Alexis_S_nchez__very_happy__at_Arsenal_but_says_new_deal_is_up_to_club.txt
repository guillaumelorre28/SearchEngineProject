
Alexis Sánchez <https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/sanchez-alexis> says 
he is “very happy” at Arsenal but insists it is up to the club to come up with 
a deal to keep him at the Emirates Stadium.

The 27-year-old has 18 months remaining on his contract but both he and Mesut 
Özil are reportedly seeking parity with the Premier League’s top earners before 
extending their deals. Sánchez has been linked with a £400,000-a-week move to 
the Chinese Super League andArsène Wenger 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/arsene-wenger> is unhappy with the 
continued questioning regarding the future of his top goalscorer.

Sánchez, whose side visit Manchester City on Sunday, has kept quiet about the 
situation up until now but has put the ball firmly in Arsenal’s court. “It 
really doesn’t depend on me,” he told Sky Sports when asked about a new 
contract. “If they want to show confidence in me. I’m very happy at the club. I 
love all the staff. I’m very grateful for everybody here. I’m focused more on 
winning football matches – I’m staying concentrated on that.”

The Chile forward went on to set out his targets. “It depends on Arsenal,” he 
added. “I enjoy the relationship with the fans. I want to achieve more for 
them. I want to win thePremier League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/premierleague> and the Champions League.”
