
Audrey Niffenegger <http://audreyniffenegger.com/> was born in Michigan in 
1963 and trained as a visual artist. In 2003, she published her debut novel,The 
Time Traveler's Wife 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2004/jan/31/featuresreviews.guardianreview17>
, originally imagined as a graphic novel, which was a worldwide bestseller and 
turned intoa film <http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=USUDlMBR-dQ>. Her second 
novel,Her Fearful Symmetry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2009/oct/10/her-fearful-symmetry-niffenegger-review>
, was published in 2009. She is a professor at theColumbia College Chicago 
Centre for Book and Paper Arts 
<http://www.colum.edu/Academics/Interarts/index.php?site=CBPA>. Next Saturday, 
she will talk atthe BD & Comics Passion festival at the Institut Français in 
London <http://www.institut-francais.org.uk/programme/bd-comics-passion>.

When were you happiest? 
When I am drawing or writing and it's going well.

What is your greatest fear? 
Worldwide economic collapse.

What is your earliest memory? 
Standing in my crib, looking at my bedroom in morning light.

Which living person do you most admire, and why? 
Patti Smith <http://www.pattismith.net/>, because she is wise.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself? 
Cynicism.

What is the trait you most deplore in others? 
Perkiness.

Property aside, what's the most expensive thing you've bought? 
An etching press – it was not the most expensive in dollars, but the most 
expensive relative to the amount of money I had at the time.

What is your most treasured possession? 
A friend gave me a ladle that belonged to Edgar Allen Poe 
<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edgar_allan_poe>. I keep meaning to send it to 
the Poe Museum.

Where would you like to live? 
In the British Library <http://www.bl.uk/>.

What would your super power be? 
To identify typefaces at a glance.

What makes you unhappy? 
Unidentified typefaces.

What is your favourite smell? 
Ink.

If you could bring something extinct back to life, what would you choose? 
An extinct language – there are so many to choose from.

What is your most unappealing habit? 
I don't drink alcohol, which makes people nervous and suspicious.

What is the worst thing anyone's ever said to you? 
A critic once called me sentimental.

What or who is the love of your life? 
My work.

Which living person do you most despise and why? 
Gosh, there are so many to choose from: Dick Cheney, Karl Rove…

Which phrase do you most overuse? 
"Two sugars, no milk, please."

What has been your biggest disappointment? 
The Rapture 
<http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2011/05/23/national/main20065398.shtml> – I was 
hoping it would take away all the folks who wanted to be taken away, leaving 
room for the rest of us to get on with things.

If you could edit your past, what would you change? 
I'd skip Catholicism and plunge directly into being a heathen.

When did you last cry, and why? 
Watching Spike Lee's documentary about Hurricane Katrina 
<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=8TRWI4CFkU0&feature=player_embedded>.

What is the closest you've come to death? 
Asthma attacks. I don't get them any more, thank goodness.

What single thing would improve the quality of your life? 
Fewer Republicans in Congress.

What do you consider your greatest achievement? 
Completing two novels.

What song would you like played at your funeral? 
I'll Sleep When I'm Dead, by Warren Zevon 
<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0otLhqSYCo0>.

How would you like to be remembered? 
I'm not picky – I will be happy to be remembered at all.
