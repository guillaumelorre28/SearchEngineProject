
Mobile phone game Fruit Ninja is to follow The Angry Birds 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/angry-birds> Movie into cinemas, in a 
move sparked by the latter’s impressive box-office results.

According to the Hollywood Reporter 
<http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/heat-vision/fruit-ninja-movie-works-896056>, 
Halfbrick Studios, the makers ofthe popular smartphone fruit-slicing game 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2015/sep/28/how-the-australian-gaming-app-fruit-ninja-reached-one-billion-downloads>
, have partnered withHansel & Gretel: Witch Hunters 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2013/feb/28/hansel-gretel-witch-hunters-review>
 producer Tripp Vinson to create “a live-action family comedy” inspired by the 
game. Few details of the proposed project have emerged, other than that the 
script is due to be written by JP Lavin and Chad Damiani, previously 
responsible for the as-yet-unproduced crime romance Kamikaze Love,which 
appeared on the 2007 Black List 
<http://deadline.com/2007/12/black-list-2007-1-danny-strongs-recount-4316/> of 
most-liked screenplays.


Fruit Ninja is one of the most popular phone games of all time, registering 
over a billion downloads andsecond only to Candy Crush on the list of most 
popular iOS games. 
<http://www.techlicious.com/blog/top-10-apple-iphone-and-ipad-apps-of-all-time-most-downloaded/>
 The Angry Birds Movie registered a solid $39m at the US box office on its 
first weekend on release on 20 May, contributing to over $150m so far, 
worldwide.
