
When a 23-year-old Asha Bhosle first met RD Burman in 1956, she was an 
experienced playback singer, and Burman the teenage son of a legendary 
composer. Ten years later, they would collaborate on their first film, Teesri 
Manzil, starting a creative and romantic partnership that revolutionised Hindi 
film music. Burman introduced elements of funk, jazz and garage rock into his 
sound, while Asha's voice – sexy and slightly cheeky – providedBollywood 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/bollywood> with a daringly risqué element.
