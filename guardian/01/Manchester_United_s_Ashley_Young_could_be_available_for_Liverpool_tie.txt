
Ashley Young may be available for Manchester United’s crucial Europa League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/uefa-europa-league> last-16 second leg 
against Liverpool at Old Trafford on Thursday after training with the squad on 
Wednesday morning.

Young has not played for United since the 1-0 league victory at Liverpool 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jan/17/liverpool-manchester-united-premier-league-match-report>
 on 17 January due to a serious groin injury. The winger required surgery and 
the manager, Louis van Gaal, stated he would be out for a “long time”.

Young, 30, may also be joined by Cameron Borthwick-Jackson, as he also 
trained. The 19-year-old full-back sustained a knock that has ruled him out 
since theFA Cup win at Shrewsbury Town 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/feb/22/shrewsbury-town-manchester-united-fa-cup-match-report>
 on 22 February.

United trail Liverpool 2-0 from the first leg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/mar/10/liverpool-manchester-united-europa-league-match-report>
 and know that if Jürgen Klopp’s side score an away goal they will have to 
score four times to progress.
