
Ashley Young has signed a new contract with Manchester United 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchester-united>, keeping him at Old 
Trafford until June 2018 with the option of a further year.

The 30-year-old winger, who joined from Aston Villa in June 2011, had an 
impressive 2014-15 season and flourished particularly in the second half of the 
campaign, scoring United’s first goal in their4-2 derby win against Manchester 
City in April 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/apr/12/manchester-united-manchester-city-premier-league-match-report>
.

“We are delighted that Ashley has signed a new contract,” said the Manchester 
United manager, Louis van Gaal. “Ashley is a very multi-functional player. He 
took to the wing-back position with great confidence and flourished on the wing 
throughout last season. Ashley is always very professional and has a great 
presence in the dressing room.”

Young, who has made 115 appearances for United and scored 13 goals, said: “I 
am delighted to have signed a new contract. The spirit and motivation amongst 
this team is very high and we are all determined to make this a season to 
remember. All I have ever wanted to do is play football and to be able to 
continue to do that at the biggest club in the world means everything to me and 
my family.”

Young’s previous contract had been due to expire next summer and last month he 
stressed his wish to remain with the club 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/jul/22/ashley-young-manchester-united-contract>
.
