
1 Le Grand Macabre

Collaborations with director Peter Sellars 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2010/nov/08/peter-sellars-interview> look 
set to be a regular feature of Simon Rattle’s years with theLondon Symphony 
Orchestra <https://www.theguardian.com/music/london-symphony-orchestra>. Their 
latest project is a semi-staging ofGyörgy Ligeti 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/tomserviceblog/2012/aug/27/gyorgy-ligeti-contemporary-music-guide>
’s only opera, an absurdist vision of the end of the world.
Barbican Hall, EC2, Saturday 14 Jan and Sunday 15 Jan 
<https://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=19270>

2 The Last Supper

Harrison Birtwistle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/may/03/harrison-birtwistle-hindsight-not-natural-musician-composers>
’s dramatic tableaux, first seen in Berlin in 2000, gets a rare outing. Martyn 
Brabbins conducts this semi-staging, with theBBC Scottish Symphony Orchestra 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/bbc-scottish-symphony-orchestra> and a cast 
led by Roderick Williams as Christ and Daniel Norman as Judas.
City Halls, Glasgow, Saturday 14 Jan 
<http://www.bbc.co.uk/events/ezjzc8#p04p77x0>

3 Bergen Philharmonic Orchestra

Edward Gardner 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2012/jun/12/edward-gardner-conductor> has 
been the Bergen Philharmonic’s principal conductor since 2015; now they tour 
the UK together for the first time. There’s music fromGrieg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/edvard-grieg>, Elgar and Bartók, with Baiba 
Skride, Truls Mørk and Leonard Elschenbroich sharing duties as concerto 
soloists.
Saffron Walden, Sunday 15 Jan 
<https://saffronhall.com/calendar/bergen-philharmonic-orchestra/>; Birmingham, 
Tuesday 17 Jan <https://www.thsh.co.uk/event/bc-bergen-philharmonic-orchestra>; 
Basingstoke,Wednesday 18 Jan 
<http://www.anvilarts.org.uk/whats-on/bergen-philharmonic-orchestra>; Sheffield,
Thursday 19 Jan 
<http://www.sheffieldcityhall.co.uk/events/Bergen-Philharmonic-Orchesta>; 
London,Friday 20 Jan 
<https://www.cadoganhall.com/event/zios1617-bergen-philharmonic-orchestra/> 

4 Royal Liverpool Philharmonic

Vasily Petrenko adds the rarely played 12th symphony, The Year 1917, to his 
Liverpool series ofShostakovich 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/dmitri-shostakovich> symphonies. It is 
preceded by Emily Howard’s Torus, her concerto for orchestra commissioned for 
the 175th anniversary of the RLPO.
Philharmonic Hall, Liverpool, Thursday 19 Jan 
<http://www.liverpoolphil.com/whats-on/100-years-of-revolution> 

5 BBC Symphony Orchestra

Oliver Knussen 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/jun/06/oliver-knussen-fiona-maddocks-interview>
’s appearances with the BBCSO invariably feature the unexpected. This time, 
it’s Elgar’s symphonic study Falstaff. The programme begins with Strauss’s 
Macbeth, and includesthe first outing of Philip Cashian’s The Book Of Ingenious 
Devices <http://www.bbc.co.uk/events/epgv2m>.
Barbican Hall, EC2, Friday 20 Jan 
<https://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=19134>
