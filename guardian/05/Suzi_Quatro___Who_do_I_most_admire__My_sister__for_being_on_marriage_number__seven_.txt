
Born in Detroit, Suzi Quatro 
<https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2014/feb/08/suzi-quatro-playing-guitar-do-something-creative>
, 66, played in her father’s jazz band, and at 14 formed her own, all-girl 
group. She moved to the UK in 1971, and has since had 18 hit singles, including
Can The Can <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xYoogY-UGio> and Devil Gate Drive 
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9vFTksaposs>, selling more than 55m records. 
Last month, she was made anhonorary doctor of music by Anglia Ruskin University 
<http://www.anglia.ac.uk/news/chelmsford-and-cambridge-ready-for-graduations>. 
She is married for the second time, has two children and lives in Essex.

When were you happiest?
When my first child was born – something I thought would never happen.

What is your greatest fear?
Departing this Earth before I have a chance to finish my bucket list.

What is your earliest memory?
My baby sister coming home from hospital when I was three. I got jealous and 
bit her fingers quite hard.

Which living person do you most admire, and why?
My sister Arlene, who is 75 and on marriage number seven. I admire her 
hopefulness.

What was your most embarrassing moment?
During a show, I asked everyone to get up, and everyone did except one person. 
I asked the spotlight to shine down on this party-pooper, only to discover he 
was in a wheelchair.

Property aside, what’s the most expensive thing you’ve bought?
A mink coat in 1975 (it was the done thing then). It cost me £5,500.

What makes you unhappy?
Dissent in the family.

Who would play you in the film of your life?
Doris Day <https://www.theguardian.com/film/doris-day> or Bette Davis 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/bettedavis>.

What is your most unappealing habit?
Talking too loud.

What is your guiltiest pleasure?
I love, love, love, Barry Manilow 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/barry-manilow>.

What is top of your bucket list?
To make a movie. I’ve worked in every other medium.

What did you want to be when you were growing up?
An entertainer.

What is the worst thing anyone’s said to you?
“I don’t like you, and I don’t know why.” That broke my heart.

What do you owe your parents?
Everything I am and everything I hope to be. My mother was straight as an 
arrow, my moral compass; my dad gave me my career.

What does love feel like?
Like coming home in every sense.

What was the best kiss of your life?
My first proper one when I was about 13. My lips were sore for weeks.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party?
Elvis Presley, Otis Redding 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/oct/21/otis-redding-interview-rocks-back-pages-mr-cool-and-the-clique-from-memphis>
,Billie Holiday <https://www.theguardian.com/music/billie-holiday>, Jesus, 
Abraham Lincoln and Winston Churchill.

Which words or phrases do you most overuse?
I always say, “I know”, even when I don’t.

What has been your biggest disappointment?
Not being able to have more kids.

If you could go back in time, where would you go?
To Egypt, to watch the pyramids being built.

When did you last cry, and why?

How often do you have sex?
Often enough for a 66-year-old.

What do you consider your greatest achievement?
Remaining normal.

What song would you like played at your funeral?
When I Fall In Love, by Nat King Cole 
<https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GfAb0gNPy6s>.

What is the most important lesson life has taught you?
To roll with the punches.
