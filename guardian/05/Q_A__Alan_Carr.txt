
Born in Dorset, Carr, 39, studied drama at Middlesex University. In 2001 he 
won the BBC New Comedy Award for standup and in 2009 he was given his own 
Channel 4 show, Alan Carr: Chatty Man. He won a Bafta in 2013, and was voted 
best chatshow host at this year’s National Television Awards. His latest DVD is
Alan Carr <https://www.theguardian.com/culture/alan-carr> – Yap, Yap, Yap! 
Live. He lives with his partner in London.

When were you happiest?
When I am out by myself walking the dogs, with a bin nearby for the dog shit.

What is your greatest fear?
Being eaten by a shark.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
Slyness.

What was your most embarrassing moment?
I did a photoshoot where I was dressed as Agnetha from Abba and my ballbag was 
hanging out. Apparently, my legs were open a bit too wide. I still cringe.

Aside from a property, what’s the most expensive thing you’ve bought?
A new sporty Mini, recently.

What is your most treasured possession?
My two Irish setters. I love them.

What would your super power be?
To mentally undress people.

What do you most dislike about your appearance?
The list keeps getting longer. I have just realised I’ve got a humped back.

Who would play you in the film of your life?
Daniel Craig.

To whom would you most like to say sorry, and why?
The maître d’ at the Fat Duck 
<https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/the-fat-duck>. I was a bit tiddly and 
I started stroking his arm, and fell off my seat.

What is your most unappealing habit?
Talking over people who are boring and not as interesting as me.

What is the worst thing anyone’s said to you?
We were staying with my partner Paul’s family a couple of years back and we 
went to visit these two old women. One said, “There was me saying how I 
couldn’t stand you, and here you are standing in my kitchen.”

What or who is the greatest love of your life?
Paul.

What does love feel like?
An elasticated waist round a pot belly.

What was the best kiss of your life?
With Bev, my eldest red setter, when I’ve had a Hobnob.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party?
Madonna, Elizabeth I, Prince Harry, Lionel Blair (I love that man), Angela 
Lansbury and Paul O’Grady.

Which words or phrases do you most overuse?
“Back off – not the face, not the face”, when people want selfies and I’m 
looking minging.

What is the worst job you’ve done?
I had to collect the trolleys at Brent Cross Tesco.

If you could edit your past, what would you change?
I was such a worrier at school. I wish I could go back and tell myself to stop 
stressing.

When did you last cry, and why?
JM Coetzee’s book Disgrace <https://www.theguardian.com/books/disgrace>. That 
last page – I was in bits.

How often do you have sex?
Not enough because I am on tour – and I’ve only got so many groupies.

What song would you like played at your funeral?
Peggy Lee – The Party’s Over <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-IZk7jP0LkM>.

How would you like to be remembered?
As a nice guy.
