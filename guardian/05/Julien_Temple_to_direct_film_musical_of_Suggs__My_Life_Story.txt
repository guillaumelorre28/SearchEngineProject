
Madness singer Suggs is having his autobiographical stage show, My Life Story 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/aug/30/singly-does-it-suggs-new-soliloquists>
, turned into a film musical directed by Julien Temple,according to Screen Daily
 
<http://www.screendaily.com/news/production/julien-temple-to-make-suggs-movie/5108877.article?blocktitle=LATEST-FILM-NEWS-HEADLINES&contentID=44435>
.

Suggs, real name Graham McPherson, has been performing his stage show since 
2012. Incorporating speech, standup and excerpts of songs byMadness 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/madness> and their contemporaries, it tells 
the story of Suggs struggling to find meaning in life as he enters his 50s.

Temple, director of The Filth and the Fury, The Great Rock’n’Roll Swindle and 
The Ecstasy of Wilko Johnson 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jul/16/the-ecstasy-of-wilko-johnson-review-dr-feelgood>
, will take the show one step beyond by combining live performance and archive 
clips with dramatised scenes from McPherson’s life. The film, which starts 
shooting in Hoxton, London next month, will be screened next year.

Suggs, now 55, has been the lead singer of Madness since 1977, helping the 
group to 15 UK Top 10 hits before they broke up in 1986. His solo career, which 
was launched in the wake of Madness’s reunion in 1992, led to the singles 
Cecilia and Camden Town. He’s supplemented his singing with stints as a TV 
presenter, radio DJ and author.
