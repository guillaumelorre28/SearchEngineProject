
The UK's leading shares opened sharply down this morning as fears over the 
Eurozone <https://www.theguardian.com/business/eurozone> debt crisis grew.

The FTSE <https://www.theguardian.com/business/ftse> 100 index was down by 
almost 80 points shortly after the markets opened. Only one of the top 100 
stocks by market capitalisation, Icap, was in positive territory.

Fears over the Eurozone have seen the dollar rise, with commodities 
denominated in the US currency falling as a result. Brent crude futures were 
down over 2%, reaching $110 a barrel.

Asian markets also fell overnight, with the Nikkei 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/nikkei> down 1.5% at the close.

Standard & Poor's cut its rating outlook for Italy to negative from stable 
over the weekend, whileFitch downgraded Greece's credit rating to B-plus and 
put the country on rating watch negative on Friday 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/business/marketforceslive/2011/may/20/ftse-falls-on-greek-woes>
.
