
Liverpool’s Roberto Firmino was targeted by burglars less than two days before 
the Brazilian was arrested and charged with drink-driving, local police have 
confirmed.

The Liverpool Echo have reported 
<http://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/news/liverpool-news/roberto-firminos-home-raided-hooded-12425279>
 that Firmino’s house, in the Mossley Hill area of the city, was burgled on 22 
December by a gang who stole £70,000 worth of items. Firmino and his family 
were moved to a hotel, with the club later placing a security team at the home.

“While the occupiers were not in at the time, it has clearly been very 
upsetting to find their home has been targeted in this way,” detective 
inspector Steve Christian ofLiverpool <https://www.theguardian.com/uk/liverpool>
 CID told the Echo. “I would ask the offenders to do the right thing and return 
the stolen items to the owner in any way possible.”

Firmino was stopped in the early hours of Christmas Eve in Liverpool city 
centre andcharged with drink driving 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/dec/26/roberto-firmino-charged-drink-driving-liverpool>
. He is due to appear in court on 31 January – the same day Liverpool host 
Chelsea.

The burglary follows an attempted raid on Wayne Rooney’s home in August, 
carried out while the Manchester United forward played in a club testimonial. 
Robert McNamara, a former serviceman from Scarborough, wasjailed for the 
attempted burglary 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/dec/21/wayne-rooney-burglary-attempt-man-jailed>
