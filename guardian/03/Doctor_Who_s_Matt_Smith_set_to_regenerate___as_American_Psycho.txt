
Talk about a regeneration: Doctor Who star Matt Smith 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/matt-smith> is to follow his four 
years as the Time Lord by playingBret Easton Ellis 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/breteastonellis>'s sadistic creation Patrick 
Bateman in a new musical adaptation ofAmerican Pyscho 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/american-psycho>.

Smith, 30, will lead the new musical as the yuppie banker – famously played by 
Christian Bale inthe film version 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/82514/american.psycho> – who retreats 
into an increasingly violent secret life of torture, rape and murder. The show 
is set to open at the Almeida theatre in London this December, directed by 
incoming artistic director Rupert Goold in a co-production with Headlong 
theatre.

It will be Smith's first stage role in five years, since his performance in 
the West End transfer ofPolly Stenham 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/polly-stenham>'s That Face 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2008/may/12/theatre1>, for which he won the 
Evening Standard outstanding newcomer award. After abandoning a career in 
football, Smith joined the National Youth Theatre before performing in several 
plays, includingAlan Bennett <https://www.theguardian.com/stage/alanbennett>'s 
The History Boys andEnda Walsh's Chatroom 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2006/mar/16/theatre> at the National theatre.

Smith has just finished filming his final episode as the Doctor with The Thick 
of It'sPeter Capaldi 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/2013/aug/04/peter-capaldi-doctor-who>
, who is set to take over in the role. American Psycho, which has a book by 
Roberto Aguirre-Sacasa and music and lyrics by Duncan Sheik, will be the 
actor's first stage musical.

The production will complete the Falling Headlong 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/apr/22/theatre-turns-to-facebook> 
season, which has already producedThe Effect 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2012/nov/14/the-effect-lucy-prebble-review> 
by Lucy Prebble and Lucy Kirkwood'sChimerica 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/may/29/chimerica-review>. It will be 
Goold's final production for the company, after eight years in charge.

American Pyscho's cast also includes Susannah Fielding 
<http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/from-icecream-seller-to-superstar-susannah-fielding-hits-the-west-end-stage-8810139.html>
, currently starring in Michael Grandage's A Midsummer Night's Dream, 
Broadchurch star Jonathan Bailey, and the former X Factor contestantLucie Jones 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2009/nov/09/tv-ratings-jedward-x-factor>.
