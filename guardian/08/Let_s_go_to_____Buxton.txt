
Why go now?


To listen to some great music: the Royal Liverpool Philharmonic at Buxton 
Opera House <http://buxtonoperahouse.org.uk/>; gigs at the Pavilion Arts Centre 
<http://buxtonoperahouse.org.uk/pavilion-arts-centre>; the Northern Chamber 
Orchestra <http://www.ncorch.co.uk/> at St John’s Church; a live-music 
screening of the Snowman <http://buxtonoperahouse.org.uk/the-snowman> in the 
Pavilion Gardens <http://www.paviliongardens.co.uk/> (29 November).

What else is there to do?


Follow the heritage trail at visitbuxton.co.uk <http://www.visitbuxton.co.uk/>
, taking in the Georgian Crescent and Pump Room. Pampering is on offer in the 
18th-centuryDevonshire Dome <http://www.devonshiredome.co.uk/>, while the 
Crescent is getting a £46m makeover into a five-star spa hotel.

Anywhere good to eat?


Newcomer Harley’s <http://www.harleysbuxton.co.uk/#home> has some imaginative 
veggie options and great Derbyshire meat dishes.Columbine 
<http://www.columbinerestaurant.co.uk> uses local suppliers for dishes such as 
mixed game pie with stout. TheDome restaurant 
<http://www.devonshiredome.co.uk/restaurant/> is the best bargain: £15 for 
three courses, cooked and served by students from the University of Derby.

How about a drink?


Monk <http://www.buxtoncafebar.co.uk/> serves fantastic seasonal cocktails. 53 
Degrees North <https://www.facebook.com/53degreesnorthbuxton> has cask ales, 
log fires and acoustic music afternoons. TheBuxton Tap House 
<http://www.buxtonbrewery.co.uk/tap/> serves 18 Buxton beers, plus guest ales. 
The real ale trail atpaviliongardens.co.uk 
<http://www.paviliongardens.co.uk/sites/default/files/Buxton%20Real%20Ale%20Trail%20web%20version.pdf>
 takes in 17 pubs and bars.

Anywhere to stay for under £100?

Bar/restaurant Number 13 <http://www.number13.co.uk/> has doubles from £50. 
The Old Hall Hotel <http://www.oldhallhotelbuxton.co.uk/> has a winter offer: 
£109 for a double room, including mulled wine, two-course dinner and breakfast.
