
Why go now?
This area of outstanding natural beauty (AONB), which covers a quarter of 
Shropshire, haswonderful autumn walks <http://www.shropshirewalking.co.uk> 
through heather-covered hills, valleys and ancient woodland.

What else is there to do?
There are charming towns and villages in and around the AONB: the Shropshire 
Hills Discovery Centre <http://www.shropshirehillsdiscoverycentre.co.uk/> is a 
good place to start. Church Stretton is the walking heart; Ludlow is the foodie 
hub; Bishop’s Castle is packed with pubs; Craven Arms is the cultural capital; 
and quiet Clun is full of history. Find more about all of these at
visitsouthshropshire.co.uk <http://www.visitsouthshropshire.co.uk/>. Medieval 
Shrewsbury, to the north, is the nearest big town, with lots of attractions 
includingnew prison tours <http://www.jailhousetours.com/>.

Anywhere good to eat?
It has to be Ludlow. The cafe at Ludlow Food Centre 
<http://www.ludlowfoodcentre.co.uk/Content.aspx?ID=1>, called the Ludlow Kitchen
 <http://www.ludlowkitchen.co.uk/Content.aspx?ID=1>, champions local food and 
serves a delicious platter of pork pie, cured meats and cheeses.Mortimers 
<https://www.facebook.com/MortimersLudlow/timeline?ref=page_internal> is a new 
modern British/French restaurant that opens next month and is taking bookings 
now. Lovers of fine dining should get to Michelin-starredMr Underhills 
<http://www.mr-underhills.co.uk/> before it finally closes on 20 December – for 
a nine-course, daily changing market menu (from £67.50).

How about a drink?
Kerry Vale Vineyard <http://www.kerryvalevineyard.co.uk/>, to the west of the 
AONB, runs tours and tastings three times a week.The Three Tuns Inn 
<http://www.thethreetunsinn.co.uk/> in Bishops Castles dates back to 1642, and 
serves ales from the adjoining brewery.

Anywhere to stay for under £100?
The Queens <http://thequeensludlow.com/>, a pub/restaurant in Ludlow, added 
three rooms last year (doubles from £95). Pen-y-Dyffryn near Oswestry is a 
multi-award-winning country house hotel (doubles from £95, peny.co.uk). Other 
options include the four-starCastle Hotel in Bishop’s Castle 
<http://www.thecastlehotelbishopscastle.co.uk/> (doubles from £95); the 
five-starRagleth House B&B <http://www.raglethhouse.co.uk/> in Church Stretton 
(doubles from £85); and the refurbishedYHA Wilderhope Manor 
<http://www.yha.org.uk/hostel/wilderhope-manor>, which has a four-poster bridal 
suite (£39, dorm beds from £18).
