
A panel appointed by India’s top court has proposed two-year suspensions on 
Chennai Super Kings and Rajasthan Royals of theIndian Premier League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/may/24/ipl-final-mumbai-indians-chennai-super-kings>
 in a corruption scandal that erupted two years ago.

Set up in January by the supreme court, the panel also recommended life 
suspensions on the Chennai Super Kings official Gurunath Meiyappan and 
Rajasthan Royals’ Raj Kundra from all cricket-related activities.

Meiyappan, the son-in-law of the International Cricket 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/cricket> Council chairman, Narayanswamy 
Srinivasan, and Kundra, a former Rajasthan co-owner, had been found guilty of 
illegal betting.

Controversy swirled around the 2013 editions of the league with the former 
India <https://www.theguardian.com/world/india> paceman Sreesanth and two other 
Rajasthan cricketers being arrested on suspicion of taking money to concede a 
fixed number of runs.

Sreesanth, who denied any wrongdoing, was subsequently banned for life by the 
Indian board.
