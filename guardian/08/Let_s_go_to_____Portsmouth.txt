
Why go now?


Ben Ainslie’s new British racing HQ 
<https://www.portsmouth.gov.uk/ext/development-and-planning/regeneration/the-camber-development.aspx>
 opens this month, a sail-like building at the Camber in Old Portsmouth. 
Visitors can watch the team training on the Solent, ahead of theAmerica’s Cup 
<http://www.americascup.com/en/world-series-AC45.html> there in July. May also 
marks the 250th anniversary of HMS Victory’s launch; it’s on view at the
Historic Dockyard <http://www.historicdockyard.co.uk/>.

Anything for non-sailors?


Loads of summer festivals: Festivities 
<http://www.portsmouthfestivities.co.uk/> (19-28 June) for arts of all kinds, 
Southsea Fest (20 September) <http://www.southseafest.com/>for comedy and food, 
andVictorious <http://www.victoriousfestival.co.uk/> (29-30 August) for music – 
Ray Davies and the Flaming Lips are headlining.Live at the Bandstand 
<http://www.visitportsmouth.co.uk/whats-on/live-at-the-bandstand-p851541> is a 
series of free Sunday gigs, starting on 31 May.

The 170m Spinnaker Tower <http://www.spinnakertower.co.uk/>, which turns 10 
this summer, has panoramic views. TheCharles Dickens trail 
<http://www.charlesdickensbirthplace.co.uk/images/uploads/Dickens-trail-leaflet-July-2014-v3.pdf>

Any decent food?

 Southsea Beach Cafe 
The new Southsea Beach Cafe <http://www.southseabeachcafe.co.uk/> is a scenic 
spot for breakfast: the eggs Benedict on bubble’n’squeak cake comes with ham 
hock and tomato hollandaise. HipSouthsea Coffee 
<http://www.southseacoffee.co.uk/>’s great cakes include red velvet with 
strawberry-coconut cream. AtPie & Vinyl <http://www.pieandvinyl.co.uk/>, diners 
browse the records and scoff vegan pies. For posher fare,Restaurant 27 
<http://www.restaurant27.com/> has a seven-course tasting menu for £39, and 
Bistro Montparnasse 
<http://bistromontparnasse.com/menus.php?mid=22&t=Spring+food+%26+wine+pairing+dinner>
 has three wine-and-food pairing dinners (£59.50) in May.

How about a drink?

 Beers at Meat and Barrel 
Southsea Castle’s fantastic new Courtyard restaurant 
<http://www.thecourtyardsouthseacastle.co.uk/> becomes a champagne bar on 
Friday nights. The recently openedMeat and Barrel 
<http://www.meatandbarrel.co.uk/> has craft ale and burgers, and the Brewhouse 
and Kitchen <http://www.brewhouseandkitchen.com/portsmouth> serves its own 
beers, including a liquorice porter and a raspberry wheat beer.
 A bedroom at Florence Gardens 
Any hotels for under £100 a night?


Florence Gardens <http://www.florencegardens.co.uk/index.html> (doubles from 
£59.95 B&B) is the latest in the Mercer Collection of lovely boutique hotels, 
all in Southsea. And the unusualG! Boutique Hotel 
<http://www.g-boutiquehotel.co.uk> (doubles from £80 B&B) has hanging 
bubble-pod chairs and cupcakes on arrival.


Do I need a car?


There are direct rail routes from London (90 minutes), Bristol, Southampton, 
Cardiff, Exeter, Reading and Brighton; more information atvisitportsmouth.co.uk 
<http://www.visitportsmouth.co.uk/visitor-information/travel>. For drivers, 
there is a newpark and ride service <http://parkandride.portsmouth.gov.uk/>
