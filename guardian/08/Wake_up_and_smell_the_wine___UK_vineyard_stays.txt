
Llanerch Vineyard, Vale of Glamorgan


Planted in 1986, Llanerch’s vineyard, which is just 20 minutes from Cardiff, 
produces award-winning wines under the Cariad label. There are two restaurants, 
one serving contemporary dishes for fine dining and a less formal bistro. 
Volunteers who help bring in the harvest each October are fêted with food, wine 
and a live band at its completion. Accommodation choices consist of two suites 
in the main farmhouse, eight rooms and an apartment.
• Doubles from £125 B&B, llanerch-vineyard.co.uk 
<http://www.llanerch-vineyard.co.uk/stay/introduction/>

Jabajak Vineyard Restaurant and Rooms, Carmarthenshire


Located close to where Pembrokeshire, Carmarthenshire and Ceredigion meet in 
south-west Wales, on the edge of the bluestonePreseli Hills 
<https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2014/sep/27/narberth-wales-pembrokeshire-food-festival>
 (the probable origin of the stones used for building Stonehenge), Jabajak 
combines its vineyard with award-winning food and 10 charming, cosy en-suite 
rooms. There are four restaurants to choose from in the carriage house, stable, 
milking parlour and grain store of the original farm, with all the food made 
from seasonal, local produce, much of it grown on site or foraged. Seyval and 
phoenix grapes are grown in the vineyards, from which a white with elderflower 
notes and a sparkling rosé is produced under the White House label.
• Doubles from £110pn B&B, jabajak.co.uk/rooms <http://jabajak.co.uk/rooms/>
