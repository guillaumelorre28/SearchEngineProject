
Monday

Told by the Wind is a dance-theatre piece created by Kaite O’Reilly, Jo 
Shapland and Phillip Zarrilli and it’s at theNorthcott in Exeter 
<https://exeternorthcott.co.uk/calendar/told-by-the-wind/> tonight. Thom 
Southerland, who has done such a great job of reviving neglected or less than 
praised musicals, revives Ragtime at theCharing Cross theatre in London 
<http://charingcrosstheatre.co.uk/theatre/ragtime>. Medea’s children tell their 
story in My Mother Medea atthe Unicorn, London 
<https://www.unicorntheatre.com/My%20Mother%20Medea>.

Tuesday

How would you treat the workers if you owned a factory? Would you value people 
or profit? Find out in the interactive World Factory at theCambridge Junction 
<http://www.junction.co.uk/world-factory>. The tour of John McGrath’s The 
Cheviot, the Stag and the Black, Black Oil 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/sep/14/the-cheviot-the-stag-and-the-black-black-oil-review>
has its final dates at theCitizens in Glasgow 
<http://www.citz.co.uk/whatson/info/the_cheviot_the_stag_and_the_black_black_oil/?_ga=1.55536872.290272861.1474475298>
. Josette Bushell Mingo examines her relationship with singer and activist Nina 
Simone in Nina atthe Unity theatre in Liverpool 
<http://www.unitytheatreliverpool.co.uk/whats-on/nina.html>. David Bowie is 
remembered in From Ibiza to the Norfolk Broads atWaterloo East theatre, London 
<http://www.waterlooeast.co.uk/from%20ibiza%20to%20the%20norfok%20broads.html>. 
Ben Duke’s terrific dance theatre pieceParadise Lost 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/may/27/paradise-lost-ben-duke-review-dance>
 (Lies Unopened Beside Me) is atthe Nuffield in Lancaster 
<https://www.lancasterarts.org/whats-on/lost-dog-paradise-lost>. Sharp Teeth’s 
modern gothic fairy tale The Fox and the Child begins atthe Wardrobe theatre in 
Bristol. <http://thewardrobetheatre.com/livetheatre/the-fox-the-child/>

Wednesday

Séance is a bite-sized spooky performance from Glen Neath and David Rosenberg 
which uses binaural technology to question what we believe. It’s programmed 
alongside the UK premiere of The Exorcist atBirmingham Rep 
<http://www.birmingham-rep.co.uk/event/the-exorcist/>. Birth, a global festival 
of theatre and debate featuring plays from seven international playwrights, 
begins at theRoyal Exchange in Manchester 
<https://www.royalexchange.co.uk/whats-on-and-tickets/brth>. Grain in the 
Blood, at theTron theatre in Glasgow 
<http://www.tron.co.uk/event/grain-in-the-blood/>, is a thriller by Rob 
Drummond, set at harvest time in a rural community, about what we are prepared 
to sacrifice for the greater good. Michael Longhurst’s revival of Peter 
Shaffer’s Amadeus opens at theNational Theatre, London 
<https://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/shows/amadeus>. The gap between what we 
think and what we say is brilliantly examined in Ursula Martinez’s Free 
Admission atWarwick Arts Centre 
<http://www.warwickartscentre.co.uk/whats-on/2016/ursula-martinez-free-admission/>
.

Thursday

Al Smith’s Pinteresque Harrogate, first seen at High Tide 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/sep/13/hightide-festival-suffolk-harrogate-lampedusa-brenda>
 in late 2015, opens at theRoyal Court in London 
<http://www.royalcourttheatre.com/whats-on/harrogate>. Living Spit rewrite 
Frankenstein and perform it with a live four-piece band atSalisbury Playhouse 
<http://www.salisburyplayhouse.com/whats-on/main-house/frankenstein/>, while in 
the same venue’sSalberg Studio 
<http://www.salisburyplayhouse.com/whats-on/the-salberg/kicking-and-screaming/>
, Tangled Feet consider parenthood in Kicking and Screaming. Tim Barlow is at 
theTraverse, Edinburgh 
<https://www.traverse.co.uk/whats-on/event-detail/951/him.aspx>, from tonight, 
reflecting on life at 80 in Him, created from conversations with Sheila Hill. 
Bonnie Greer rewrites The Cherry Orchard in The Hotel Cerise at theTheatre 
Royal Stratford East, London 
<http://www.stratfordeast.com/whats-on/all-shows/the-hotel-cerise/>. The 
experiences of refugees are explored in Phone Home atLondon’s Shoreditch Town 
Hall 
<http://shoreditchtownhall.com/theatre-performance/whats-on/event/phone-home-previews>
, a piece made by theatre companies in the UK, Greece and Germany.

Friday and the weekend

David Greig’s new version of the world’s second oldest extant play, Aeschylus’s
The Suppliant Women 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2016/oct/06/suppliant-women-review-royal-lyceum-edinburgh>
, is at theGrand Opera House in Belfast 
<https://www.goh.co.uk/the-suppliant-women> as part of the Belfast 
international festival, which also includes Stacey Gregg’s terrific Scorched. 
The Broadway musical Side Show, about conjoined twins Daisy and Violet Hilton, 
gets its UK premiere atSouthwark Playhouse in London 
<http://southwarkplayhouse.co.uk/show/side-show/>. On Saturday afternoon, Greg 
Sinclair’s As the Crow Flies, a piece that celebrates different connections, is 
at the excellentPlatform in Easterhouse, Glasgow 
<http://www.platform-online.co.uk/whats-on/event/234/>, which is celebrating 
its 10th birthday with a strong programme of work. Ruby Dolls’ feminist fairy 
tale rethinkThe Brides of Bluebeard 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2016/sep/25/the-brides-of-bluebeard-review-ruby-dolls-cabaret>
 is on for one night only on Saturday at theSt James Studio in London 
<https://www.stjamestheatre.co.uk/studio/the-brides-of-bluebeard/>.
