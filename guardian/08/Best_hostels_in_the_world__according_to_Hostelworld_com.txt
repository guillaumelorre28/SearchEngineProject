
Reminding us once again that a good hostel is so much more than a bunk and a 
blanket, booking site Hostelworld.com has revealed the best in backpacker 
accommodation around the world.

Based on the reviews of one million customers posted over the past 12 months, 
Hostelworld.com <http://www.hostelworld.com> has announced the winners of this 
year’s “Hoscar” awards across nine categories.

Wombats City Hostel <http://www.wombats-hostels.com/london/> in London took 
the prize for the best hostel in England. The modern hostel, located in 
Whitechapel, opened in 2014 after the European chain refurbished a building 
that originally opened in 1865 as a hostel for British Merchant Navy seaman.

Other leading UK hostels include the Skyewalker Hostel 
<http://www.skyewalkerhostel.com/>, Portnalong, ranked best in Scotland; The 
Riverhouse Backpackers <http://www.riverhousebackpackers.com/>, Cardiff, ranked 
best in Wales; andGlobal Village <http://globalvillagebelfast.com/>, Belfast, 
which took top spot in Northern Ireland.

The award for the best small hostel in the world went to Soul Kitchen 
<http://www.soulkitchenhostel.com/> in St Petersburg. Located in a neo-baroque 
building, the hostel has a contemporary design, with private en-suites, cosy 
bunks and a homely communal kitchen.

Home Lisbon <http://www.homelisbonhostel.com/> took the prize for the best 
medium-size hostel worldwide. The boutique accommodation, located in the centre 
of the Portuguese capital, has scooped the award four years running thanks to 
its snug living room and home-cooked dinners by the owner’s mother.
