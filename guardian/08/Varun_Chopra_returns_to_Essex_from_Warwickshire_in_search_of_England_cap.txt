
Essex have announced the return of the opening batsman Varun Chopra from 
Warwickshire <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/warwickshireccc> on a 
three-year deal.

The 29-year-old, born in Barking, started his career at Chelmsford, where he 
scored a County Championship century on debut in 2006, before opting for a 
switch to Edgbaston three years later.

Chopra was part of the Warwickshire side who won the 2012 County Championship 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/sep/06/warwickshire-worcestershire-county-championship-title>
 and theNatWest T20 Blast in 2014 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/aug/23/warwickshire-lancashire-t20-blast-final-match-report>
, the latter as captain on home soil at Edgbaston, but will return to his 
hometown club when his contract expires at the end of the season.

Chopra said: “I am delighted to be rejoining Essex 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/essex>, a club where I have fond memories 
from the early stages of my career. I look forward to returning as a more 
developed player and being part of a talented, ambitious and exciting team led 
by Chris Silverwood.

“As I enter a key stage in my career, I am intent on contributing in all 
formats of the game and in doing that, I hope to achieve my desire to play for 
England.”

Silverwood, the head coach, said: “His Division One experience will be 
invaluable to our batting lineup and younger players.”
