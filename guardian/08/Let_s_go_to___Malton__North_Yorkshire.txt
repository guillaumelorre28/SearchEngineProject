
Why go now?


The self-styled food capital of Yorkshire is gearing up for its Food Lovers 
festival <http://maltonyorkshire.co.uk/food-lovers-festival/> (23-24 May). This 
year there is a night market and a party with live music, street food and local 
beer. The town also has amonthly food market 
<http://maltonyorkshire.co.uk/monthly-food-market/>, an old-fashioned Saturday 
market and twice-weekly livestock markets. A newGame and Seafood Festival 
<http://maltonyorkshire.co.uk/malton-game-seafood-fest/> is planned for 12 
September. TheMade in Malton food tour 
<http://maltonyorkshire.co.uk/made-in-malton/> takes in the artisan producers 
in and around Talbot Yard, while the town’s traditional shops include two 
butchers, two greengrocers, delis and a fishmonger. The newMalton Cookery School
 <http://www.maltoncookeryschool.co.uk/pages/home> has short courses in 
everything from perfect pies to marvellous macaroons.

Malton sits between the Yorkshire Moors, Wolds and the coast. Scarborough is 
25 minutes away by train <http://www.tpexpress.co.uk/>, and steam locomotives 
<http://www.nymr.co.uk/> chug from neighbouring Pickering to Whitby. Eden Camp 
<http://www.edencamp.co.uk/>, on the outskirts of Malton, is a museum in a 
former prisoner-of-war camp.Castle Howard <http://www.castlehoward.co.uk/> (of 
Brideshead Revisited fame) andKirkham Priory 
<http://www.english-heritage.org.uk/visit/places/kirkham-priory/> are each six 
miles away.

Where shall we go for dinner?

 Modern British cuisine at the Talbot hotel 
Malton Relish <http://www.maltonrelish.co.uk/home.html> deli is open for lunch 
every day and holds supper clubs three times a month – the May menu includes a 
summer pudding with local sloe gin and Yorkshire clotted cream.The New Malton 
<http://www.thenewmalton.co.uk/menu.htm> serves gastropub dishes such, as sea 
trout with crab butter. Therestaurant at the Talbot Hotel 
<http://www.talbotmalton.co.uk/pages/food-and-drink/the-restaurant> has modern 
British food – such as roast halibut with lardo, fried oyster and sea cabbage.

How about a drink?


Leoni’s coffee shop (16 Wheelgate) is owned by an award-winning barista. The 
Brass Castle brewery <http://brasscastle.co.uk/> opens its taproom on the 
second Saturday of the month, with at least eight craft beers.

Anywhere to stay under £100 a night?

Burythorpe House (doubles from £75, 01653 658200, burythorpehouse.co.uk 
<http://www.burythorpehouse.co.uk/>) is a boutique hotel five miles from 
Malton. Nearby Habton House Farm (doubles from £90, 01653 669707,
habtonhouse.co.uk <http://www.habtonhouse.co.uk/>) has three elegant rooms. The 
Wentworth Arms (doubles from £60, 01653 692618,wentwortharmsmalton.co.uk 
<http://www.wentwortharmsmalton.co.uk/>) in Old Malton is a more basic coaching 
inn.


Do I need a car?

It might be handy, unless you splash out on one of the pricier hotels in town. 
However, there are good rail links: direct trains on theManchester to 
Scarborough line <http://www.tpexpress.co.uk/>, and connections in York from 
London. Visitmaltonyorkshire.co.uk <http://maltonyorkshire.co.uk/> for more 
information.
