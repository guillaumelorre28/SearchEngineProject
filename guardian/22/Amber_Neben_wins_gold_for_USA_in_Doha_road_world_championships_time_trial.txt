
Amber Neben of the United States won gold in the women’s time trial at the UCI 
Road World Championships in Doha on Tuesday. The 41-year-old, who claimed the 
same title in 2008, was crowned champion once more after winning the 28.9km 
test in Qatar with a time of 36min 37.04sec.

Holland’s Ellen van Dijk was six seconds back in second with Australia’s 
Katrin Garfoot third in 36min 45.36sec.

Annemiek van Vleuten, who cracked three bones in her neck  
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/aug/07/annemiek-van-vleuten-intensive-care-olympic-road-race>
two months ago in the Rio Olympic time trial, was on course for a fairytale 
bronze medal for much of the race but was eventually edged down into fifth.

Neben’s victory completed a brilliant year for US women’s time trialling, 
after herfellow veteran Kristin Armstrong’s gold in Rio 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/aug/10/kristin-armstrong-beats-wild-weather-olympic-cycling-time-trial-gold>
.

Great Britain’s Hannah Barnes was 14th in 39min 00.37sec and her compatriot 
Hayley Simmonds was 25th after coming home in 40min 24.22sec.

The men’s time trial, featuring Britain’s Steve Cummings and Alex Dowsett, 
takes place today.
