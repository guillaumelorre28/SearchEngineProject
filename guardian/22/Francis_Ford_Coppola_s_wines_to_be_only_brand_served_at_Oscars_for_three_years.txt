
Francis Ford Coppola has struck a deal to be the official wine sponsor to the 
Oscars.According to the Hollywood Reporter 
<http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/francis-ford-coppola-winery-sets-wine-partnerships-academy-sundance-institute-965224>
, the Francis Ford Coppola Winery has signed an agreement to supply the Academy 
Awards for the next three years, as well as the 2017 Sundance film festival.

The deal covers the Academy Award ceremony from 2017 to 2019, as well as 
allied events such as the Governors Ball, the officialOscars 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/oscars> after-party, for which Coppola and 
the winery’s director of winemaking, Corey Beck, have created custom blends and 
commemorative labels.

Coppola, an Oscar-winner for his screenplays for Patton, The Godfather, and 
The Godfather: Part II – for which he also won best director – as well as 
receiving the Irving G Thalberg Memorial award in 2010, began winemaking after 
buying a vineyard in California’s Napa Valley in 1975. TheFrancis Ford Coppola 
Winery <https://www.francisfordcoppolawinery.com/> is one of the film-maker’s 
ventures, a vineyard-cum-amusement park he describes as “a wine wonderland”, 
located in Geyserville, California.

The Sundance sponsorship involves the Winery hosting parties and a venture 
called The Big Picture which involves the commission of a custom, oversized 
wine cork mural which will grow in size during the festival.
