
France’s first floating hotel has opened in Paris, giving visitors a chance to 
enjoy a riviera lifestyle in the heart of the capital. Launched in June, the 
stylish and contemporaryOFF Seine <http://offparisseine.com/hotel.html> hotel 
is on a purpose-built barge docked on the Seine.

 One of the hotel’s 58 guestrooms 
The 58-room hotel, with doubles from £140 a night, was designed by the team at 
floating architecture specialistSeine Design <http://www.ronzatti.com/en>. 
Docked near the Gare d’Austerlitz, it is now the largest boat moored in the 
city.

Guests will have views along the river while lounging besides the hotel 
dipping pool. There is also a cocktail and tapas bar, which is open to 
non-residents.

The hotel is the latest addition to a growing number of offerings found along 
the Seine, as the city continues to develop its riverside as a destination. 
Launched in 2002, and expanded in 2007, theParis-Plages 
<http://en.parisinfo.com/discovering-paris/major-events/Paris-Plages> project 
has helped develop the banks of the Seine as an urban beach. Three years ago 
the redevelopedBerges de Seine 
<http://en.parisinfo.com/discovering-paris/sustainable-tourism/the-berges-de-seine-in-pictures>
, a riverside promenade, was opened, while mayorAnne Hidalgo has promised to 
make swimming in the river possible by 2024 
<http://www.lonelyplanet.com/news/2016/05/18/paris-seine-swim-2017/>.
