
Tommy Hilfiger, 60, was born in Elmira, New York State, one of nine children. 
He opened a small chain of boutiques called People's Place in 1969 when he was 
still at high school. Ten years later he moved to New York City, became a 
fashion designer and in 1985 showed his first signature men's collection. Over 
the years he expanded into womenswear, clothes for children, accessories, 
fragrances and home furnishings. There are more than 1,000 Tommy Hilfiger 
stores worldwide and a newflagship shop 
<http://www.vogue.co.uk/news/2011/08/22/tommy-hilfiger-opens-flagship-uk-store> 
in London has just opened on Brompton Road. Hilfiger is married for the second 
time and has seven children.

When were you happiest? 
At the birth of each of my children.

What is your greatest fear?
Running out of energy.

What is your earliest memory?
A big family Christmas in Elmira, New York.

Which living person do you most admire, and why?
Jeffrey Sachs <http://www.earth.columbia.edu/articles/view/1804>, director of 
the Earth Institute. The work he and the team are doing to reduce extreme 
poverty is phenomenal.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself? 
Impatience.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
Laziness.

Aside from a property, what's the most expensive thing you've bought?
I've been lucky enough to collect some wonderful pieces of work from Andy 
Warhol.

Where would you like to live?
I currently live in the Plaza in New York and I love it – all that history, 
all those interesting stories.

What would your super power be?
To understand every language in the world, that would be cool.

What is your most treasured possession?
Sweet Pungent, it's the first canvas created from a unique collaboration 
betweenAndy Warhol <http://www.warhol.org/> and Jean-Michel Basquiat 
<http://basquiat.com/>.

What makes you unhappy?
Ignorance.

If you could bring something extinct back to life, what would you choose?
If it's gone, you have to accept it.

Who would play you in the film of your life? 
It'd have to be Dustin Hoffman.

What is your favourite smell?
The beach in summertime.

Cat or dog?
I don't believe in favouritism.

What do you owe your parents?
Everything, they were hard-working and moral.

What or who is the greatest love of your life? 
My wife, Dee.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party?
Grace Kelly, Jackie and John Kennedy, Andy Warhol, Mick Jagger.

What has been your biggest disappointment? 
My first business, People's Place, went bankrupt in the mid-70s.

If you could go back in time, where would you go?
1969 – Woodstock.

What keeps you awake at night?
Occasionally, my two-year-old son, Sebastian.

How would you like to be remembered?
Kindly.

What is the most important lesson life has taught you?
Doing it all yourself is impossible; surround yourself with great people.

Tell us a joke
How many hipsters does it take to change a light bulb? What, you don't know?
