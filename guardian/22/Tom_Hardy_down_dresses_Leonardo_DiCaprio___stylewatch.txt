
In the age of the hoodie-clad Silicon Valley billionaire, it is not the man in 
the suit but the man who dares to wear a crumpled T-shirt next to him who is 
making the power move with his wardrobe.

Tom Hardy illustrated this point beautifully on Sunday night with creased 
<https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2015/dec/02/creasing-up-is-ironing-a-thing-of-the-past>
 jeans, grey trainers and a top fromRug Man Art <http://www.rugmanart.com/>. 
The T-shirt was illustrated with the face of a man who looked spookily like 
Hardy himself, genetically spliced withBilly Gibbons 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/nov/17/billy-gibbons-zz-top-solo-album-perfectamundo-interview>
. TheMark Zuckerberg prize for dressing-down 
<https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/2015/sep/08/mark-zuckerberg-takes-a-tip-from-the-steve-jobs-school-of-getting-dressed-stylewatch>
, however, was clinched with a sweat band worn high on his left arm; its 
locker-room connotations bravely at odds with the red carpet.

Leonardo DiCaprio’s very MOR suit and shirt – top button undone like Nick 
Clegg during an “at home with” – didn’t stand a chance.
