
Sir Bradley Wiggins <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/bradleywiggins> has no 
plans to race in the Abu Dhabi Tour, which begins on 20 October, and will focus 
instead on the Six Day track races in London and Ghent.

Although Wiggins has been used in promotional material for the Abu Dhabi 
event, where his eponymous team will compete, and was named in the provisional 
start list on Wednesday, it is understood the four-day race was never in his 
confirmed schedule.

Wiggins plans to compete in the London Six Day, from 25 October to 30 October, 
and the Ghent Six Day, from 15 to 20 November, with Mark Cavendish.The pair are 
Madison world champions 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/mar/06/sir-bradley-wiggins-mark-cavendish-world-madison-championships>
.

The five-times Olympic champion, who last raced on the road at the Tour of 
Britain in September, has announced his intention to retire at the end of 2016 
but it is possible he could continue racing next year.
