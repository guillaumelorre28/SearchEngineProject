
Marni <https://www.theguardian.com/fashion/marni> has announced that its 
founder and creative director, Consuelo Castiglioni, will be leaving the 
company after 22 years at the helm to focus on her “private life”.

Castiglioni founded the company in 1994, in collaboration with her husband 
Gianni, naming the line after her sister. Marni’s new creative director has 
been announced as Francesco Risso, who comes to the company from Prada.

In a statement, 56-year-old Castiglioni said: “The time has now come to 
dedicate myself to my private life.” Speaking about her time as creative 
director, she added: “These were hectic and exciting years, which absorbed all 
of my energies to create a project I am proud of.”

Marni is owned by Italian fashion group Only The Brave <https://www.otb.net>, 
and its president, Renzo Rosso, said: “The world pays tribute to the original 
vision of Consuelo,” and added that, under Risso’s guidance, Marni would “reach 
new goals” and that the creative director would write “a new chapter in the 
history of the house”.
