
Monday

The show of the week is Us/Them 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2017/jan/11/us-them-terrorism-national-theatre>
 at theNational Theatre <https://www.nationaltheatre.org.uk/shows/us-them> – a 
playful, moving and multilayered examination of the Beslan school massacre of 
2004. Katherine Soper’s devastatingWish List 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2016/sep/28/wish-list-review-lives-boxed-in-by-relentless-work-and-benefit-cuts>
 continues at theRoyal Court Upstairs 
<https://royalcourttheatre.com/whats-on/wish-list/>. The London international 
mime festival <http://mimelondon.com/> continues, with highlights including 
Joli Vyann’s elegant Imbalance at the Lilian Baylis Studio and two shows from 
Stephen Mottram’s Animata atJacksons Lane, north London 
<https://www.jacksonslane.org.uk/whats-on/the-parachute-watch-the-ball>. At the 
Chapel of the Holy Cross inDurham Cathedral 
<https://www.eventbrite.co.uk/e/samuel-becketts-company-tickets-30077205732>, 
there’s a fascinating-sounding performance of Samuel Beckett’s Company which 
invites audience members (limited to nine at a time) to lie on a bed in the 
darkened chapel and listen to a voice. ThePush festival continues all week at 
Home in Manchester <http://homemcr.org/event/push-festival-2017/> showcasing 
new work and emerging companies from the region. Tonight, Powder Keg’s 
performance gigMorale is High 
<http://homemcr.org/production/morale-is-high-since-we-gave-up-hope/> (Since we 
Gave up Hope), sounds great as does Mill Theatre Company’s piece about 
loneliness,I’m Standing Next to You 
<http://homemcr.org/production/im-standing-next-to-you/>. 
 Stephen Mottram talks about his show The Parachute, part of London 
international mime festival <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=f-i8ReV5EU8> 
Tuesday

Sacekripa’s Marée Basse is a tale of acrobatic one-upmanship at the Barbican’s 
Pit <https://www.barbican.org.uk/theatre/event-detail.asp?ID=20438>. It’s your 
last chance this week for Nofit State’s Bianco which takes place in a big top 
next to theRoyal Festival Hall 
<https://www.southbankcentre.co.uk/whats-on/117873-bianco-201617>, and also for 
Sally Cookson’s delicious Cinderella: a Fairytale at theTobacco Factory in 
Bristol <https://www.tobaccofactorytheatres.com/shows/cinderella-a-fairytale/>. 
The Doppel Gang, set during the Blitz, and inspired by the comedy of the Marx 
Brothers, sounds as if it could be fun at theTristan Bates theatre in Covent 
Garden <https://www.tristanbatestheatre.co.uk/whats-on/the-doppel-gang>.

Thursday

This is a good idea from Theatre503 in Battersea 
<https://theatre503.com/whats-on/top-trumps/>’s new artistic director Lisa 
Spirling: Top Trumps is a series of short plays from Caryl Churchill, Neil 
LaBute, Roy Williams and others responding toDonald Trump 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/donaldtrump>’s election, in his 
inauguration week. Also includes post-show discussions.

Friday and the weekend

Nick Ahad’s The Chef Show, a piece that mixes performance and cookery, 
considers Friday night down the local curry house; it’s atKirkgate Centre in 
Cockermouth <http://www.thekirkgate.com/2017/01/the-chef-show/> as part of a 
wider tour. Project O’s O at theRoyal Exchange Studio 
<https://www.royalexchange.co.uk/whats-on-and-tickets/o> in Manchester offers a 
dance that winds itself through identity politics and female experience. 
Clowning is celebrated in Nothing to Say, Spanish clown Leandre’s show set in a 
house with no walls. It’s atJacksons Lane 
<https://www.jacksonslane.org.uk/whats-on/nothing-to-say> from tonight and on 
Saturday Jackson’s Lane also hosts anall-day symposium exploring Clowns and 
Power <https://www.jacksonslane.org.uk/whats-on/symposium-clowns-and-power>. On 
Saturday, the NT’s staging ofThe Curious Incident of the Dog in the Night-Time 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2013/mar/13/curious-incident-dog-night-time-review>
 sets off on tour with two weeks atthe Lowry in Salford 
<http://www.thelowry.com/event/the-curious-incident-of-the-dog-in-the-night-time>
.
