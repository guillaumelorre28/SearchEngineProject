Fees £9,000 for home/EU undergraduates at the Coventry University and Coventry 
University <https://www.theguardian.com/education/coventryuniversity> London 
Campus. Part-time fees are up to £6,750, the maximum fee charged pro-rata to 
the equivalent full-time fee. International fees range from £11,359-£13,476.

Coventry University College (CUC) and Coventry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk/coventry> Uni Scarborough Campus (CUSC) fee is 
£5,835 for classroom-based courses and £6,896 for laboratory-based courses.

Bursaries and scholarships A range of scholarships are on offer, including: 
the Sports Scholarship (awarded at two levels of either £1,500 or £3,000 in a 
mixture of cash and services per academic year), the Phoenix Scholarship 
(£1,000 per year for students who achieve 260 Ucas points or above in specified 
subjects and who are from areas where few people attend university) and the 
academic excellence scholarship (awarded at two levels of either £1,250 or 
£2,000 for students who achieve outstanding A-levels or equivalent 
qualifications).

Figures quoted are for the year 2016-17. Visit coventry.ac.uk/scholarships 
<http://www.coventry.ac.uk/study-at-coventry/student-support/finance/undergraduate-finance/scholarships/scholarship-information-2016-17>
 for more information.

Accommodation From £110-149 for fresher accommodation in 2016-17, with 
contracts ranging from 38 to 44 week. Around 80% of freshers are guaranteed 
accommodation.
