Fees £9,000 for full-time undergraduates in 2016-17. Part-time fees are £1,125 
per 15 credit module (part-time). The maximum part-time fee charged in a given 
year will not exceed the government permitted rate of £6,750 (maximum 90 
credits)

Tuition fees for international students (non-EU) are 11,300 per year for 
full-time courses or £1,410 per 15 credit module. Fees for placement years are 
£700 for placement year (where applicable and subject to visa conditions).

Bursaries Eligible students on full-time courses with a household income under 
£25,000 will be awarded a cash bursary of £1,000 per year.Students 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/students> with a household income 
between £25,000 and £42,611 will be awarded a cash bursary of £600 per year. 
This will be paid in May of each academic year, a time when students need that 
extra bit of financial support.

The university also offers a variety of sports and academic excellence 
scholarships. For full details visit theuniversity website 
<http://www.winchester.ac.uk/startinghere/Students%20and%20money/undergraduate/Universitysupport/Pages/Bursaries.aspx>
.

Accommodation The University offers a range of awards and bursaries to help 
students pay for their studies. For 2017 entry, full-time UK and EU 
undergraduate students with a household income under £25,000 (as assessed by 
Student Finance) are awarded a cash bursary of £1,000 per year.

Full-time UK and EU undergraduate students with a household income between 
£25,000 and £42,620 (as assessed by Student Finance) are awarded a cash bursary 
of £600 per year. Bursaries are paid in May of each academic year, a time when 
students need that extra bit of financial support.

 The university also offers a variety of subject, sport and academic 
excellence scholarships. For full details visitwinchester.ac.uk/studentsandmoney
 <http://www.winchester.ac.uk/studentsandmoney>
