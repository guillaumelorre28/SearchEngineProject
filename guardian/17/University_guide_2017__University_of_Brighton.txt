Fees £9,000 per year for UK/EU full-time students in 2016-17. Most part-time 
courses are calculated on a pro-rata basis.

For international students, classroom-based courses are from £12,420 per year, 
while laboratory/studio-based courses are from £13,800 per year.

Bursaries The university offers a number of funding packages through bursaries 
and scholarships which eligible students can apply for. This includes: the
University of Brighton 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/universityofbrighton> bursary, a 
means-tested award worth up to £2,000 cash. A professional bursary pays £1,000 
to those studying architecture, pharmacy and undergraduate teacher training 
courses in their final year. A care leaver’s bursary pays £1,000 per year for 
students under 25 who have been in local authority care for at least 13 weeks.

For full details visit brighton.ac.uk/fees-and-finance 
<https://www.brighton.ac.uk/studying-here/fees-and-finance/index.aspx>. Figures 
quoted are for the year 2016-17.

Accommodation 70% of eligible students were offered places in university 
accommodation in 2015. The average rents for 2016 are between £108–£192 per 
week for a room in halls of residence, £160 per week for one-bedroom flat or 
£90–95 per person per week for a room in a shared house.
