
Sherlock <https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/sherlock> was the 
most-watched TV programme over the festive season for the second year running.

The BBC1 detective drama, starring Benedict Cumberbatch 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/benedict-cumberbatch> as the titular 
detective and Martin Freeman as Dr Watson, returned for a fourth series on New 
Year’s Day.

Consolidated viewing figures show that the 90-minute episode drew 11.3 million 
viewers. Entitled The Six Thatchers – an update of the original Sherlock Holmes 
story The Adventure of the Six Napoleons – it had a audience share of 37.8%.

Last year, Sherlock: The Abominable Bride topped the festive ratings, with 
11.6 million viewers. The conclusion of the new series, Sherlock: The Final 
Problem, will air on Sunday at 9pm on BBC1.
