
It’s a tradition, of sorts: this August, thousands of students will collect 
their exam results, cry over them for various reasons, and descend upon remote 
pastureland for a party in the mud.

For many of these revellers, it will be first their taste of a music festival, 
where all manner of pitfalls await the unprepared.

To lend a helping hand to the newbies, we’re asking Guardian readers to share 
their own festival wisdom.


Your tips and advice can cover any aspect of festival preparation. What items 
should no festival-goer go without, and why? How best can you avoid having all 
your stuff nicked? What’s the best way to stay dry? Perhaps you’ve had a 
particularly rubbish experience that others could learn from? Tell us all about 
it.
