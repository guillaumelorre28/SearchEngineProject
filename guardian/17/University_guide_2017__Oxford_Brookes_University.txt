Fees £9,000 for full-time home/EU undergraduates or £750 per unit for 
part-time students (no more than five units are studied per academic year). 
International fees range from £12,640-13,460. Figures quoted are for the 
academic year 2016-17.

Bursaries The Oxford Brookes bursary scheme is eligible for all UK students on 
£9,000 courses with a household income below £25,000 who are in receipt of a 
higher education maintenance grant. The bursary amount provided is on a sliding 
scale (dependent on household income and the year in which you join your 
course) from £1,000-£2,000 for year one, and from £1,750 to £3,000 for years 
two and three.

Figures quoted are for the year 2016-17. For more information and updates, 
visitbrookes.ac.uk/financial-support-uk-eu-2016-17 
<http://www.brookes.ac.uk/studying-at-brookes/finance/undergraduate-finance---uk-and-eu-students/financial-support-uk-eu-2016-17/>

Accommodation Most halls have a 38-week contract, with some having up to 
49-week contracts. 2016-17 prices range from £108 per week for a self-catered, 
single-study bedroom with a shared kitchen and bathroom, to £154 per week for 
ensuite accommodation. Studio flats are approximately £195 per week.
