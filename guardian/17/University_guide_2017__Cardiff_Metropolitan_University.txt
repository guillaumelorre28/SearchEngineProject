Fees £9,000 for full-time home and EU students; £300 per 10 credits 
<http://www.cardiffmet.ac.uk/study/finance/Pages/Postgraduate,-Research-and-Part-Time-Students-.aspx>
 for part-time home and EU students. £11,500 for full-time international 
students. Figures quoted refer to the academic year 2017-18. Visit
cardiffmet.ac.uk/finance 
<http://www.cardiffmet.ac.uk/study/finance/Pages/default.aspx> for more 
information.

 Welsh and EU students are entitled to a tuition fee grant of up to £5,100 
which means that eligible Welsh and EU students will actually borrow £3,900 
(not £9,000) per year.

Bursaries and scholarships Bursaries currently on offer include: the 
undergraduate academic achievement award, £2,000 for the highest achieving 
students within each department during first year. The award is paid as a 
contribution towards accommodation and day-to-day costs such as food or printing

 The undergraduate sport scholarship offers awards for students’ sporting 
prowess; the study first bursary for students who live in some local 
neighbourhoods is worth £1,000 per year to help with living costs. Scholarship 
for the academic year 2017-18 are yet to be confirmed, visit
www.cardiffmet.ac.uk/scholarships <http://www.cardiffmet.ac.uk/scholarships> 
for updates.

Accommodation Prices range from £102 for a self-catered, en-suite on the Plas 
Gwyn Campus to £151 for a catered en-suite (two meals a day provided) on 
Cyncoed Campus. Both are offered on a 40 week contract. Figures quoted refer to 
2016-17 prices, for updates visit cardiffmet.ac.uk/accommodation 
<http://www.cardiffmet.ac.uk/accommodation/Pages/default.aspx>
