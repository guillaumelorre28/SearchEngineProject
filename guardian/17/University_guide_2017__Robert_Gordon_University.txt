Fees If you live in Scotland, you are not liable to pay tuition fees for study 
in Scotland. However, you need to apply online to the Student Awards Agency for
Scotland <https://www.theguardian.com/uk/scotland> to have these fees paid.

For students from the rest of the UK (RUK) including England, Wales and 
Northern Ireland,Robert Gordon University 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/robertgordonuniversity> has three bands 
of undergraduate fees in 2016-17, subject to Scottish government legislation 
approval and annual review.

Band 1: All business, management and social science – £5,000 a year.

Band 2: All art and design, architecture and built environment, computing, 
engineering, health and science – £6,750 a year.

Band 3: Masters of Pharmacy – £8,500 a year.

If you are from elsewhere in the EU you are entitled to be charged the same 
level of tuition fees as Scottish students.

International (non-EU) student’s fees range from £2,740 to £20,900 a year 
depending on the course and mode of study.

Bursaries Young students’ bursaries are available for Scottish students under 
25 from low-income families. Supplementary grants are also available for 
eligible students. A range of scholarships is available from some of the 
biggest and most successful companies in the UK.

Company scholarships are available in architecture, construction & surveying, 
business, management & accounting, engineering, computing and the School of 
Pharmacy and Life Sciences. A number of sports scholarships are also offered to 
promising sports men and women.

Visit rgu.ac.uk/scholarships/ 
<http://www.rgu.ac.uk/future-students/finance-and-scholarships/scholarships/scholarships/>
 for more information.

Accommodation RGU accommodation is located in and around the city centre and 
Garthdee campus. For sessions 2016-17, the weekly rent at is between £100 and 
£148 for a 40 week lease starting in September. Contracts for halls last for 1 
year and the rent includes utilities, cooking facilities, personal contents 
insurance, cleaning and internet access.
