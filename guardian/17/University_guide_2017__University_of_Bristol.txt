Fees £9,000 for UK and EU students in 2016-17.

Between £14,600 and £35,000 for international students, depending on their 
degree course.

Between £9,000 and £19,000 for Channel Island and Isle of Man students, 
depending on their degree course.

Bursaries and scholarships The University of Bristol 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/universityofbristol> bursary is an award 
of up to £2,000 for UK students who register with the university in 2016 and 
whose household income is £42,620 or under.

For details of this scheme and links to other sources of financial support 
visitbristol.ac.uk/uob-bursary/2016-entrants 
<http://www.bristol.ac.uk/fees-funding/awards/uob-bursary/2016-entrants/>.

Accommodation Accommodation is guaranteed for new full-time undergraduate 
students during their first year of study in Bristol, provided that they return 
the accommodation form by the given deadline, they firmly accept their offer of 
study by the given deadline, and are unaccompanied.

There are various types of accommodation available in halls of residence and 
student houses. Weekly rents range from £81.97 per week (shared room, 
self-catered) to £186.34 per week (en-suite room, catered).
