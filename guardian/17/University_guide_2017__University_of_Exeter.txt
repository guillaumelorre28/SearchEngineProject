Fees £9,000 for home/EU full-time students in 2016-17.

Fees for international students starting courses at the same time vary from 
£15,950 (arts, humanities, social sciences, business, maths, geography) up to 
£18,550 for science subjects and £28,500 for medicine.


Part-time fees, where applicable, are pro rata.


Bursaries The access to Exeter bursary is a guaranteed, non-competitive 
bursary available to all home and EU students with a household income below 
£25,000, which does not have to be paid back. Under the scheme the university 
will provide a range of bursaries depending on household income.

For 2016 entry, the bursary is £1,000 for each year of study for those with a 
household income of between £16,001 to £25,000, and £2,000 in year one and 
£1,500 in subsequent years for those with a household income of less than 
£16,000.


The university also provides financial support for care leavers under the age 
of 25, including a full fee waiver for all years of study, as well as various 
sport scholarships for exceptional athletes, a music learning programme, and a 
number of other scholarships.


Visit exeter.ac.uk/scholarships/ 
<http://www.exeter.ac.uk/undergraduate/money/scholarships/> for more 
information and updates. Figures quoted are for 2016-17.


Accommodation Guaranteed for first-year students who apply before 31 July. 40% 
of accommodation is owned by the university. Costs in catered accommodation 
range from £4,569.33 per year / £142.80 per week (budget standard) to £7,415.19 
per year / £231.70 per week (single en-suite with view) for the academic year 
for 2016-17.

In self-catered accommodation, costs range from £4,007.40 per year / £95.42 
per week (standard, off-campus) to £7,325.02 per year / £166.48 per week 
(studio) for the academic year for 2015.


Accommodation suitable for couples and families is also available.
