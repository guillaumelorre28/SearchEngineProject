Fees Fees for UK/EEA students are £6,000 a year for the LLB (hons) three-year 
law degree and law with business; £9,000 a year for the LLB (hons) two-year 
accelerated course; £4,500 a year for the LLB (hons) four year part-time and 
i-LLB.

Fees for international students are £9,500 for the LLB (hons) three-year law 
degree and law with business; £11,500 a year for the LLB (hons) two-year 
accelerated course; £4,500 a year for the LLB (hons) four year part-time and 
i-LLB.

Scholarships The university offers a range of scholarships and bursaries. You 
need to have received an offer for an undergraduate courses to apply and be 
from a household where the average income for the last 12 months has been 
£25,000 or less.

As a guide for 2016-17 academic year, a law first scholarships is worth £2,000 
for students obtaining AAA at A-level (or equivalent). A lawyer 2B creative 
thinking scholarship, in partnership with Lawyer 2B, is worth £5,000.

The Lord Blunkett widening access award is worth between £1,000 - £3,000 to 
those whose circumstances may otherwise prevent them from studying law.

Accommodation Guaranteed if you have accepted a firm choice and applied for 
accommodation by 30 June 2017. Accommodation is usually in flats shared with 
three or five other undergraduate students. Typically you’ll have a shared 
kitchen/dining room and your own fully-furnished bedroom with study area and 
en-suite bathroom.

As a guide, in 2016, prices start £260 per week in London, and £105 for 
centres outside London.
