
The government has started to sell off its shares in Lloyds Banking Group 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/sep/17/lloyds-share-sale-makes-60m-pounds-for-government>
, following a taxpayer bailout that brought it back from the brink at the 
height of the financial crisis.

2007

11 December

Lloyds TSB writes off £200m 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2007/dec/11/lloydstsbgroup.banking> as a 
result of the US sub-prime mortgage crisis.

2008

30 July

First-half profits plunge 70% 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/jul/30/lloydstsbgroup.creditcrunch> 
at Lloyds TSB as it takes a further £585m hit on the back of the credit crisis. 
The new write-downs take the total credit crunch impact on Lloyds to £865m.

17 September

Lloyds TSB steps in to rescue HBOS  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/sep/18/marketturmoil.hbosbusiness>
after its share price halves in the first hour of trading as markets are 
gripped by crisis.

11-12 October

Government ministers and top bankers spend the weekend thrashing out plans to 
bail out RBS, Lloyds and HBOS 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/oct/12/banking-economy>.

13 October

The bailout is announced and the banking system brought back from the brink 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/oct/13/marketturmoil-creditcrunch>. 
Darling says the government does not want to be "in the business of running 
banks" – "we are in the business of stabilising banks, that is our purpose". 
The government takes a 43.4% stake in Lloyds.

2009

13 February

Lloyds Banking Group stuns the City 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/feb/13/lloyds-hbos-losses> by 
revealing £11bn of losses at HBOS.

17 May

Lloyds chairman Sir Victor Blank is forced to step down 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/may/17/sir-victor-blank-lloyds> 
after losing the confidence of major shareholders.

3 November

The government invests another £5.7bn in Lloyds 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/nov/03/banking-bailout-in-numbers> 
to maintain the taxpayer's stake at 43% after a rights issue.

2010

27 April

Lloyds returns to profit  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/apr/27/lloyds-banking-group-returns-to-profit>
for the first time since the banking crisis and earlier than expected.

20 September

The bank announces chief executive Eric Daniels will stand down 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/sep/20/lloyds-chief-executive-eric-daniels-retire>
 in 2011, surprising the City. He is eventually succeeded bySantander boss 
António Horta-Osório 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/nov/03/santander-boss-tipped-new-lloyds-chief-executive>
.

2012

15 March

RBS chief executive Stephen Hester says the government should start selling 
its stakes 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/blog/2012/mar/15/stephen-hester-government-share-sales>
 in RBS and Lloyds.

2013

22 May

George Osborne prepares to set out his plans for selling the government's 
stakes in Lloyds and RBS 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/may/22/lloyds-banking-group-investors-fresh-capital>
after the IMF called on to devise a "clear strategy" for the two banks.

19 June

The chancellor uses his annual Mansion House speech 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/jun/19/osborne-lloyds-rbs-sell-off> 
to signal he is ready to start selling the taxpayer's stake in Lloyds.

16 September

The government announces the start of its Lloyds share selloff  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/sep/16/government-sale-lloyds-bank-stake>
 just after the stock market closes.

17 September

George Osborne claims the economy has "turned a corner" as the government 
makes a £60m profit 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/sep/17/lloyds-share-sale-makes-60m-pounds-for-government>
from selling off the first tranche of its stake in Lloyds Banking Group – 
exactly five years to the day since Lloyds TSB rescued HBOS to create the 
enlarged bank.
