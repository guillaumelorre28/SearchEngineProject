Fees 

 £1,820 for Scottish students. If you are a first degree student and 
ordinarily resident in Scotland, you are eligible for your tuition fees to be 
covered by the Scottish government.

 For other UK students, there is an annual fee of £9,000 in 2016-17. If you 
are from elsewhere in the EU you are entitled to be charged the same level of 
tuition fees as Scottish students.


For international students, fees cost up to £29,600 per year.

Bursaries/Scholarships A range of bursaries on offer. The University of 
Edinburgh <https://www.theguardian.com/education/universityofedinburgh> Bursary 
provides support to students whose household income is less than £16,000. 
Undergraduate students can receive between £500 and £7,000 a year.

The Lloyds Scholars Programme also provides 15 scholarships a year to UK 
undergraduates from below average income families to study at the University of 
Edinburgh. Each scholarship could be worth a total of £19,000 to the most 
successful scholars.

Accommodation All new undergraduates, assuming they apply by a specified date 
and are from outside of the City of Edinburgh, are guaranteed an offer of 
university accommodation for their first year of study.

Costs (in 2016/17) for single study bedrooms in catered accommodation are 
£168–£227 per week, with self-catered £90–£160 per week; shared room options 
are available at lower prices, catered from £127 per week and self-catered from 
£62 per week. Rents include internet provision, utilities, contents insurance 
and secure bicycle storage.
