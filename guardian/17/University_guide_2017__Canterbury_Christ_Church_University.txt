Fees £9,000 per year for most courses for full-time home and EU 
undergraduates; part-time home and EU fees are pro-rata, with students studying 
50% of a full-time course paying £4,500 per year.

Fees for international students are £11,000 for most courses. Figures quoted 
are for the academic year 2016-17, checkcanterbury.ac.uk/funding-your-degree 
<https://www.canterbury.ac.uk/study-here/funding-your-degree/funding-your-degree.aspx>
 for updates.

Bursaries and scholarships Home and EU students from low-income families may 
be entitled to a university bursary. Other financial support packages include a 
care leavers bursary (£1,200 per academic year); sports scholarships (of up to 
£1,000 per annum); music scholarships of up to £300 for students in the school 
of music and performing arts; and choir director and organ scholarships. Grants 
are also available for international students.

Visit canterbury.ac.uk/funding-your-degree 
<https://www.canterbury.ac.uk/study-here/funding-your-degree/2015-16-entry-financial-support.aspx>
 for more details.

Accommodation Guaranteed for all first year students who accept the university 
as their firm choice and submit an application form by 31 July. Prices range 
from £92 per week for a bedroom with a shared kitchen and bathroom, to £195 per 
week for a self-contained one bedroom flat with its own kitchen and lounge. 
Ensuite accommodation, with shared kitchen and dining areas, ranges from £125 
to £160 per week. Prices quoted refer to 2016-17 rates, for more updates visit
canterbury.ac.uk/accommodation 
<https://www.canterbury.ac.uk/study-here/accommodation/accommodation.aspx>.
