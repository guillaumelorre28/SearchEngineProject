Fees £9,000 for home/EU full-time students in 2016-17, or £75 per credit point 
for part-time students.

For international students, £12,800 for full-time classroom-based subjects, or 
£14,500 for international students of full-time laboratory-based subjects


Scholarships The university offers a range of awards, including excellence 
scholarships, worth £1,000, to nine first year undergraduates from across the 
university.Students <https://www.theguardian.com/education/students> who 
attained the greatest total entry qualifications of all new undergraduates 
within their college receive the scholarship.


From the nine excellence scholarship winners, one recipient is selected to 
also receive the vice-chancellor’s scholarship, worth £9,000 over three years 
of study, which rewards a first year student who demonstrates outstanding 
academic talent, proven commitment to their subject discipline and a personal 
impact on their community.


The University of Lincoln 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/universityoflincoln> scholarship is a 
means-tested award for undergraduate students from households with an income of 
up to £40,000.


Accommodation Guaranteed for new undergraduate students starting in September 
2016 who make an application and have confirmed the University of Lincoln as 
their first choice by the end of June 2016.

Prices for on-campus accommodation range from £4,285.44 for a standard room to 
£4,991.31 an en-suite (both 40 weeks of accommodation paid in 3 instalments of 
£1,663.77). An advance payment of £250 (deductible from your first rent 
payment) is also required.
