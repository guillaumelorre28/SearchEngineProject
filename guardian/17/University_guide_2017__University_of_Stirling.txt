Fees Scottish domiciled, full-time, first-degree students may be eligible to 
have their tuition fees covered by the Student Awards Agency for Scotland 
(SAAS). First-degree students from non-UK EU countries may also be eligible to 
have their tuition fees covered by SAAS. Full-time Scottish-domiciled students 
and non-UK EU students should apply to the SAAS to cover this cost.

Scottish and non-UK EU part-time students are charged £303 per module, and may 
be eligible for SAAS support. The annual tuition fee for students from the rest 
of the UK from September 2017 is £6,750 a year (£1,125 per module).

The annual tuition fee for international students from September 2017 is 
£11,845 for class-based subjects (£1,975 per module), and £14,105 for lab-based 
subjects (£2,351 per module).

Bursaries/Scholarships Young students’ bursaries for Scottish students under 
25 from lower-income groups (level is dependent on family income), as well as 
discretionary childcare and hardship funds. Other grants are also available, 
including more than 70 sports scholarships.

For updates, and a comprehensive list of scholarships and bursaries visit 
stir.ac.uk/scholarships <http://www.stir.ac.uk/scholarships/>.

Accommodation All new first-year undergraduate students are prioritised for a 
place in university accommodation, provided they apply by the advertised 
deadline date. This priority extends to one offer of accommodation per student. 
Weekly prices range from £90 for a room with shared bathroom facilities to £159 
for a studio flat (prices are for 2016-17). All rooms are offered on a 
self-catered basis.
