Fees Welsh and EU students are entitled to a tuition fee grant of up to £5,100 
which means that eligible Welsh and EU students will actually borrow £3,900 
(not £9,000) per year in 2016-17.

Fees for 2016-17 are £9,000 for full-time students, or £8,000 for some 
foundation year courses. Most part-time courses carry the standard fee of £580 
per 20 credits. A typical part-time course consists of three 20 credit modules 
totaling 60 credits per year.

International fees are £10,400.

Bursaries A range of bursaries on offer. Excellence scholarships (£2,000) for 
new students starting full-time eligible undergraduate honours degree course, 
who accept an unconditional offer and achieve high grades. A progression 
bursary (£500) forUniversity of South Wales 
<https://www.theguardian.com/education/university-of-south-wales> students who 
have studied at partner colleges is also available.


Academic achievement scholarship (£2,000) for outstanding students from the EU 
who achieve the EU equivalent qualifications of at least ABB at A level.

Figures quoted are for the year 2016-17, visit www.southwales.ac.uk/money 
<http://www.southwales.ac.uk/money> for updates and more information.

Accommodation University-owned accommodation ranges from £105 per week for on 
suite, self-catered room to £159 for a self-catered, ensuite studio flat with 
its own kitchen facilities. Private hall of residence costs range from £79 - 
£131 per week.
