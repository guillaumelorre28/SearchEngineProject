Fees  £9,000 per year for home/EU full-time students in 2016-17. For part-time 
students, £1,500 per 20 credits.

For international students, £11,500 with a scholarship of £1,000 resulting in 
£10,500 for degree or foundation degree.

Bursaries/Scholarships The University has a range of bursary and scholarship 
awards on offer. The Chester bursary, a means-tested fund for students who have 
a household income of £25,000 or less, is worth £2,000 in cash (or part cash 
and part in kind (pcpk). A care leavers bursary, worth £7,000 in benefits (or 
pcpk) over the three years is also available, as is a Cheshire young carers 
bursary, which is comprised of a package totalling £4,500 over three years of 
study. Figures quoted are for the year 2016-17. Visit
chester.ac.uk/campus-life/finance <http://www.chester.ac.uk/campus-life/finance>
 for the latest information and criteria.

Accommodation Currently around 65% of first-year students are housed in halls 
of residence, but availability of student accommodation will increase in 
2016-17 with new developments near the city centre. Weekly prices for 2016-17 
will range from £81.20 (self-catered accommodation in a shared house) to 
£165.00 (gold standard studio apartment), depending on facilities and meal 
packages (highest price reflects the Tramways rates).
