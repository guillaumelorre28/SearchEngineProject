Fees £9,000 per year for new full-time home/EU undergraduates in 2016-17. 
There are a number of degrees for which tuition fees are different for part of 
the course, and these are outlined on the university website. 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/finance/tuition-fees/different-fees/> 
Tuition fee costs for international students (from outside the EU) vary and are 
available in individualdegree pages 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/degrees/>.

Bursaries and Scholarships Around 30% of new undergraduate students starting 
their degrees in 2016 will receive a bursary or scholarship. The main award 
schemes are:promise scholarships 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/finance/scholarships/promise/>, 20 
scholarships worth up to £9,000 per year for high ability home/EU students from 
low-income backgrounds,opportunity scholarships 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/finance/scholarships/opportunity/>, 
means-tested cash bursaries worth up to £2,000 per year, andaccess scholarships 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/undergraduate/finance/scholarships/access/> – cash 
bursaries of £500 per year for students from certain postcodes or students who 
have taken part in the university’sPartners programme supported entry route 
<http://www.ncl.ac.uk/schools/partners/> or realising opportunities programme 
<http://www.realisingopportunities.ac.uk/>.

Accommodation Prices range from £83.30 for a self-catered, single, washbasin 
room, situated off campus to £161.42 for a catered, single, en suite room 
situated on campus. 62% of the student accommodation at Newcastle is University 
owned.
