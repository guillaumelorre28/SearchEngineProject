Fees £9,000 for full-time home and EU undergraduates in 2016-17. For 
international students, £12,600. Part-time fees vary according to course.

Bursaries/Scholarships £1,000 a year for students for students who are paying 
the maximum tuition fee and who have a household income of £30,000 per year or 
less. Bursaries are also available to students who are under the age of 25 and 
who have been in local authority care. Figures quoted are for the year 2016-17, 
for updates and a full list of the bursaries on offer, visit
ntu.ac.uk/scholarships 
<http://www.ntu.ac.uk/study_with_us/fees_funding/scholarships/undergraduate/index.html?utm_campaign=ugbursaries&utm_medium=short_url&utm_source=short_url&utm_term=Other>
.

Accommodation
All new students who have NTU as their first choice are guaranteed an offer of 
university-allocated accommodation as long as they book by the 31 July. Prices 
ranges for £93.03 for a self-catered, twin room to £154.77 for a self-catered 
studio.
