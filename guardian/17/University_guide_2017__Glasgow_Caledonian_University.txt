Fees £7,000 for students from England, Wales or Northern Ireland (RUK) and a 
maximum fee of £25,000 is set for any honours degree completed in three or four 
years.

Scottish and EU students may not have to pay tuition fees if eligible for 
funding. Eligible students must apply each year through the Student Awards 
Agency for Scotland (SAAS). To find out if you are eligible contact SAAS or the 
GCU Funding Team in the first instance.


For overseas students, annual tuition fees for 2016-17 undergraduate 
programmes vary from £10,200 - £12,000.

Bursaries A range of bursaries are available, including: the Sir Alex Ferguson 
Scholarships and Awards Fund which offers widening access scholarships, travel 
bursaries and awards to students on any programme.

Students <https://www.theguardian.com/education/students> from England, Wales 
and Northern Ireland from low income families will pay no more than £17,000 for 
an honours degree. Where students exceed the entry level requirements for their 
chosen course of study, GCU will offer a bursary of £1,000, students who are in 
receipt of this award will only have to pay £21,000 fees for their normal 
length of studies.

The Principal’s Common Good Scholarship will also cover tuition fees in full 
for talented RUK students from low income backgrounds who are first in their 
families to go to university. All of the above scholarships refer to 2016-17 
entry. For more details visitwww.gcu.ac.uk/scholarships/ 
<http://www.gcu.ac.uk/study/scholarships/>

Accommodation Prices at Caledonian Court, which provides self-catering flats 
for 660 students, range from £98.32 to £111.42 per week in 2015-16, including 
utilities. Priority is given to first years, particularly those who are some 
distance from home or who have additional needs.
