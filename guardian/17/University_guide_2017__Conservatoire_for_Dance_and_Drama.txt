Fees £9,000 for full-time home and EU undergraduates for the year 2016-17. 
Fees for international students vary according to school.

Bursaries and scholarships Income-assessed scholarships of £1,500 per year for 
the first two years of training are available to students who are new to HE and 
have a household income of £25,000 or less.

Students whose household income ranges from £25,001-42,260 are entitled to up 
to £1,500 per year for the first two years of training. Talent and need-based 
scholarships are also available. Visitcdd.ac.uk/fees-and-financial-support/ 
<http://www.cdd.ac.uk/students/fees-and-financial-support/> for more 
information.

Accommodation The Conservatoire does not provide its own accommodation, but 
each school will offer advice to students who are looking for somewhere to live.
