
Andrés Iniesta is likely to sign a new five-year contract with Barcelona which 
will scupper the hopes ofManchester United 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchester-united> and Manchester City of 
signing the midfielder.

The Barça vice-president, Josep Bartomeu, said: "The negotiations are under 
way and both sides want to sign. Iniesta's new contract will be the maximum 
allowed of five years."

It had been suggested the 29-year-old Spain international, who is in his 12th 
season at Barça, was stalling on a new deal, leading tospeculation that a move 
to the Premier League was a possibility 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/sep/23/football-transfer-rumours-di-matteo-sunderland>
.

Bartomeu said that a new deal for the club's director of football, the former 
Spain goalkeeper Andoni Zubizaretta, was also imminent and that his appointment 
in 2010 had been "one of the best decisions we have made".
