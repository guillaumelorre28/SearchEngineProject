
It's time for the latest in our now-weekly roundups of the best new apps for 
iPhone <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/iphone> and iPad, which will 
vary between 10 and 20 depending on the quality and quantity available. This 
week is a busy week.

As ever, games aren't included – there's a separate post for them 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/technology/gamesblog/2012/aug/30/best-mobile-games-bastion-heroes-call>
. Android will be getting its own roundup later in the day, and Windows Phone 
and BlackBerry are set for their first monthly roundups in early September.

Here's this week's iOS selection:

C4 Paralympics <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/c4-paralympics/id550410206?mt=8>

As the Paralympic Games continue in London, Channel 4's official app is an 
impressive way to follow the action. It includes live text commentary, videos 
of highlights from each day, and news, schedules and results. TheiPad version 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/channel-4-paralympics/id554157549?mt=8> adds in 
livestreaming video.
iPhone / iPad

iTunes Festival London 2012 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/itunes-festival-london-2012/id431168066?mt=8>

Apple is about to kick off its own London event: the annual iTunes 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/itunes> Festival in Camden, with 30 
gigs planned. As usual, Apple will be livestreaming each gig to its official 
app, which will also stream videos after the performances, and help fans apply 
for tickets beforehand.
iPhone / iPad

Ladybird: I'm Ready for Phonics 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/ladybird-im-ready-for-phonics/id547616940?mt=8>

Penguin <https://www.theguardian.com/books/penguin> Books' latest app is aimed 
at five and six year-olds learning phonics. Designed with the help of literacy 
experts and tested in schools, it offers 12 levels of tests for phonemes, 
graphemes and tricky words, as well as a guide to the phonics system for 
parents.
iPhone / iPad

Lift <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/lift/id530911645?mt=8>

Spawned from the incubator launched by Twitter co-founders Evan Williams and 
Biz Stone, Lift wants to help people "overcome complacency by enabling you to 
declare your habit goals and see when you're actually making progress on them". 
From eating healthily and exercising through to reading more books, it's a 
check-in based app with social features to get encouragement from other people 
with the same goals.
iPhone

Kodiak PHP <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/kodiak-php/id542685332?mt=8>

Something for programmers here: Kodiak PHP runs any PHP code on the iPad 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/ipad> itself, providing a way for 
coders to "write and edit PHP with beautiful syntax highlighting and an 
enhanced on-screen keyboard". A file manager provides the necessary exporting 
functions.
iPad

Pinnacle Studio 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/pinnacle-studio/id552100086?mt=8>

Apple's iMovie has proved popular with iOS amateur filmmakers, but now it has 
competition from Pinnacle's new iPad app, promising "big-screen moviemaking" 
technology. It uses familiar storyboard and timeline-based editing tools, with 
export to YouTube and Facebook, or to the Pinnacle Studio PC software.
iPad
 Shuffler.fm's music discovery app is now on iPhone 
Shuffler.fm <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/shuffler.fm/id456966147?mt=8>

Music discovery app Shuffler.fm has been around for a while on iPad, but it 
has just been updated to include an iPhone version too. It's very slick, 
helping you "channel surf through the music web" – translation: browse the 
music being recommended by music blogs in various genres, and play them as a 
streaming radio station.
iPhone

FreshBooks Cloud Accounting 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/freshbooks-cloud-accounting/id542796025?mt=8>

Aimed at small businesses and freelancers, this aims to help them create 
invoices, store expenses details and track time spent on projects, with all the 
data stored on the FreshBooks server for access on multiple devices. The app 
ties in with the existing web service.
iPhone

NFL Game Rewind 
<http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/nfl-game-rewind/id548213544?mt=8>

Rights reasons mean this iPad is US-only, at least for now. It's an official 
release from theNFL <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/nfl>, streaming full 
replays of American football matches from its 2012 season, as well as providing 
"Telestrator" coaches film, replays of key moments, and other scores and stats. 
A subscription to the web version of the service is required.
iPad

Net-A-Porter <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/net-a-porter/id318597939?mt=8>

Fashion site Net-A-Porter's iPhone app has been upgraded to a universal 
version, meaning it's now on iPad as a native app too. It focuses on shopping, 
with products from 350 designers to browse, and integration with the 
Net-A-Porter wish list feature to store details of the ones you're keen on.
iPad

Learnist <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/learnist/id522850398?mt=8>

Learnist is the work of Grockit, and wants to make "sharing what you know and 
learning from others fast and easy". How? By gathering resources on specific 
topics – e-books, videos, blog posts, podcasts etc – and making them available 
as "Learnboards" to the community. The app ties into the existing Learnist web 
service.
iPhone / iPad

MetroLyrics <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/metrolyrics/id547825550?mt=8>

MetroLyrics is one of the biggest web lyric databases in the world, but now it 
has launched a new iPhone app to provide mobile access. Users can search for 
specific songs or identify what's playing in the real world, while also pulling 
in videos, scrobbling to Last.fm – a fellow CBS subsidiary – and browsing 
charts of popular tracks.
iPhone
 Tomo ABC Town offers a new spin on letter-learning for iPad 
Tomo ABC Town <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/tomo-abc-town/id547823268?mt=8>

Developer That's Junior has come up with an intriguing interactive toy for 
children based around words and letters. They drag blocks of letters into place 
to unlock each word, corresponding to an object in a virtual house, garage or 
playground.
iPhone / iPad

Dandelion <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/dandelion/id521903725?mt=8>

This children's book-app started life as a project on crowdfunding site 
Kickstarter, successfully raising $36k. Now it's available on the App Store as 
a lovely-looking "interactive, magical story" around a theme of bullying. 
Unlike the real world (sadly), here the bullies can be fended off by blowing 
virtual dandelions at them.
iPhone / iPad

Richard and Judy Book Club 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/richard-and-judy-book-club/id555630532?mt=8>

This is the work of retailer WH Smith 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/whsmith>, working with augmented reality 
firm Aurasma. Tying in with the latest edition of its book club campaign, the 
app has to be pointed at the cover of any of the featured books – at which 
point a virtual Richard and Judy pop up to deliver their review.
iPhone / iPad

Well <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/well/id526164927?mt=8>

The latest buzzy one-word-name listmaking app (see also: Clear) claims to be 
"beautiful, social, and fun". It's a way to create lists on the iPhone, while 
also getting friends to contribute their own suggestions. The app also enables 
users to browse the most popular lists from others and "relist" the most 
interesting items.
iPhone

Dan Deacon <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/dan-deacon/id536378735?mt=8>

Musician Dan Deacon has a new album out, and an official app to go with it. 
This is more about creativity than promotion, though. At gigs, the app 
"synchronizes all the phones in the room, turning you and the rest of the 
audience into the light show and sound system for the concert". Elsewhere, it 
offers a Deacon-themed synthesizer and other information on his doings.
iPhone

Stereomood 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/stereomood-tuning-my-emotions/id524634435?mt=8>

Music app Stereomood has travelled in the opposite direction to Shuffler.fm, 
moving from iPhone to iPad. The app aims to supply music based on moods: 
sleepy, chill, sunday morning, jazzy, romantic and so on. More than 100 
playlists are provided, with links to buy individual songs from iTunes.
iPad

Teatime <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/teatime/id534257003?mt=8>

If tensions are running high in your office over who hasn't made a cup of tea 
for months, Teatime may be the answer. Published by the UK's Tea Council, it's 
a way to create "tea groups", store details of how everyone likes their cuppa, 
and then automatically decide each time who should make the round.
iPhone / iPad

Mental Elf <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/mental-elf/id542192849?mt=8>

Despite the punning title and elf character (more like a gnome, in all 
honesty), this is a serious app for mental health professionals. It aims to 
provide daily articles on the latest research, policy and guidance in the area, 
with the option to add notes and share articles with colleagues.
iPhone
