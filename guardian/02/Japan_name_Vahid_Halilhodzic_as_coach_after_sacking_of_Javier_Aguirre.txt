
The Japanese football association says it has reached an agreement with Vahid 
Halilhodzic to become the national team coach.

Masahiro Shimoda, the JFA’s technical committee chairman, says the association 
is expected to approve the deal at a meeting of its executive board on 12 
March. Japanese media reported that the 62-year-old Bosnian will be paid an 
annual salary of $2.2m (£1.44m).

Japan’s previous coach, Javier Aguirre, was fired in February 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/feb/03/japan-sack-coach-javier-aguirre-investigation>
 over concerns that allegations of his involvement in a match-fixing scandal 
would interfere with qualifying for the 2018 World Cup.

Halilhodzic guided Algeria into the knockout stages of the World Cup for the 
first time in Brazil last year 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jun/27/algeria-heroic-world-cup-russia-vahid-halihodzic>
. He resigned after the tournament,vowing never to forgive the press for their 
treatment of him 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/jul/07/algeria-coach-vahid-halilhodzic-resigns-forgive-press>
.

The former Yugoslavia striker has been out of work since quitting the Turkish 
side Trabzonspor in November following a poor run.
