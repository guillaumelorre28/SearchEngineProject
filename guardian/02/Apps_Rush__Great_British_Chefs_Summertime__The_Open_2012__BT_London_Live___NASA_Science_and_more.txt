
A selection of 15 new and notable apps for you today:

Great British Chefs - Summertime 
<http://www.greatbritishchefs.com/apps/great-british-chefs-summertime>

The excellent Great British Chefs cooking app has spawned a new version for 
the summer of 2012. If it ever comes. If has 105 recipes from 21 British chefs, 
with all proceeds going to charity Action Against Hunger.
iPhone / iPad

The 2012 Open Championship <http://apps.theopen.com/>

As this year's The Open Championship tees off, golf fans can download The 
R&A's official app for Android and iOS. It'll offer live scores, video 
highlights, player profiles and stats, as well as live video streams from three 
holes (9, 10 and 11 if you're wondering) during the tournament.
Android / iPhone <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/iphone> / iPad

Decisions on the Rules of Golf 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/decisions/id535094690?mt=8>

The R&A has a second golf app out this week, but this is more for amateur 
players: a collection of every single decision and rule of golf (more than 
1,300 in total). The idea being to hit friends with a lawhammer smackdown if 
they try to cheat against you, or settle friendlier confusion.
iPhone / iPad

World Series of Poker 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.ea.game.wsop_row>

The World Series of Poker has had mobile games before, but the licence is 
owned by Electronic Arts these days. Cue a new game for smartphones and tablets 
to play online (for fun, not real money) against players around the world. It 
requires a Facebook login to play, though. The link above is for Android but
here's the iOS version 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/world-series-of-poker/id504643601?mt=8>.
Android / iPhone / iPad

BT London Live <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/bt-london-live/id540712388?mt=8>

BT's London Live events will be held during the Olympic Games with a mixture 
of music, entertainment and live sports. There's now an official app for iPhone 
– I'm guessing an Android version is imminent too – offering news, maps of the 
three sites, and a personal calendar.
iPhone

Nimbuzz 
<http://www.windowsphone.com/en-US/apps/dbff9b0e-7c9d-4914-b56a-9d6052a9a7c2>

Windows Phone <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/windows-phone> will soon 
have the full set of messaging alternatives to its own clients, with Nimbuzz 
the latest to launch for Microsoft-powered smartphones. It offers instant 
messaging, picture-sharing and Twitter integration, as on other platforms.
Windows Phone

Mirror's Edge 
<http://www.wpcentral.com/xbox-live-mirrors-edge-released-exclusively-lumia-windows-phones>

For now, only owners of Nokia Lumia handsets will be able to get the Windows 
Phone version of Mirror's Edge, thanks to an exclusivity deal. As on iOS, it's 
a slinky touchscreen-focused version of EA's parkour-based platformer.
Windows Phone (Lumias)

Jewel Quest Heritage 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.iwin.jewelquest4>

Casual games firm iWin has launched a new game in its Jewel Quest franchise, 
looking to take the battle back to PopCap's Bejeweled. Expect 175 boards of 
jewel-swapping puzzling, and three modes of play.
Android

Thomas's Travels in Bookland 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/thomass-travels-in-bookland/id539433008?mt=8>

French developer La Souris Qui Raconte's latest original book-app focuses on a 
boy called Thomas, who while locked in a library meets a host of literary 
characters including Little Red Riding Hood and Alice. A neat idea, 
complemented by some characterful visuals.
iPad

NASA Science: A Journey of Discovery 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/nasa-science-journey-discovery/id541482963?mt=8>

NASA is on a big apps push at the moment: two out last week, and now another 
one. ThisiPad <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/ipad> app promises "the 
latest information from NASA's Science Missions, including the spacecraft, 
their instruments, the data, and what we are learning about the questions we 
seek to answer".
iPad

Pocketmags - Digital Magazine Newsstand 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/pocketmags-digital-magazine/id447766921?mt=8>

There are an increasing number of iOS alternatives to Apple's digital magazine 
Newsstand, with Pocketmags the latest to launch. Going head to head with that, 
as well as Zinio, Lekiosk and Next Issue, Pocketmags' app includes mags like 
Good Homes, Macworld, Digital Arts, Xbox 360 Gamer and more.
iPad

Cowboy Guns <http://appworld.blackberry.com/webstore/content/124963/?lang=en>

There are some interesting original games coming through for the BlackBerry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/blackberry> PlayBook, with Cowboy Guns 
the latest. Launched by developer Union, it's a cinematic Western with guns, 
bounties and boss battles galore.
BlackBerry PlayBook

Ripley's Believe It or Not! 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/ripleys-believe-it-or-not!/id524046235?mt=8>

Ripley's has become famous for its collection of weird phenomena, and now it 
has an official app. It offers a mixture of photos, videos, stories and trivia, 
along with an "Oddscan" mode to scan targets in the company's books and museums 
to get extra stuff.
iPhone

Marvin and Molly Book 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/marvin-and-molly-book/id541534615?mt=8>

My children really enjoyed a book called Marvin Wanted More, about a greedy 
sheep. Now he's popped up on iOS, albeit with a different story. It includes 22 
pages, lots of interactivity, and the App Store's first mention (I think) of 
"sheep face painting" as a feature.
iPhone / iPad

Smudge. <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/smudge./id541699567?mt=8>

Whoah! We're going to Ibiza... Better take an innovative sound-installation 
app with us. It's the work of music technology firm Liine, and works with 
something called ENTER.Interakt at the Space Ibiza club – a solo residency by 
techno pioneer Richie Hawtin. "Twist, pull and warp Smudge to shape a step in 
the sequence of audio/visual events in the room," explains its App Store 
listing.
iPhone / iPad
