
Destroyer’s tenth album has already been receiving rave reviews, with some 
claiming that it could be the critically-adored Canadian outfit’s best album 
yet.

Destroyer: Poison Season review – overwhelming, aggravating, brilliant
 Read more  
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/aug/20/destroyer-poison-season-review> 
It’s certainly a record that nods to the classics – Lou Reed and Bruce 
Springsteen are two reference points here – yet it also has its own unique 
feel. Dream Lover is an explosive and joyous burst of guitars and sax, whereas 
Hell features strident strings and a melancholic brass part. In fact, the whole 
album is orchestrated beautifully – drifting dreamily in places; bursting into 
life during others.
