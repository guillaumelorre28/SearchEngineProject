<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/jan/29/american-sniper-old-fashioned-western>
 screenwriter Jason Hall is to make his directorial debut with a drama about 
post-traumatic stress disorder.

The film is based on Thank You for Your Service, Washington Post reporter 
David Finkel 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2013/nov/08/thank-you-service-david-finkel>
’s account of Iraq war veterans’ readjustment to domestic life after finishing 
their tours; the book is a follow-up toThe Good Soldiers 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/2011/mar/26/good-soldiers-david-finkel-review>
, the result of Finkel’s experience of being embedded with the US army in 
Baghdad during the2007 “surge” 
<https://www.theguardian.com/news/video/2007/jul/13/inside.surge.part2>. 

Thank You for Your Service has been in the works since 2013, when DreamWorks 
acquired the film rights prior to publication. Hall had been commissioned to 
write a script before the release ofAmerican Sniper 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/jan/18/american-sniper-review-bradley-cooper-real-life-tale-legendary-marksman>
, and thelatter’s stellar success 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/feb/10/american-sniper-biggest-film-of-2014-us-box-office>
 has seen Hall elevated to the director’s chair.

Before Sniper, Hall had a moderately successful career as an actor, including 
several appearances in Buffy the Vampire Slayer as regular characterDevon 
MacLeish <http://buffy.wikia.com/wiki/Devon_MacLeish>.
