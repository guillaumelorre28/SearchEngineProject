
A selection of seven new and notable apps for you today:

Draw Race 2 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.chillingo.drawrace2gplay.android.aja>

Developer RedLynx's touch-racing game sequel has been ported to Android 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/android> by Chillingo. As with its 
predecessor, you have to trace your finger round the track to pick the best 
racing line for your car. It includes 180 challenges in solo mode, and online 
challenges for friends.
Android

Apptivity WWE Rumblers 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/apptivity-wwe-rumblers/id531810505?mt=8>

This is Mattel's second Apptivity app for iPad 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/ipad>. Like Disney's AppMates, it 
involves placing a physical toy onto the touchscreen, and using it to interact 
with a game. In this case, the game is wrestling, with a series of licensed WWE 
stars to battle.
iPad

Samsung: Take Part 2012 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.samsung.samsungtakepartolympics>

Another day, another official Olympic Games app. This one's from Samsung, 
which involves 3D mini-games for running, swimming and shooting. Oh, and 
augmented reality penalty kicks and archery thrown in for good measure.
Android

Mini Empires <http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/mini-empires/id510873386?mt=8>

Mini Empires is a massively multiplayer online blend of Advance Wars, 
FarmVille and Civilization, released foriPhone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/iphone>. An intriguing prospect, not 
least because it's the work of developer Midverse Studios, which was founded 
recently by Riz Virk and Mitch Liu, of Tap Fish app-fame.
iPhone

MapleStory Live 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/maplestory-live/id479812410?mt=8>

Interesting to see this quietly appear on the App Store: "The Complete 
MapleStory Mobile Experience" for iPhone. MapleStory being Nexon's popular 
massively multiplayer roleplaying game, with tens of millions of players in its 
existing online version.
iPhone

Zombies, Run! 
<http://www.windowsphone.com/en-US/apps/f772fc86-96dc-4093-b944-dde8fcd4e8f0>

Running game Zombies, Run! has made plenty of headlines from its versions on 
other platforms, but now it's available onWindows Phone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/windows-phone> too. The idea: you run 
(in the real world) while being pursued by (not-real) zombies, with a storyline 
from novelist Naomi Alderman fuelling your exercise.
Windows Phone

Cartoon ABC <http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/cartoon-abc/id528233122?mt=8>

Dreadful ABC apps are ten-a-penny on the App Store, but Cartoon ABC could be 
one of the good ones. Developed by Kids Academy Company, it promises 
well-designed flashcards and animated videos.
iPhone / iPad
