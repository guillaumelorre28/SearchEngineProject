
Juventus made light of their woeful domestic form to record a 2-1 victory 
againstReal Madrid <https://www.theguardian.com/football/realmadrid>. 
Alessandro Del Piero gave Juventus the perfect start to their Group H tie in 
Turin, scoring in the fifth minute. The Brazilian Amauri made it 2-0 soon after 
half-time with a header. Ruud van Nistelrooy headed home for Real.

The Juventus <https://www.theguardian.com/football/juventus> coach, Claudio 
Ranieri, said: "Tonight we were a little bit lucky. Real is used to starting a 
game at 1,000 kilometres an hour and we wanted to do that to them, and that is 
what we did by starting with an incredible first 20 minutes."

In the group's other game Fatih Tekke salvaged a 1-1 draw for Zenit St 
Petersburg <https://www.theguardian.com/football/zenitstpetersburg> after 
levelling 10 minutes from the end against BATE Borisov.

A hat-trick that included two goals in three minutes for Joseba Llorente helped
Villarreal <https://www.theguardian.com/football/villarreal> to a 6-3 win over 
Aab Aalborg at El Madrigal in Group E.

Dynamo Kiev secured second spot in Group G behind Arsenal thanks to Oleksandr 
Aliev's 27th-minute free-kick that gave the Ukraine side a 1-0 win againstPorto 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/porto> in Lisbon.
