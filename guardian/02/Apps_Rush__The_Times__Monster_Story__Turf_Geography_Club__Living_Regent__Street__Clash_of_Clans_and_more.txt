
A selection of 12 new and notable app for you today:

The Times 
<http://www.windowsphone.com/en-US/apps/0d583b02-1d81-4070-ac23-cdc464236b3e>

News International has launched an official Windows Phone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/windows-phone> app for newspapers The 
Times and The Sunday Times, offering a 30-day free trial before users have to 
sign up for a full digital subscription on the Times website. Existing 
subscribers can access it for no additional charge.
Windows Phone

Monster Story 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.teamlava.monster>

Freemium mobile games publisher TeamLava has a new game in its Story series. 
Monster Story focuses on hatching and breeding monsters, decorating their 
island home and completing quests, as well as interacting with Facebook 
friends. The link above is for Android, buthere's the iOS version 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/monster-story/id544840181?mt=8>.
Android / iPhone / iPad

Turf Geography Club 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/turf-geography-club/id515839839?mt=8>

People have tried the idea of location-based Monopoly-style games before 
– MyTown being one prominent example. Turf Geography Club is the latest 
example, built on top of Foursquare. The idea: own real-world businesses near 
you, while investing virtual coins in them. Tiny Tower-esque visuals and a bear 
with a hand-puppet also star.
iPhone

Living Regent Street 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/living-regent-street/id546626504?mt=8>

There's an official app for London's Regent Street developed by the Living Map 
Company, which promises that the map will evolve over time, rather than 
remaining static. Every shop has opening times info as well as website links 
and phone numbers, with social media, photos and transport data pulled in too.
iPhone

Clash of Clans <http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/clash-of-clans/id529479190?mt=8>

Supercell is trying to put a bit more depth into freemium social gaming on 
iOS, with Clash of Clans its second game. It involves raising a fantasy army, 
defending your own village and conquering those of other players.
iPhone / iPad

The Bourne Reality 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/the-bourne-reality/id546546135?mt=8>

Published by Ludlum Games – presumably related to author Robert Ludlum – this 
game based on the Bourne series of books/films offers a mixture of trivia and 
spying tasks.
iPhone

National Geographic Traveller - Family 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/national-geographic-traveller/id528066508?mt=8>

National Geographic's bi-annual magazine for travelling families has been 
turned into an iOS app, aiming to open the eyes of parents and children alike 
"from beach to museum, city to village, and villa to self-catering, glamping 
and more".
iPhone / iPad

Agent Dash 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.fullfat.android.agentdash>

I covered Full Fat's Agent Dash earlier this week on iOS, but the game is 
actually out onAndroid <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/android> too. 
Across both platforms, it takes the Temple Run format and applies it to a 
dashing secret agent.
Android

Flight! <http://itunes.apple.com/us/app/flight!/id496191902?mt=8>

Armor Games' popular web game Flight! has made the leap onto iOS, with a 
casual flight-sim that involves catching shooting stars and upgrading your 
plane as you play.
iPhone / iPad

Kairobotica 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=net.kairosoft.android.starkairo_en>

Japanese games publisher Kairosoft has a fervent following in the West for its 
simulation games, so Kairobotica is likely to make a splash on Android. Yes, 
robots are the focus here, along with space colonisation and plenty of battles.
Android

Ghostbusters Paranormal Blast 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/ghostbusters-paranormal-blast/id533816549?mt=8>

XMG Studio has put a new spin on classic film Ghostbusters, turning it into an 
augmented reality game for iOS challenging players to "battle ghosts in your 
neighbourhood".
iPhone / iPad

SolaRola 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.squareenix.solarola>

Another game to launch on iOS in recent days, then quickly appear on the 
Google Play store for Android. SolaRola harks back to Sony's LocoRoco with its 
gameplay: colourful physics-based ball-rolling fun.
Android
