The Bourne Legacy: Operation Intel 
<http://itunes.apple.com/app/bourne-legacy-operation-intel/id540749997>

More movie-based appage here, albeit only available in the US for now. NBC 
Universal's new iPhone app offers "real-world missions" based onThe Bourne 
Legacy <https://www.theguardian.com/film/the-bourne-legacy>. Players have to 
complete the Google Map-based missions and compete using Facebook Connect.
iPhone

Omega <https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.omegacatalog.android>

Fresh from launching an Olympic Games-themed app for iPad, watch-maker Omega 
has a newAndroid <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/android> smartphone 
app too. This one is focused on its wider collection, including news, plenty of 
photographs and images of "brand ambassadors" like George Clooney and Nicole 
Kidman.
Android

Checkmark <http://itunes.apple.com/app/checkmark/id524873453?ls=1&mt=8>

Checkmark is picking up a lot of buzz: it's a reminders app for iPhone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/iphone> that lets you set reminders 
based on date and time, but also based on location. For example, reminding 
yourself to buy milk next time you're near the local supermarket, or an alert 
when you reach your train stop.
iPhone

Viral Video eXchange <https://www.vvx.io/>

Here's something fun: a game for trading virtual shares in viral videos, with 
the aim of getting people to pick the videos they think will find a wide 
audience, as early as possible. It's an HTML5-based web app that runs in 
smartphone and tablet browsers as well as on the desktop.
HTML5

Fotopedia China 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/fotopedia-china/id533413751?mt=8>

Photography service Fotopedia has launched its latest travel app, this time 
focusing on China. It promises photos and visual stories from across the 
country, as well as interactive maps and a trip builder feature for users who 
are actually going there.
iPhone / iPad

Change4Life Fun Generator 
<http://www.nhs.uk/Change4Life/Pages/fun-generator-mobile.aspx>

Today is the last day of term for many schools in the UK, with parents 
wondering how to keep their children occupied for the six-week summer holiday. 
This app from the Department of Health's Games4Life campaign hopes to help, 
suggesting more than 100 indoor and outdoor games and activities for children.
Android / iPhone / iPad

Shark Dash Free 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.gameloft.android.ANMP.GloftFDHM>

Gameloft's fishy physics-puzzler Shark Dash gets a new free-to-play version 
for Android, tying in with the Discovery Channel's annual Shark Week. The game 
involves launching a shark at ducks while jumping through levels.
Android

Cat Cora's Kitchen 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/cat-coras-kitchen/id540859310?mt=8>

TV chef Cat Cora is a famous name in the US thanks to her appearances on the 
Iron Chef America show, and more recently on Bravo's Around the World in 80 
Plates. Now she has aniPad <https://www.theguardian.com/technology/ipad> app 
with 76 recipes and more than 20 tutorial videos, as well as a scheduling 
feature that helps you time when cooking multiple dishes at once.
iPad

Bakery Story: World Games 
<http://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/bakery-story-world-games/id538065388?mt=8>

Social/mobile games developer TeamLava has released a spin-off from its Bakery 
Story freemium game that In No Way At All is related to this summer's Olympic 
Games. Expect gold medal cookies, podium cakes, trampoline tables and torches.
