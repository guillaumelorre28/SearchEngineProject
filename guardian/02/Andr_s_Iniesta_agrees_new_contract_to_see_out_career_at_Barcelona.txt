
Andrés Iniesta <https://www.theguardian.com/football/andres-iniesta> has 
extended his contract with Barcelona until 2018.

The 29-year-old midfielder, whose existing deal would have expired in 2015, is 
now set to see out his career at the Spanish champions, having already won 21 
trophies at the club.

The club's president, Sandro Rosell, confirmed the move at a press conference 
on Thursday. 
<http://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/detail/article/rosell-announces-new-iniesta-contract-until-2018>
 Rosell told the club's official website: "Andrés Iniesta's renewal is 
confirmed. It's a great Christmas present.

"He will stay with us until 2018 and he could renew every year after that 
based on his playing performances."

Iniesta will have been at the club for 22 years when his contract expires 
during which time he has won back-to-back European Championships and the first 
World Cup forSpain <https://www.theguardian.com/football/spain> when he scored 
the winning goal against Holland in the final.

He becomes the fourth first-team player to be signed up until 2018 following 
Sergio Busquets, Cristian Tello, Leo Messi and Neymar.
