
Biopics Trumbo and Steve Jobs <https://www.theguardian.com/film/steve-jobs> 
have gained an extra push in the race for next year’s Oscars after scoring 
multiple nominations from the Screen Actors Guild.

<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/dec/08/oscars-2016-revenant-creed-hateful-eight-joy>
The 1940s-set drama Trumbo 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/13/trumbo-review-bryan-cranston-toronto-tiff>
, about a blacklisted Hollywood screenwriter, picked up the most with three 
nods: best ensemble cast, best actor for Bryan Cranston and best supporting 
actress for Helen Mirren, who also picked up a surprise best actress nomination 
for the widely panned dramaWoman in Gold 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/woman-in-gold>.

Other unexpected nominees included Sarah Silverman as best actress for her 
role in the addiction dramaI Smile Back 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/jan/26/sundance-2015-review-i-smile-back-sarah-silverman>
, and Michael Shannon as best supporting actor for the thriller99 Homes 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/aug/29/99-homes-review-andrew-garfield-and-michael-shannon-flog-the-foundations-of-america>
.

Michael Fassbender and Kate Winslet also scored nominations for their roles in 
Steve Jobs <https://www.theguardian.com/film/steve-jobs> while journalism drama 
Spotlight 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/03/spotlight-review-catholic-church-child-abuse-film-decently-tells-an-awful-story>
 was recognised for best ensemble and Rachel McAdams as best supporting actress.

The transgender drama The Danish Girl 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/the-danish-girl> has also picked up added 
Oscar buzz with nominations for both Eddie Redmayne and Alicia Vikander, while 
Netflix received two nominations forBeasts of No Nation 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/02/beasts-of-no-nation-review-brutal-epic-opens-awards-season-in-style>
: best ensemble, and Idris Elba as best supporting actor.

Other nominees included Leonardo DiCaprio for best actor in The Revenant 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/the-revenant>, Johnny Depp as best actor in 
Black Mass 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/sep/04/black-mass-review-compelling-true-drama-is-mighty-comeback-for-johnny-depp>
 and Cate Blanchett as best actress inCarol 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/may/16/carol-review-cate-blanchett-captivates-in-woozily-obsessive-lesbian-romance>
.

There was, however, no sign of The Martian 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/oct/04/the-martian-review-matt-damon-shines-stranded-astronaut>
,The Hateful Eight <https://www.theguardian.com/film/hateful-eight>, Creed 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/nov/18/creed-review-new-rocky-movie-is-a-split-decision>
 orJoy 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/dec/07/joy-film-review-jennifer-lawrence-david-o-russell-bradley-cooper>
, all of which had been considered potential nominees, leaving stars Matt 
Damon, Jennifer Jason Leigh, Sylvester Stallone and Jennifer Lawrence in need 
of some added buzz to feature in the race.

Last year’s SAG winners perfectly matched the Oscars, with Eddie Redmayne, 
Julianne Moore, JK Simmons and Patricia Arquette all taking home awards.
