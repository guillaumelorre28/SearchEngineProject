
The former Fulham, Wigan and Hull City 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/hullcity> midfielder Jimmy Bullard has 
been appointed as the new manager of Leatherhead, the Ryman League Premier 
Division club have announced.

It is a first coaching role for the 37-year-old, who also had spells with 
Peterborough, Ipswich and MK Dons, as well as West Ham where he never made an 
appearance. He was called into the England squad three times but again without 
playing a game. He has since worked as a broadcaster and also appeared onthe 
ITV reality TV show I’m a Celebrity, Get Me Out Of Here 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/dec/02/jimmy-bullard-voted-off-im-a-celebrity>
 in 2014.

experience into a variety of management and coaching techniques as a player and 
[has] a very clear philosophy in terms of how he wishes his team to play.”

Bullard’s first game in charge will be away to Folkestone Invicta on Tuesday.
