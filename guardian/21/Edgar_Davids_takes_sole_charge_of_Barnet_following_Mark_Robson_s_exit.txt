
Edgar Davids has taken sole charge of Barnet 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/barnet> after the club announced Mark 
Robson has left his duties as joint head coach.

Robson shared the role with the former Ajax, Juventus and Tottenham 
midfielder, whosigned as a player-coach in October this year 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2012/oct/11/edgar-davids-barnet>.

Robson leaves the club with immediate effect while Davids will continue in his 
role as head coach and takes full responsibility for first-team matters.

The Bees have recently won back-to-back games, including victory over the 
League Two leaders, Gillingham, to lift them out of the relegation zone.

A club statement read 
<http://www.barnetfc.com/news/article/club-announcement-28.12.12-565126.aspx>: 
"Barnet FC can confirm Mark Robson has left his duties as joint head coach at 
the club.

"It was felt that clarification was required regarding the coaching 
responsibilities at the club and to this end it was agreed that Mark should 
leave with immediate effect.

"The club would like to thank Mark for all his efforts and hard work and wish 
him the very best for the future."
