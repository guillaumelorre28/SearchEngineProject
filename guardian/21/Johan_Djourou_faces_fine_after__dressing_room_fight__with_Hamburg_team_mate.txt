
The Hamburg players Johan Djourou and Valon Behrami are set to be fined by the 
strugglingBundesliga <https://www.theguardian.com/football/bundesligafootball> 
club after allegedly coming to blows at half-time during last weekend’s match 
against Wolfsburg.

According to the German newspaper Bild, the two Swiss internationals started 
arguing in French before ending up on the floor with shoes flying through the 
home dressing room.

The club’s sporting director, Dietmar Beiersdorfer, said: “There was an 
emotional dispute. We have disciplinary rules and those who break them will be 
punished.”

Hamburg, who have never been relegated from the Bundesliga, lost the game 
againstWolfsburg <https://www.theguardian.com/football/wolfsburg> 2-0 and are 
bottom with six games remaining.

After the defeat against Wolfsburg, Lewis Holtby was involved in an emotional 
exchangewith some of the club’s supporters, saying “I am trying my best for 
this club. I don’t want it to be relegated either. I am only a human being.” 
<http://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/hsv/holtby-verzweifelt-am-fan-zaun-40516200.bild.html>

Hamburg <https://www.theguardian.com/football/hamburg> face Werder Bremen on 
Sunday.
