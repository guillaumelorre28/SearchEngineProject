
Non-league Dover shocked Cheltenham 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/cheltenham> to book their place in the FA 
Cup third round thanks to a late goal from captain Connor Essam.

The former Crawley and Gillingham defender threaded his shot beyond Town 
goalkeeper Trevor Carson in the 82nd minute to send the Conference side through 
at the expense of the League Two outfit.

Worcester are also in the hat after coming from behind to draw 1-1 at 
Scunthorpe.

The Conference North side, who knocked out Coventry in round one, were 
trailing to Paddy Madden’s 10th goal of the season.

But the picture changed dramatically in the opening minute of the second half 
when striker Daniel Nti lashed home an equaliser from close range after the 
home defence had failed to deal with a left-wing cross.

Barnsley <https://www.theguardian.com/football/barnsley> also face a replay 
after they were held to a goalless draw at home by Chester FC while another 
Conference side, Aldershot, drew 0-0 with Rochdale.

Kieran Agard spared Bristol City’s blushes with an injury-time winner as the 
League One leaders beat spirited AFC Telford 1-0.

Sean Rigg scored the only goal as AFC Wimbledon reached the third round for 
the first time in their 12-year history following a 1-0 win at Wycombe.

Bradford <https://www.theguardian.com/football/bradford> avoided an upset as 
they beat Conference side Dartford 4-1 in a one-sided tie at Valley Parade.

Gateshead progressed to the third round 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/dec/07/gateshead-warrington-fa-cup-second-round-match-report>
 for the first time in 60 years with a 2-0 win over Warrington, the 
lowest-ranked side left in the competition, while Southport beat Conference 
rivals Eastleigh.
