
Born in Sunderland and raised in Scotland, Emeli Sandé 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/emeli-sande>, 29, studied to be a doctor 
before concentrating on singing and songwriting. Her debut album,Our Version Of 
Events 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/feb/09/emeli-sande-version-events-review>
, was 2012’s bestseller, and she has won three Brit awards and two Ivor Novello 
awards. Her new album isLong Live The Angels 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2016/nov/10/emeli-sande-long-live-the-angels-review-immaculately-tame>
 and she tours the UK in October. She is divorced and lives in London.

When were you happiest? 
Last night, on stage in New York.

What is your greatest fear? 
I don’t want to get to the end of my life and wish I’d lived it differently.

What is your earliest memory?
My mum listening to Yazz <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UtKADQnjQmc>.

Which living person do you most admire, and why?
Stevie Wonder <https://www.theguardian.com/music/steviewonder>: he’s brought 
so much joy to the world for so many years.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself?
I’m a bit naive sometimes.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
Dishonesty.

What was your most embarrassing moment?
When I was on stage and could feel my stick-on ponytail slipping off.

Property aside, what’s the most expensive thing you’ve bought? 
A Feurich piano: one of the first things I bought when I could afford it.

What is your screensaver?
A painting by a spray-paint artist called Jamie Evans 
<http://www.jamielawsonevans.com>.

What makes you unhappy?
If I’m in a place with no music.

What is your most unappealing habit?
I like to crack my knuckles.

What is your favourite word?
Abracadabra.

What is top of your bucket list?
Performing in Zambia, where my father’s family lives.

Which book changed your life?
Malcolm X’s autobiography.

What did you want to be when you were growing up? 
I always wanted to be a singer, but I also wanted to be a doctor. I almost got 
there: I did four years and got my neuroscience BSc.

What is the worst thing anyone’s said to you?
When I was trying to leave medical school, they said I should give up this 
music thing, because it’s not really going to be anything.

What is your guiltiest pleasure?
Butterscotch Angel Delight.

What does love feel like?
Freedom: it’s euphoric.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party? 
Nina Simone, Jacqueline du Pré, Nelson Mandela, Malcolm X and Joni Mitchell.

What is the worst job you’ve done?

I was a perfume girl at a department store in Aberdeen. I was so bad at it, I 
didn’t make it to the end of the day.

If you could edit your past, what would you change?
I would instil confidence in my younger self.

How often do you have sex?
Often enough.

What is the closest you’ve come to death?
On a camping holiday in Scotland when I was nine: I was in an inflatable 
dinghy that drifted out to sea. A lifeboat had to come.

What song would you like played at your funeral?
Sir Duke by Stevie Wonder <https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hmKshpLXnxE>: it 
would leave everyone feeling good.

What is the most important lesson life has taught you?
The only thing that matters is right now.

Tell us a secret 
By night I am a rapper and my rap name is Ngosa.
