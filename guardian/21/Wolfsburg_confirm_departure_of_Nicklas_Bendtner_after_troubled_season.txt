
Nicklas Bendtner’s departure from Wolfsburg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/wolfsburg> has been confirmed by the 
Bundesliga club, who said expectations have not been fulfilled by the Danish 
striker nor the club.

The 28-year-old had been contracted to until the end of next season but a 
mutual decision was taken after a disruptive campaign in whichhe has been fined 
for oversleeping 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/mar/16/nicklas-bendtner-wolfsburg-oversleeping>
 and missing training among other indiscretions.

Bendtner made 47 appearances for Wolfsburg since joining from Arsenal in 2014, 
scoring nine goals.

“After two years we have to admit that the expectations have not been 
fulfilled, neither for him nor for the club,” the sporting director, Klaus 
Allofs,told the club’s website 
<https://www.vfl-wolfsburg.de/info/aktuelles/detailseite/artikel/vertragsaufhebung-42505.html>
. “To continue would not have made sense. Therefore the decision to end this 
deal is the best solution for everyone. We wish Nicklas all the best for his 
future career.”
