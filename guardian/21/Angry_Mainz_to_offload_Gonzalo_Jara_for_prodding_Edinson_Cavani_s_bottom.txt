
Mainz are ready to offload Gonzalo Jara after the Chile international made 
headlines at theCopa América <https://www.theguardian.com/football/copa-america>
 by appearing to poke Edinson Cavani’s bottom before falling to the floor 
theatrically when the Uruguay striker retaliated.

Television images showed the 29-year-old defender, who has a contract until 
2016, prodding Cavani duringChile’s 1-0 victory 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/jun/25/copa-america-chile-beat-uruguay-to-reach-semi-finals>
 in Santiago on Wednesday. Cavani reacted by pushing Jara away with the back of 
his hand but the Chilean fell to the ground as if he had been punched. The 
Brazilian referee showed Cavani his second yellow card of the game and sent him 
off.

“He knows that if an offer comes he can go,” the Mainz sports director, 
Christian Heidel,told Bild 
<http://www.bild.de/sport/fussball/mainz-05/ist-empoert-41517336.bild.html>. 
“We do not tolerate that. More than the prod, however, it is what comes 
afterwards that makes me angry. I hate theatrics more than anything.”

Images of the incident spread quickly on social media and it was the main 
talking point from the match, which sentChile 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/chile> into the last four of the 
competition for the first time in 16 years.
