
Sienna Miller <https://www.theguardian.com/film/sienna-miller> is set to 
return to Broadway to play the nightclub singer Sally Bowles in the hit musical 
Cabaret.

The actor, who is enjoying a career renaissance with roles in the films 
American Sniper 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/nov/21/clint-eastwood-heartfelt-tribute-us-muscle-iraq>
andFoxcatcher 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/may/19/cannes-2014-foxcatcher-review-steve-carell>
,will replace Emma Stone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2014/nov/12/emma-stone-cabaret-review-fragile-sally-bowles>
 for the final six weeks of the show’s Broadway run. It is set to close on 29 
March.

Many high-profile actors have played Sally Bowles, including Judi Dench in the 
first British production in 1968 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2014/oct/27/how-we-made-cabaret-judi-dench-john-kander>
,Liza Minnelli <https://www.theguardian.com/music/2008/may/04/popandrock> in 
the 1972 film and Jane Horrocks in the 1993 Sam Mendes production, on which the 
current revival is based.

The most recent Broadway run saw Michelle Williams play Bowles to a mixed 
critical reception 
<http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-2612840/Michelle-Williams-poses-Cabaret-opening-night-party-performance-receives-tepid-reviews.html>
, before Stone succeeded her.

Miller made her Broadway debut in Patrick Marber’s After Miss Julie in 2009. 
She gota rough ride from critics 
<http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/culturemonster/2009/10/sienna-miller-in-after-miss-julie-on-broadway-what-did-the-critics-think.html>
, with the New York Times’s Ben Brantley saying that Miller “never had a firm 
grasp” on the role.

However, Cabaret, which plays at the site of the legendary New York disco 
Studio 54, has been a smash hit,taking $1,051,850 over New Year’s week 
<http://variety.com/2015/legit/news/record-broadway-box-office-2014-wicked-new-years-1201392876/>
. It stars Alan Cumming as the Emcee, who has won multiple awards for the role, 
including a Tony in 1998.
