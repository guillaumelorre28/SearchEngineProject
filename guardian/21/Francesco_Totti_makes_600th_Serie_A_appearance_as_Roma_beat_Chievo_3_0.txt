
Francesco Totti came off the bench to make his 600th league appearance for 
Roma as they beat in-form Chievo 3-0 at home on Sunday to climb to second place 
inSerie A <https://www.theguardian.com/football/serieafootball>.

The victory moved Roma <https://www.theguardian.com/football/roma> up to 77 
points with one game left in the race for the second automatic Champions League 
berth, one ahead of Napoli who play Torino later on Sunday.

The Chelsea target Radja Nainggolan 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/apr/08/radja-nainggolan-chelsea-roma-in-talks-transfer>
 slammed home the opener in the 18th minute before Antonio Rudiger doubled the 
home side’s lead with a 39th-minute header.

Miralem Pjanic added a third when he was picked out by an excellent pass from 
the 39-year-old Totti and he finished calmly past Albano Bizzarri.

Totti, whose future at the club beyond the end of the season remains uncertain 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/apr/18/francesco-totti-luciano-spalletti-roma-meeting-argument>
, was introduced as a 59th-minute substitute for Mohamed Salah and the club’s 
beloved captain received an emotional ovation in what may be his final match at 
the Stadio Olimpico.
