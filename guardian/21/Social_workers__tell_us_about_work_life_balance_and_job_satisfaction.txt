
How valued do the UK’s social workers feel? What are their job satisfaction 
levels, and can they achieve a good work-life balance?

To better understand the issues affecting social workers and their managers, 
the Guardian, in partnership with theBritish Association of Social Workers 
<https://www.basw.co.uk/>, has launched its latest Social Lives survey 
<http://www.surveys.com/SocialLives2016>.

Pressure mounts for social workers in the north of England. Is austerity to 
blame?
 Read more  
<https://www.theguardian.com/social-care-network/2016/may/11/pressure-mounts-for-social-workers-in-the-north-of-england-is-austerity-to-blame>
Whatever stage you’re at in your career – whether a student or a head of 
department – we want to hear how you feel about the profession, its public 
standing and the opportunities it offers.

Share your thoughts; take the survey here 
<http://www.Surveys.com/SocialLives2016> and be part of the research. The short 
survey takes no more than 10 minutes to complete.

The findings will be compared with previous surveys in the series 
<https://www.theguardian.com/social-care-network/2015/dec/08/mission-impossible-on-a-daily-basis-the-real-effect-of-spending-cuts-on-social-work>
 and will be published in 2017.
