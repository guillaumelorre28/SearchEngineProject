
Manchester City <https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchestercity> have 
signed the defender Pablo Mari from Gimnàstic Tarragona while sending out their 
Spanish midfielder Manu García to Alavés on a season-long loan.

Mari is set to go straight out on loan to Gimnàstic’s fellow Spanish 
second-tier side Girona as the City manager Pep Guardiola wheeled and dealed in 
his home country. The 22-year-old had been with Gimnàstic since 2013, making 90 
appearances.

A statement on the club’s website 
<http://www.gimnasticdetarragona.cat/pablo-mari-cat/> said: “We thank Pablo 
Mari for his commitment, discipline and hard work that he has contributed 
during these three years in the ranks of the first team.

“Gimnàstic de Tarragona wishes Mari good luck on a personal and professional 
level in his new team, Manchester City FC.”

Meanwhile a statement from Alavés  
<http://www.deportivoalaves.com/noticia/manu-garcia-calidad-y-criterio-para-el-centro-del-campo>
read: “Deportivo Alavés has secured the services of Manu García, a young and 
promising player from Manchester City.

“The talented footballer arrives on a season-long loan and with a lot of 
enthusiasm to showcase his talent in La Liga.”

City also confirmed the move on their Twitter account: “Manu García has joined 
La Liga side Alavés on a season-long loan...Good luck Manu!”

The 18-year-old made his City debut last season and has featured four times 
for the first team in all competitions to date, scoring one goal. He arrived at 
City’s youth academy in 2013 from Sporting Gijón and is under contract with the 
English club until June 2017.

Alavés finished top in Spain’s second division in the 2015-16 campaign and 
will play in La Liga after a 10-year wait. The Basque side begin the campaign 
against Atlético Madrid at the Vicente Calderón stadium on Sunday.
