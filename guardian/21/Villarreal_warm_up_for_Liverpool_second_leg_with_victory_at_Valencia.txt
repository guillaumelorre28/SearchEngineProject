
Villarreal warmed up for their Europa League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/uefa-europa-league> semi-final second leg 
at Liverpool by clinching a berth in Europe’s elite competition.

Villarreal’s 2-0 win at their regional rivals Valencia guaranteed they will 
finish fourth in the Spanish league, giving the club aChampions League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/championsleague> playoff spot next season.

The “Yellow Submarine” can now focus on earning a spot in this season’s Europa 
League final. Marcelino Garcia’s team head to Anfield on Thursday for the 
second leg of the semi-final a week afterAdrián López’s stoppage-time goal 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/apr/28/villarreal-liverpool-europa-league-match-report>
 gave them a 1-0 advantage.

Roberto Soldado was the only player to return to Villarreal’s starting 11 from 
the first-leg win overLiverpool <https://www.theguardian.com/football/liverpool>
 and the former Valencia striker excelled as a playmaker, setting up Samuel 
García’s 14th-minute opener before López continued his strong form with another 
goal cued by Soldado in the 33rd.

The win gave Villarreal <https://www.theguardian.com/football/villarreal> a 
six-point cushion over Athletic Bilbao, with two rounds left, and a better 
head-to-head record.

Earlier Aritz Aduriz and Raúl García led Bilbao’s 2-1 fightback over 10-man 
Celta Vigo.
