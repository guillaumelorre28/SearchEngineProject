
Wladimir Klitschko <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/wladimir-klitschko> has 
accused Tyson Fury of “dragging boxing through the mud” after the he twice 
called off a fight against the Ukrainian, briefly retired from the sport and 
admitted to substance abuse.

Fury was due to defend his WBA and WBO title, two of the three he won via a 
shock victory over Klitschko in Düsseldorf last November 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/live/2015/nov/28/wladimir-klitschko-v-tyson-fury-boxing-live>
, against the same opponent in July butpulled out with an ankle injury 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/jun/24/tyson-fury-pulls-out-wladimir-klitschko-injury>
. He then rescheduled for this month but postponed again, citing anunspecified 
medical condition 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/sep/25/tyson-fury-peter-fury-wladimir-klitschko>
, with Fury making further headlines bytelling Rolling Stone magazine he had 
taken to drinking and cocaine to deal with depression 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/oct/04/tyson-fury-cocaine-live-anymore-boxing>
.

“Fury is dragging boxing through the mud,” Klitschko told the German newspaper 
Bild on Thursday.

In regards to Fury’s total inactivity since becoming world heavyweight 
champion, Klitschko’s manager, Bernd Bönte, also told the newspaper. “He stalls 
with Wladimir. He blocks the world title.”

It was announced by the British Board of Control on Wednesday that they will 
review Fury’s licence at a meeting on 12 October 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/oct/05/tyson-fury-licence-discussion-british-boxing-board-control>
 in light of his interview with Rolling Stone and failure to defend his the 
titles he took off Klitschko 11 months ago.

The WBO are also poised to strip Fury of their version of the heavyweight title
 <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/oct/05/tyson-fury-world-title-wbo> for 
similar reasons.
