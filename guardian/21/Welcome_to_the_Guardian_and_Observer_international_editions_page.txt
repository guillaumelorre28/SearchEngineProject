
Guardian News & Media has ceased publication of international print editions

Guardian News & Media (GNM) announced 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/guardian-observer-cease-international-editions>
 in July 2011 it would cease publishing the international editions of the 
Guardian and Observer, as part of its digital-first strategy.

Guardian and Observer readers abroad are now able to subscribe to the Guardian 
<https://www.qssweb.co.uk/guardiansubscriptions/subscribe/default.aspx?title=gua&prom=web6>
 andObserver 
<http://www.qssweb.co.uk/guardiansubscriptions/subscribe/default.aspx?title=obs&prom=webc>
 domestic editions sent out from the UK. They are also to subscribe to our
digital editions <http://guardian.newspaperdirect.com/epaper/viewer.aspx>, 
Kindle editions <https://www.theguardian.com/mobile/guardian-kindle-edition> and
Guardian iPad edition 
<https://www.theguardian.com/mobile/ipad/guardian-ipad-edition> and access 
Guardian content via theiPhone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/mobile/iphone/guardian>, Android 
<https://www.theguardian.com/mobile/android/guardian>, Nokia and Windows 
<https://www.theguardian.com/mobile/windows-guardian> apps.

Please note that The Guardian Weekly <https://www.theguardian.com/weekly>, our 
blend of international news, politics, culture and comment, combining articles 
from the Guardian and selected features from the Observer, the Washington Post 
and Le Monde, is still widely available in newsagents worldwide.
