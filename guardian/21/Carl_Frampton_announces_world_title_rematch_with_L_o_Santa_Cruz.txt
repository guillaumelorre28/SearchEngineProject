Carl Frampton has announced he will defend his WBA world featherweight title 
in a rematch against the former champion Léo Santa Cruz.

The Belfast-born boxer defeated the 28-year-old Mexican in July 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/jul/31/carl-frampton-beats-leo-santa-cruz-wba-featherweight-title>
 to become the first Northern Irishman to win world titles at two different 
weights following a majority decision in New York.

Frampton tweeted on Thursday 
<https://twitter.com/RealCFrampton/status/789059751489499136>: “Done deal! The 
rematch vs @leosantacruz2 is on!!!” He first became a world champion in 
September 2014 afterovercoming Kiko Martínez on a points decision 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/sep/07/carl-frampton-beats-kiko-martinez-world-super-bantam-title>
 in their IBF super bantamweight clash. Frampton went on to defend that title 
on two more occasions before adding the WBA super bantamweight belt to his 
collection with victoryover long-time rival Scott Quigg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/feb/27/carl-frampton-beats-scott-quigg-world-super-bantamweight-title>
 in a unification fight in February this year.
