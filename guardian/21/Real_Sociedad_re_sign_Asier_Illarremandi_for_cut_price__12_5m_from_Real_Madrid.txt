
Asier Illarramendi is said to be “very happy” to be returning to his boyhood 
clubReal Sociedad <https://www.theguardian.com/football/realsociedad> after a 
disappointing spell at Real Madrid. The 25-year-old arrived in San Sebastián on 
Wednesday morning and added that he was “pleased” to be returning.

Illarramendi, a holding midfielder, was in the Spain squad that won the 
Under-21 European Championship in 2013 and moved to the Bernabéu soon after. 
However, he failed to make an impact there and is returning to the club where 
he made his name, now under the management of David Moyes, for around £12.5m.He 
joined Real for £28.5m 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/jul/12/real-madrid-sign-asier-illarramendi>
.

“I’m very happy everything has been completed as it is what I was looking for 
and what I wanted,” said Illarramendi, who had reportedly attracted interest 
from Tottenham. “I’m very happy to be back home and from now on I will work 
very hard with the team.”
