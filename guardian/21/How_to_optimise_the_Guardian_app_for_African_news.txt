
Hope everyone is enjoying our new Android 
<https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.guardian> and iPhone 
<https://itunes.apple.com/gb/app/the-guardian/id409128287?mt=8> apps for phones 
and tablets.

We’re constantly looking to improve how the app delivers a personalised and 
engaging experience for each and every reader, which includes how it can best 
serve you content most relevant to your location and interests.

In the meantime we’ve created a step-by-step guide for getting the most out of 
theGuardian’s Africa 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/series/guardian-africa-network> coverage. 
Helping you to find the best news, videos and opportunities to contribute to 
our stories. We’ll show you how to keep tabs on your favourite correspondents 
and commentators and how to bookmark your stories to read offline.

Add to homepage

You can add the Africa keyword page <https://www.theguardian.com/world/africa>
, theGuardian Africa Network 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/series/guardian-africa-network> series page 
or any contributor page (such as your favourite writer).

Start by searching for them. Open the navigation by tapping on the blue bottom 
top-left, then the search icon at the bottom of the menu.

Type in ‘Africa’

Read offline and save for later

Almost all of our content is available to read offline. If you can see a link 
to a piece of content on the homepage or a section page, you should be able to 
read it offline. Simply open the app or visit a section to automatically cache 
all of the most recent content to read wherever you are.

You can also save any content to read later by tapping the bookmark icon on 
the top right. The content will then appear in your ‘saved items’ list which 
can be accessed from the menu
