
Milan have appointed the former Fiorentina and Sampdoria manager Vincenzo 
Montella on a two-year contract, the Serie A clubannounced on Tuesday 
<http://www.acmilan.com/en/news/show/162263>.

The 42-year-old will begin work with the Italian side from 1 July and will 
replace Cristian Brocchi, who took charge on an interim basisafter Sinisa 
Mihajlovic was sacked 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/apr/12/sinisa-mihajlovic-sacked-milan-christian-brocchi-silvio-berlusconi>
 in April.

The former striker has also managed at Catania following a playing career that 
took him to Empoli, Genoa, Sampdoria and Roma, as well as a brief loan spellat 
Fulham in 2007 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2007/jan/04/newsstory.fulham>.

Montella, who took over at Sampdoria last November and led them to 15th in 
Serie A, represented Italy 20 times between 1999 and 2005, scoring three times 
for his country.

In a statement, the club said: “It is the belief of the club that the style of 
play of the new Rossoneri coach will be the basis of an important, fruitful and 
positive collaboration. Heartfelt thanks to Cristian Brocchi for the 
enthusiasm, preparation and intensity with which he approached his career as
Milan <https://www.theguardian.com/football/acmilan> coach with the youth sides 
and the first team.”

Milan, who lost to Juventus 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/may/21/juventus-milan-italian-cup-final-match-report>
 in the Coppa Italia final last month, finished seventh in the league last 
season and will miss out on Europe for a third successive campaign.
