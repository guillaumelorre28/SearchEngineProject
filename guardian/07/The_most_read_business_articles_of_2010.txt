
Debt, bailouts, more debt and delinquent homeowners: those were the themes of 
our most popular business stories last year, along with some entertaining 
Greek-bashing by the Germans and some explosive revelations from theWikiLeaks 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/wikileaks> embassy cables.

Here, as measured by pages viewed, are the 10 most-read stories on 
theguardian.com/business <https://www.theguardian.com/uk/business> last year.

1. Get up earlier, Germans tell Greeks 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/mar/05/bild-open-letter-greece-papandreou>

A bit of Greek-bashing by the German press was our most popular story of 2010, 
although probably not in Greece. Amid growing anger over the bailout, the 
tabloid Bild published an open letter to the prime minister, George Papandreou, 
advising the Greeks to "get up earlier and work all day".
2. WikiLeaks cables: Shell's grip on Nigerian state revealed 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/dec/08/wikileaks-cables-shell-nigeria-spying>

US embassy cables revealed top executive's claims that the company "knows 
everything" about key decisions in government ministries.
3. Now we know the truth. The financial meltdown wasn't a mistake – it was a 
con 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/apr/18/goldman-sachs-regulators-civil-charges>

Will Hutton's call to break up the banks and launch an official investigation 
into what went wrong, written in the wake of news that Goldman Sachs was to 
face civil charges for fraud, struck a chord with readers.
4. $2tn debt crisis threatens to bring down 100 US cities 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/dec/20/debt-crisis-threatens-us-cities>

More than 100 American cities could go bust in 2011 as the debt crisis 
threatens next to spark a municipal meltdown, according to leading US analyst 
Meredith Whitney.
5. Angela Merkel dashes Greek hopes of rescue bid 
<https://www.theguardian.com/theguardian/2010/feb/11/germany-greece-merkel-bailout-euro>

Talks over the Greek bailout were fraught, with prudent Germany reluctant to 
dig its more profligate European neighbour out of the mire. It all worked out 
OK, though - so far.
6. UK budget deficit 'to surpass Greece's as worst in EU' 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/may/05/uk-budget-deficit-worse-than-greece>

Greece again, except this time it was the eve of election warning from the 
European commission that the British budget deficit was likely to become the 
biggest in the EU – overtaking even Greece and Ireland.
7. WikiLeaks cables: Pfizer 'used dirty tricks to avoid clinical trial payout 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/dec/09/wikileaks-cables-pfizer-nigeria>
'

Cables say the drug company hired investigators to find evidence of corruption 
on Nigeria's attorney general to persuade him to drop legal action.
8. The rise and fall of American Apparel 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/aug/25/rise-fall-american-apparel>

It wasn't so much the well-publicised sexual exploits of American Apparel 
founder Dov Charney that did for the floundering hipster brand, it was more a 
case of mounting debts and unhappy creditors. And the fact that it became more 
fashionable to laugh at hipsters than to be one.
9. US homeowners flock to Florida event in desperate bid to save properties 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/aug/30/us-homes-borrowers-foreclosures>

Welcome to America's biggest jamboree of delinquent borrowers – more than 
20,000 American borrowers hit Palm Beach for a five-day mortgage modification 
marathon.
10. US military warns oil output may dip causing massive shortages by 2015 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/apr/11/peak-oil-production-supply>

Surplus oil production capacity could disappear within two years, creating 
serious shortages by 2015, the US military warned.
