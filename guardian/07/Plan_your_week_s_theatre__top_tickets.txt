
Monday

Sh!t Theatre’s Sing-a-long-a Muppet Christmas Carol should be fun at Camden 
People’s theatre 
<https://www.cptheatre.co.uk/production/sing-a-long-muppets-christmas-carol/>. 
In his show Icons atSoho theatre 
<http://www.sohotheatre.com/whats-on/le-gateau-chocolat-icons/>, London, the 
cabaret star Le Gateau Chocolat explores his personal objects of worship, 
performing songs byKate Bush <https://www.theguardian.com/music/kate-bush>, 
Whitney Houston <https://www.theguardian.com/music/whitney-houston> and more. 
For more alternative Christmas entertainment, the Royal Vauxhall Tavern’s 
Charming Dick atthe Cockpit <http://www.thecockpit.org.uk/show/charming_dick> 
should be camp fun. Also in the capital, The Flanagan Collective’s A Christmas 
Carol at theArts theatre 
<https://artstheatrewestend.co.uk/whats-on/the-flanagan-collectives-a-christmas-carol-(dinner-theatre)/>
 invites you to Mr Scrooge’s house for a two-course Christmas feast.

Tuesday

Marisa Carnesky aims to put the magic back into menstruation with Dr 
Carnesky’s Incredible Bleeding Woman atSoho theatre 
<http://www.sohotheatre.com/whats-on/dr-carneskyandrsquo-s-incredible-bleeding-woman/>
. Matthew Bulgo’s Last Christmas, about growing up and facing your demons, is 
at theTraverse 
<https://www.traverse.co.uk/whats-on/event-detail/959/last-christmas.aspx> in 
Edinburgh, which also hasBlack Beauty 
<https://www.traverse.co.uk/whats-on/event-detail/881/black-beauty.aspx>. David 
Walliams <https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/david-walliams>’s space 
adventure The First Hippo on the Moon, sets out on tour from theRoyal Hippodrome
 <http://royalhippodrome.com/show/david-walliams-the-first-hippo-on-the-moon> 
in Eastbourne. There are two great shows for younger members of the family: 
Oily Cart’s In a Pickle, inspired by The Winter’s Tale, is atArts Depot 
<https://www.artsdepot.co.uk/family-christmas/pickle> in London, and one of my 
favourite shows of 2015, Theatre Rites’Beasty Baby 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2015/oct/21/beasty-baby-review-polka-london-theatre-rites>
, is back and atLakeside 
<http://www.lakesidearts.org.uk/theatre/event/3205/beasty-baby.html> in 
Nottingham.

Wednesday

Baddies: The Musical <https://www.unicorntheatre.com/baddies2016> and Boing! 
<https://www.unicorntheatre.com/Boing> are both hit family shows at the 
Unicorn, London. Dreamgirls opens at theSavoy theatre 
<http://www.savoytheatre.org/dreamgirls/>. The Snow Child 
<https://www.sheffieldtheatres.co.uk/whats-on/snow-child> and Annie Get Your Gun
 <https://www.sheffieldtheatres.co.uk/whats-on/annie-gun> are at the Crucible 
in Sheffield. Thebes Land by Franco-Uruguayan playwright Sergio Blanco 
considers classical and contemporary myth-making and is definitely worth your 
time at theArcola <https://www.arcolatheatre.com/event/thebes-land/> in London, 
where you can also catch Samantha Ellis’s clever romcomHow to Date a Feminist 
<https://www.arcolatheatre.com/event/how-to-date-a-feminist/>.

Thursday

Shon Dale-Jones’s The Duke 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/theatreblog/2016/aug/04/the-duke-edinburgh-fringe-shon-dale-jones>
, asking what we really value, is atthe Pit 
<http://www.barbican.org.uk/theatre/event-detail.asp?ID=20123> at the Barbican. 
The York Theatre Royal’sJohn Cooper Studio 
<https://www.yorktheatreroyal.co.uk/event/the_great_gatsby.php#.WEmZ0jaLTAw> is 
where you can find the Guild of Misrule’s version of The Great Gatsby. Alan 
Lane from Slung Low directs The Siege of Christmas, which is interactive fun at 
theContact 
<http://contactmcr.com/whats-on/59496-contact-and-slung-low-present-the-siege-of-christmas/>
 in Manchester. Mr Popper’s Penguins arrives at theCriterion 
<https://www.criterion-theatre.co.uk/mr-poppers-penguins?eventid=1401> in 
London; Isaw it at the Lowry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/2016/jan/05/mr-popper-s-penguins-pins-and-needles-review>
 in January and it’s good fun.

Friday and the weekend

The RSC’s Love Labour’s Lost 
<http://www.trh.co.uk/whatson/rsctheatre-royal-haymarket-season/> and Much Ado 
<http://www.trh.co.uk/whatson/much-ado-about-nothing-or-loves-labours-won-performances-from-9th-december/>
, set before and after the first world war, have settled into the Theatre Royal 
Haymarket. Cinderella at theLondon Palladium 
<http://www.reallyusefultheatres.co.uk/performances/show/cinderella> is so 
stuffed with stars it surely can’t be a turkey. Murray Schisgal’s 1960s comedy, 
Luv, is revived at thePark <https://www.parktheatre.co.uk/whats-on/luv> in 
north London. Mac Barnett’s book Extra Yarn is adapted for the over-threes at 
the Orange Tree <https://www.orangetreetheatre.co.uk/whats-on/extra-yarn> in 
Richmond. You’re supposed to go wearing your favourite jumper …
