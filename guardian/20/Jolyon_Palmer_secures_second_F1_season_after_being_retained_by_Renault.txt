
Jolyon Palmer has secured a second season in Formula One 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/formulaone> after the British driver was 
retained by Renault for 2017.

Palmer, whose future in the sport was thrown into doubt 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2016/oct/20/jolyon-palmer-deserves-a-formula-one-future-insists-his-father-jonathan-renualt>
 after only his second race, will line up alongside Nico Hülkenberg next season.

Kevin Magnussen, Palmer’s team-mate with whom the 25-year-old from Horsham had 
been battling for the second vacant seat atRenault 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/renault>, is poised to join the American 
team Haas.

Palmer lies 17th in the current drivers’ standings with one point from 19 
starts.
