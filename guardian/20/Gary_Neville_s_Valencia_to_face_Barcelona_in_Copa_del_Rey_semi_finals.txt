
The holders Barcelona will meet Valencia in the semi-finals of the Copa del Rey
 <https://www.theguardian.com/football/copa-del-rey>, with Sevilla taking on 
Celta Vigo in the other last-four tie.

The first legs will be played on 3 and 4 February at the Camp Nou and Ramón 
Sánchez Pizjuán, respectively, with the return meetings a week later.

Barça eased into the last four earlier this week with a 3-1 victory over 
Athletic Bilbao 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jan/27/barcelona-athletic-bilbao-copa-del-rey-match-report>
, which sealed a 5-2 aggregate win.

Celta stunned Atlético Madrid 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/atleticomadrid> in Wednesday’s other 
quarter-final with a 3-2 victory at the Vicente Calderón, following a goalless 
first leg.

Valencia <https://www.theguardian.com/football/valencia> – yet to win in seven 
Primera División matches under the coach Gary Neville – reached the semi-finals 
on Thursday thanks to a 1-0 win, 2-1 on aggregate, over Las Palmas.

Sevilla <https://www.theguardian.com/football/sevilla> cruised through thanks 
to a 3-0 win against second-tier Mirandés, which secured Unai Emery’s side a 
5-0 aggregate rout.
