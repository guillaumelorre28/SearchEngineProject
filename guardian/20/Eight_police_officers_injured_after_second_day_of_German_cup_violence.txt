
Eight police officers were injured in clashes with fans before the German Cup 
match betweenEintracht Frankfurt 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/eintrachtfrankfurt> and Kaiserslautern on 
Wednesday – 24 hours after violence marred another tie between Dynamo Dresden 
and Borussia Dortmund.

Eintracht and Kaiserslautern 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/kaiserslautern> have a long history of 
animosity between their fans. Police say they were attacked with stones, 
bottles and firecrackers and responded with batons, and made eight arrests. One 
woman was seriously injured when she fell on to railway tracks.

On Tuesday, 15 people were arrested and several injured after clashes between 
officers and Dynamo Dresden supporters at their cup match atBorussia Dortmund 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/borussiadortmund>.

Dresden's fans fought police before, during and after the match, leaving two 
officers injured. The match was briefly interrupted several times when Dresden 
fans threw firecrackers and flares on to the field.

Dortmund's chief executive, Hans-Joachim Watzke, said the violence caused 
damage estimated at £130,000, with around 200 seats destroyed, kiosks damaged 
and toilets set on fire.

German police reported that the number of people injured in violence at games 
in the two top divisions reached a 12-year high last season.
