
Gianfranco Zola <https://www.theguardian.com/football/gianfranco-zola> has 
been sacked as coach of Cagliari after less than three months in the job, the 
Serie A club announced on Monday.

The Italian, who took over on 24 December, has not been able to turn around 
the team’s fortunes in his 10 games in charge withCagliari 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/cagliari> four points adrift of safety. 
Zdenek Zeman, who made way for Zola but is still being paid by the club, is 
likely to be put back in charge.

In a statement confirming Zola and his backroom staff’s departure, Cagliari 
said: “The decision was made reluctantly against a great man who wrote 
unforgettable pages in the history of Cagliari, giving lustre and international 
prestige to the land of Sardinia.

“He and his staff go with the thanks for the work done in recent months with 
dedication, professionalism and passion, and the heartfelt good luck for the 
rest of his career.”

The former Napoli, Chelsea and Italy forward, who finished his playing career 
at the club in 2005, managed two wins, two draws and six defeats, leaving 
Cagliari joint 18th inSerie A 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/serieafootball>.

Claudio Ranieri, Walter Zenga and Delio Rossi are also in the frame should 
Zeman opt not to return.
