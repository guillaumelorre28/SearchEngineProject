
The former Germany <https://www.theguardian.com/football/germany> and FC Köln 
striker Dieter Müller is in a coma following a heart attack.

Kickers Offenbach, the club where he was president for 12 years, said on 
Friday that the 58-year-old collapsed at his home near Frankfurt and was 
revived by his wife before the arrival of the ambulance.

Müller's six goals for Köln in a 7-2 win over Werder Bremen in 1977 is still a 
Bundesliga individual record. He scored 231 in 326 matches forKöln 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/cologne> and was the league's top scorer 
in 1977 and 1978, when the club won the championship.

He had nine goals in 12 games for Germany and also won French league titles 
with Bordeaux in 1984 and 1985.
