
Quique Sánchez Flores has been named as the new head coach of Espanyol. Flores 
has joined the La Liga club on a three-year contract, havingleft Watford at the 
end of the season 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/may/13/watford-part-company-quique-sanchez-flores>
.

“This is consolidating the beginning of Espanyol’s sports project in this new 
and exciting stage for all our fans,” said Espanyol’s sporting director, Angel 
Gómez. “In the next few days the club will confirm Sánchez Flores’ staff.“

Flores’s coaching career includes spells as Getafe, Valencia, Benfica and 
Atlético Madrid, for whom he won the Europa League. The 51-year-oldtook Watford 
to the FA Cup semi-finals 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2016/apr/24/crystal-palace-v-watford-fa-cup-semi-final-live>
 and a respectable finish in the Premier League but parted company with the 
club at the end of the season.

Flores replaces the Romanian Constantin Galca, who left the club last month 
after only five months in charge.
