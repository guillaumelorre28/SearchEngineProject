Preamble

Hello. Manchester United 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchester-united> are not yet certain of 
being in the next round of the Europa League – they’re no Apoel Nicosia, 
Krasnodar or Genk, after all – but they will reach it if they muster a draw or 
better in Ukraine today. In fact, victory would enable them to finish top of 
their group if Fenerbahce do not win away at Feyenoord.

United toiled to beat Zorya 1-0 at Old Trafford back in September but they are 
in better form now and showing clear signs of progress under Jose Mourinho. 
Still far from perfect but significantly more vibrant than they were under 
Louis Van Gaal. And tonight’s team sheet reveals more good news: Eric Bailly is 
back! The Ivorian hasn’t played since limping out of the 4-0 defeat at Chelsea 
in late October and now here he is making his return, on a frigid pitch in 
Luhansk. It’s the stuff of which dreams are made. Everything is coming up Jose. 
If there’s danger today, it is likely to come from Ivan Petriak, the left 
winger who looked very useful at Old Trafford and could provide a stern test of 
Ashley Young’s right-back credentials.
