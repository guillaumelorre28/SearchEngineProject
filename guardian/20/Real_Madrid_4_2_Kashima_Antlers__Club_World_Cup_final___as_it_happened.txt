does so well and Madrid have secured their second Club World Cup in three years.

Cristiano Ronaldo hat-trick wins Club World Cup for Real Madrid after scare

Here it is! Sergio Ramos lifts the Club World Cup to fireworks and jubilation! 
Well done, Real Madrid, and well doneKashima Antlers 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/kashimaantlers> too. That’s all from me, 
thanks for reading.
 Real Madrid celebrate with the trophy. Photograph: Mike Hewitt - FIFA/FIFA 
via Getty Images  
Out of nowhere Colombian side Atlético Nacional have turned up to collect 
their third-place medals. Unbelievable. Please, please dish out the trophy.
 
Gaku Shibasaki took the bronze ball. His goals today were lovely to watch. 
Ronaldo now accepts a big cardboard key. I’m lost. Please dish out the trophy.
 
Modric receives the ‘silver ball’ for being the second best player of the 
tournament. Cristiano Ronaldo accepts the ‘golden ball’ of which he has many.
 Another one. Photograph: Reuters / Issei Kato/Reuters  Go me. Photograph: 
Shuji Kajiyama/AP  
Fifa chief Gianni Infantino is on hand to dish out some medals. First the 
officials, and now theKashima Antlers 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/kashimaantlers> – who also take down the 
fair play award.
 
Real Madrid’s unbeaten record, by the way, now stretches to 37 games and this 
will be the third trophy they’ve picked up during that run.
 
A medium-budget light show ensues before the Club World Cup 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/worldclubchampionship> is dramatically 
taken from one plinth and placed on another. That seemed like a crucial piece 
of the narrative but I’m unsure why.
 
It’s a little chilly in Yokohama and Ronaldo is wearing a puffa jacket as his 
team go to collect the trophy. The lights go out, some intense music comes on, 
and I can tell this is going to be a terrific trophy presentation.
 
That’s Cristiano Ronaldo’s 40th hat-trick for Real Madrid 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/realmadrid>. Not bad.
 
The Kashima Antlers <https://www.theguardian.com/football/kashimaantlers> were 
brilliant, as were their tireless fans, and gave Real Madrid a real scare. They 
might have got more had the referee gone through with his initial reaction when 
it seemed Sergio Ramos might be sent off, and had Yuma buried his header in 
extra-time we might now be in a penalty shootout. But Ronaldo did what Ronaldo 
does so well and Madrid have secured their second Club World Cup in three years.
 
Well that was an awful lot of fun. 
 
ET 119 min A couple of minutes left and the Kashima fans will bounce and chant 
their way through them..
 
ET 116 min Naomichi Ueda (of pre-match crocodile fame) blocks Morata’s shot on 
the line to deny what would have been a sensational goal. The striker weaved in 
and out on a long dribble, leaving defenders lying on the ground before 
pummelling a shot past the keeper Sogahata but Ueda did well to get back.
 
ET 113 min Kovacic injects a little pace into a tired game and wins a foul in 
Kashima’s half. Akasaki replaces Nagaki.

ET 105 min The second half gets under way. Kovacic replaces Modric and almost 
immediately puts Ronaldo in on goal, but Sogahata comes out of goal to 
intercept.
 
Half-time in extra-time: Real Madrid 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/realmadrid> 4-2 Kashima Antlers

Had Yuma found the back of the net instead of the meat of the crossbar we 
could be looking at a thrilling finale, but instead it looks like being Real 
and Ronaldo’s night.
 
Kroos’s shot is scuffed into the path of Ronaldo in the box who quickly shifts 
the ball from under his feet before slamming in his hat-trick. It’s a brilliant 
reaction from the forward and finally Real have surely shaken off Kashima for 
good.
 Ronaldo celebrates with Casemiro. Photograph: Kimimasa Mayama/EPA  
ET 101 min Crossbar! Shibasaki delivers the free-kick and Yuma slams a header 
on to the bar from six yards. Kashima should be level again!
 
ET 99 min Mu shows a lovely touch of skill to shimmy away from Casemiro and 
draw a foul. The Madrid midfielder is booked and Kashima will have a free-kick 
wide on the left.
 
And just like that Real lead. Benzema drops deep, turns, and slides Cristiano 
Ronaldo into the area who hits a quick shot low past Sogahata.

ET 93 min Substitute Fabricio is booked for an uncultured lunge on Varane. 
 
Real immediately get their collective foot on the ball and make Kashima chase. 
 
What a game. Kashima have stunned Real Madrid 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/realmadrid> and Shibasaki’s second goal 
in particular was a thing of beauty. I wouldn’t like to accuse the European 
champions of complacency, but you can draw your own conclusions from moments 
like Ramos’s spectacular assist for that Shibasaki goal. Another 30 minutes to 
come. The players have finished their pre-extra-time huddles...
 
Kashima finish on the attack and Endo should win it, but with the last kick of 
the 90 minutes he slices his shot badly wide from an admittedly tight angle. 
Extra-time awaits.
 
90 min Controversy in Yokohama. Ramos commits a foul and the referee jogs over 
reaching for his pocket. Ramos has already been booked so this is his game 
over, surely... but the Madrid captain runs away from the scene and the referee 
seems to backtrack on the decision in the meantime, simply awarding a free-kick.
 
88 min A couple of brilliant chances for Kashima which could have been game 
winners, the latter of which saw Navas make a crucial save from Kanazaki Mu.
 
87 min Kashima attack, lifting crosses into the box, but when Real eventually 
win it back they are badly exposed. Ronaldo is released one on one and this 
looks ominous as he approaches the area, but then seems to lose his footing and 
has the ball taken away.
 
85 min Real come forward again and Benzema releases Ronaldo on the left, whose 
cross is blocked. Corner. Kroos works a short one with Ronaldo before bending a 
lovely ball to the back post for Benzema, who shrugs off his marker and sends a 
diving header over the bar from no more than five yards. That was an excellent 
chance.
 
83 min Carvajal attempts a cross which is too close to keeper Sogahata. 
Ronaldo looks disgusted.
 
80 min Zidane makes his first change: Isco replaces Lucas Vázquez.
 
77 min Vázquez approaches from the right of the box but his route is closed 
off so he tees up the arriving Modric instead. The midfielder slams a shot 
towards goal but a couple of defenders in red and black throw themselves in its 
path to make the block.
 
75 min The Kashima supporters continue their constant wave of noise in 
Yokohama, although their team are having to chase the ball again as Real begin 
to assert some control. Kroos drifts a cross from the left towards Ronaldo in 
the centre but it’s flicked away by Shoji.
 
72 min Ronaldo has the ball in the Kashima net again after pouncing on a 
rebound from Benzema’s powerful volley, but he was well offside when the shot 
was hit.
 
71 min Endo shoots from just outside Madrid’s area but it is straightforward 
for Navas to gather.
 
69 min This looks nasty – Kaylor Navas rushes out unnecessarily to get his 
head to a ball 30 yards from goal and in doing so crashes into the retreating 
Sergio Ramos. The Real captain takes a bit of time to get his breath back but 
he’s OK to continue.
 
66 min Fabricio replaces Ogasawara for Kashima. 
 
62 min Real almost take the lead, first when a fired-up Ronaldo strikes a low 
drive towards the post which Sogahata tips away, before a Ramos shot flies just 
over.
 
Lucas Vázquez chases a through pass into the box where he is first held back 
by Shibasaki before being clattered by the captain Yamamoto. It’s a definite 
penalty and the latter is booked. Ronaldo drives the penalty into the 
bottom-left corner before rushing back to the centre with the ball under his 
arm.
 
Penalty to Real Madrid!
