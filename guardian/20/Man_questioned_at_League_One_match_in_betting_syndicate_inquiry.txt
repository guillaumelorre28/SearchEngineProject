
A man was questioned at a League One 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/leagueonefootball> football match by 
investigators probing an alleged overseas betting syndicate fraud.

He was held during the Coventry City 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/coventry> v Crawley Town match at 
Sixfields Stadium in Northampton on Sunday, which ended in a 2-2 draw. Sussex 
Police said the man was not arrested but was spoken to by the FA fraud unit in 
relation to an alleged overseas betting syndicate fraud.

A police spokesman said: "He was not arrested and was not linked to any club. 
This is part of an ongoing investigation by the FA into betting syndicate 
fraud, and Northampton and Sussex Police officers were there to support the FA 
fraud team in dealing with the man and obtaining his information."
