
Scunthorpe <https://www.theguardian.com/football/scunthorpe> United have 
sacked their manager Mark Robins.

The former Rotherham, Coventry and Barnsley manager was appointed in October 
2014 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/oct/13/scunthorpe-mark-robins-new-manager-russ-wilcox>
, succeeding the sacked Russ Wilcox.

He had been out of work since leaving Huddersfield just one game into the 
2014-15 season and was handed a three-year contract.

A statement on the club website said: 
<http://www.scunthorpe-united.co.uk/news/article/club-statement-2907770.aspx>: 
“Scunthorpe United Football Club have terminated the contracts of manager Mark 
Robins and assistant manager Ned Kelly with immediate effect.

“United chairman Peter Swann said: ‘We are sad to part company with Mark and 
Ned, but we have to think about the best interests of Scunthorpe United 
Football Club.

‘We wish Mark and Ned all the best for the future.’”

Scunthorpe lie 16th in League One 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/leagueonefootball>, six points clear of 
the relegation zone.
