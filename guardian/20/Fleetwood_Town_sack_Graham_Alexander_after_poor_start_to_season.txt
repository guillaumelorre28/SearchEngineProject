
Fleetwood Town <https://www.theguardian.com/football/fleetwood-town> have 
sacked their manager, Graham Alexander, in the wake of Tuesday’s 5-1 defeat at 
Gillingham.

The 43-year-old has been at the club since December 2012 but has been 
dismissed after only two wins this season. The Scot leaves Fleetwood 20th in 
League One but guided the team to the third tier for the first time in their 
history withvictory over Burton Albion 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/may/26/burton-albion-fleetwood-town-league-two-play-off-final-report>
 in the 2014 play-off final.

Speaking after the heavy defeat at Priestfield, Alexander told BBC Radio 
Lancashire: “I’m the manager, of course I take responsibility. I’m not stupid, 
I don’t hide, I’m a man. I’ve been through hard times as a player, I’ve been 
through hard times as a manager and this is a hard time.”
