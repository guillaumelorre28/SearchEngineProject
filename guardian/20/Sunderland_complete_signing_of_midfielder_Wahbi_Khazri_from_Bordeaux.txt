The Premier League strugglers Sunderland 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/sunderland> have completed the signing of 
the Tunisia international Wahbi Khazri from Bordeaux for an undisclosed fee, 
believed to be in the region of £9m.

The French-born Khazri, an attacking midfielder, is Bordeaux’s top scorer in 
the league this season with five goals. Sunderland, second from bottom in the 
Premier League,also signed the Ivory Coast central defender Lamine Koné from FC 
Lorient this week 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2016/jan/27/sunderland-lamine-kone-lorient-sam-allardyce>
.

During the transfer window the north-east club have also recruited the Senegal 
striker Dame N’Doye on loan from Turkey’s Trabzonspor and the defender Jan 
Kirchhoff from Bayern Munich.

The Sunderland manager Sam Allardyce said of the 24-year-old Khazri: “He is a 
quick, dynamic attacking player.”
