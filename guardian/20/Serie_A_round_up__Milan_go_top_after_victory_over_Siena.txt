
Milan moved above Juventus and Udinese at the Serie A 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/serieafootball> summit with a 2-0 victory 
over struggling Siena at the San Siro. After a frustrating first half, Milan 
made the breakthrough in the 54th minute through Antonio Nocerino.

Zlatan Ibrahimovic sealed the win with a penalty in the 63rd minute, after 
Kevin-Prince Boateng had taken a theatrical tumble overSiena 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/siena> goalkeeper Zeljko Brkic in the box.

The result moved Milan <https://www.theguardian.com/football/acmilan> a point 
clear of Juve and Udinese, who both play on Sunday.

Fiorentina <https://www.theguardian.com/football/fiorentina> and Atalanta 
shared the points after a glut of goals in the final 10 minutes saw their 
meeting end 2-2 in Florence.

Alberto Gilardino's early goal gave the Viola a lead which lasted for 72 
minutes, before Andrea Masiello and Germán Denis turned the game on its head – 
but a final twist was to follow as Stevan Jovetic levelled with two minutes 
remaining.

Chievo saw off Cagliari <https://www.theguardian.com/football/cagliari> 2-0 to 
move above their opponents into the top half of the standings. Cyril Théréau 
and Gennaro Sardo scored the goals for the Flying Donkeys in front of a paltry 
crowd of 12,000 at the Stadio Bentegodi.
