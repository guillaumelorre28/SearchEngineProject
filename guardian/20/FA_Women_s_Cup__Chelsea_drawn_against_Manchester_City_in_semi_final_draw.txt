
Chelsea will face Manchester City in the semi-finals of the FA Women’s Cup, 
while last season’s beaten finalists Everton will play Notts County for the 
other spot in the 3 May final,which takes place at Wembley for the first time 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/mar/09/wembley-host-womens-fa-cup-final-2015>
.

Chelsea <https://www.theguardian.com/football/chelsea>, who overcame last 
year’s winners Arsenal in the quarter-finals, are the early pace-setters in the 
Women’s Super League after two wins from their opening two games. City lie 
second after a win and a draw.

In the second semi-final, Everton – the only former winners left in the 
competition – go against WSL 1 opposition inNotts County 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/nottscounty>, who thrashed Aston Villa 
5-1 in the last round.
