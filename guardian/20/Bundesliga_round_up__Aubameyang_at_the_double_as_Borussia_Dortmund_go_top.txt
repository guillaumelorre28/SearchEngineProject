
Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang struck twice as Borussia Dortmund took over at the 
top of theBundesliga <https://www.theguardian.com/football/bundesligafootball> 
table with a 5-1 mauling ofWolfsburg on Tuesday night.

The full-backs Raphael Guerreiro and Lukasz Piszczek, as well as the teenage 
forward Ousmane Dembélé, were also on target as Dortmund gained revenge for 
their defeat toWolfsburg <https://www.theguardian.com/football/wolfsburg> in 
the final of the 2015 DFB-Pokal – Jürgen Klopp’s last game in charge.

Daniel Didavi pulled a goal back for Wolfsburg but it was not enough to deny 
Dortmund, who go top on goal difference after picking up a third win in four 
games.

Eintracht Frankfurt are also on nine points – along with Bayern Munich and 
Hertha Berlin, who face each other on Wednesday – after seeing off winless
Ingolstadt 2-0. David Abraham and Bastian Oczipka scored either side of 
half-time for Niko Kovac’s team.

Hamburg are also still waiting for their first win of the campaign after 
falling to a 1-0 defeat atFreiburg, which turns the heat up on their coach 
Bruno Labbadia.

The substitute Nils Petersen scored the only goal of the game almost 
immediately after coming on in the 69th minute, earningFreiburg 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/freiburg> their second win of the season 
and a place in the top half of the table.

In the other game on Tuesday, Darmstadt left it late to snatch a point at home 
to unbeatenHoffenheim, with Denys Oliynyk cancelling out Andrej Kramaric’s 
opener with the last kick of the game.
