
West Ham have completed the permanent signing of the Toulouse 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/toulouse> defender Herita Ilunga until 
2013, the club have announced.

The 27-year-old joined the Hammers on a season-long loan last summer but he 
will now make the move permanent for an undisclosed fee.

The Democratic Republic of Congo player has been a regular for West Ham in the 
league this season and has scored two goals in the FA Cup, against Barnsley and 
Middlesbrough.

"It is a great joy for me to have signed permanently for a club that believed 
in me and gave me the opportunity to play in thePremier League 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/premierleague>," Ilunga told the club's 
website.

"My goal is to keep developing as a player here, working for the manager and 
his staff. I am very happy to be here and there is a great atmosphere with my 
team-mates."
