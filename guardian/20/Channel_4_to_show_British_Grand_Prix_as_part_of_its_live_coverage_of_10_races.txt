
Channel 4 will show the British Grand Prix 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/british-grand-prix> live on 10 July as part 
of its three-year deal for Formula One after replacing the BBC as the UK’s 
terrestrial broadcaster for the sport.

It will show 10 races live with highlights packages of the other 11 grands 
prix, with all events to be screened in high definition. Sky Sports will show 
all 21 races live.

Channel 4 <https://www.theguardian.com/media/channel4>, which has recruited 
the former McLaren driver David Coulthard as part of its presenting team, will 
make its live debut at the season’s second race, the Bahrain Grand Prix on 3 
April.

Their next live race will be the Spanish Grand Prix on 15 May and it will also 
show the first-ever race in Azerbaijan, with the European Grand Prix in Baku 
provisionally pencilled in for 19 June.

Channel 4’s head of F1, Stephen Lyle, said: “We are delighted to announce the 
full lineup of races we will be showing live across 2016. The British Grand 
Prix is a huge highlight of the sporting calendar and we’re looking forward to 
covering all the action live from Silverstone this summer.”

Another trio of European races in Hungary, Belgium and Italy will be shown 
live followed by grands prix in Malaysia and Mexico before live coverage of the 
season finale in Abu Dhabi on 27 November.
