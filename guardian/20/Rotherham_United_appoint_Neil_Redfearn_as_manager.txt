
Rotherham <https://www.theguardian.com/football/rotherham> United have 
appointed Neil Redfearn as their new manager.

Redfearn, who has been out of work since leaving Leeds United in the summer, 
has signed a two-and-a-half year deal with the Championship club and replaces 
Steve Evans,who surprisingly left last week 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/sep/28/rotherham-united-part-company-manager-steve-evans>
. The 50-year-old beat off competition from more than 50 applicants, with his 
first game in charge coming at Brentford next weekend.

A club statement read 
<http://www.themillers.co.uk/news/article/rotherham-united-neil-redfearn-2736935.aspx>
: “Rotherham United are delighted to announce the appointment of Neil Redfearn 
as the club’s first-team manager. The former Leeds manager has penned an 
agreement until the end of the 2017-2018 season to join the Millers, and he 
will be officially unveiled at a press conference on Monday afternoon.

“Redfearn came out on top after a meticulous selection process where he 
impressed the chairman Tony Stewart and the board of directors with his vision 
for the future of the club.

The 50-year-old, who has vast experience of coaching at all levels, plus 
experience of managing atChampionship 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/championship> level, will speak about his 
new appointment for the first time on Monday.

Redfearn also enjoyed a long and illustrious playing career which included 
spells in the Premier League with Barnsley, Charlton Athletic and Bradford 
City, whilst he also enjoyed time with Crystal Palace, Oldham Athletic and 
Doncaster Rovers.”

Rotherham are one place off the relegation places having won just two matches 
this season.
