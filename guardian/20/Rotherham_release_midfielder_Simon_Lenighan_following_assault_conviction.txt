
Rotherham <https://www.theguardian.com/football/rotherham> have terminated the 
contract of midfielder Simon Lenighan after an internal investigation.

The 21-year-old was suspended by the club 
<http://www.themillers.co.uk/news/article/rotherham-united-2835011.aspx> in 
early December, just a week after he joined, when he was convicted of 
assaulting three women.

Lenighan avoided jail but was sentenced to a community order for 12 months, 
including 150 hours of unpaid work, and ordered to pay damages.

The former Leeds academy product did not make a first-team appearance during 
his time with Rotherham.

A statement on the club’s website 
<http://www.themillers.co.uk/news/article/rotherham-united-2938368.aspx> read: 
“Following an internal investigation in to the matters recently in the press 
and following the reported conduct of the player, Rotherham United can announce 
that it has terminated the contract of Simon Lenighan.

“The club will be making no further comment on this matter.”
