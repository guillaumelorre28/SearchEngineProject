
Kevin Nolan is to train with Leyton Orient following his departure from West 
Ham 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/aug/27/kevin-nolan-leaves-west-ham-mutual-consent>
.

The 33-year-old midfielder – West Ham’s captain until he left the club 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/video/2015/aug/27/slaven-bilic-thanks-kevin-nolan-west-ham-captain-leaves-club-video>
 by mutual consent just days before the conclusion of the summer transfer 
window – will remain with the League Two team for an indefinite period.

“It is great to have such a model professional in Kevin at the club, and I am 
more than happy for him to come in and train with the squad,” said the Orient 
manager, Ian Hendon, who has inspired his side tofirst place and five wins from 
their opening six league 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/leaguetwofootball/table> fixtures this 
season, told the club’s official website.

“It is good for our players to be training with a vastly experienced and 
talented Premier League midfielder.”
