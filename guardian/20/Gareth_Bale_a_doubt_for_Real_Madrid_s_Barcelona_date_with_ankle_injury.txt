
Gareth Bale <https://www.theguardian.com/football/gareth-bale> risks missing 
el clásico after suffering an ankle injury in Real Madrid’s Champions League 
win at Sporting Lisbon on Tuesday. The 27-year-old Wales forward hobbled off 
just before the hour mark in Real’s 2-1 victory in the Portuguese capital and 
medical tests have revealed damage to his peroneal tendons.

A statement published on the club’s website said Bale’s recovery “will 
continue to be assessed”, with theLa Liga 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/laligafootball> game at Barcelona – 
scheduled for 3 December – looming large.

The former Tottenham player may also miss the table-topping Champions League 
showdown with Borussia Dortmund, which comes four days after the Barcelona game.

Real Madrid’s coach, Zinédine Zidane, speaking immediately after the Lisbon 
game, said: “Gareth Bale suffered more of an ankle twist than a knock.”

Real lead La Liga by four points from Barça, who visit Real Sociedad on Sunday.
