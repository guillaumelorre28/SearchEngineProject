
A newsreader, a hobo, a primary school teacher, a construction worker: Cate 
Blanchett <https://www.theguardian.com/film/cate-blanchett> plays all these 
characters and more in the idiosyncratic Manifesto, the first full trailer for 
which has just been released online.

Manifesto is written and directed by German artist Julian Rosefeldt, and is 
due to receive its world premiere at the Sundance film festival later this 
month. Originallya multiscreen video installation 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2015/dec/09/julian-rosefeldts-manifesto-review-13-cate-blanchetts-in-search-of-a-meaning>
 at Melbourne’s Australian Centre for the Moving Image, but now reassembled as 
a 90-minute feature, Manifesto’s synopsis describes it as “an homage to the 
20th century’s most impassioned artistic statements and innovators, from 
futurists and dadaists to pop art, fluxus, Lars von Trier and Jim Jarmusch ... 
this series of reenactments performed by Cate Blanchett explores these 
declarations’ performative components and political significance.”

Blanchett reportedly plays 13 different characters in the full film, which 
could be seen as a progression from her role as one of multiple Bob Dylans in 
the Todd Haynes-directedI’m Not There 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2007/dec/21/drama>.

Manifesto screens on 23 January at the Sundance film festival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/sundancefilmfestival>.
