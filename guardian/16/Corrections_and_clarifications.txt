
• A recipe for mince pie blondies (The second coming 
<https://www.theguardian.com/lifeandstyle/2016/dec/25/christmas-leftovers-ideas-mince-pies-stilton-buns-recipes-baking-claire-ptak>
, 24 December, page 8, Cook) called for the melting and cooling of 250 grams of 
unsalted butter, but neglected to say what to do with it after that. It should 
be whisked into the egg mixture after step four.

• A photo illustrating an article about train services, which showed a crowded 
platform, was captioned: “Disruption on the Southern network left thousands of 
people with a long wait on Falmer station near Brighton.” The caption should 
have acknowledged that the crowd was largely made up of fans making their way 
home after a football match at a nearby stadium (The misery of Britain’s rail 
users 
<https://www.theguardian.com/money/2016/dec/31/rising-fares-and-crowded-trains-the-misery-of-britains-rail-users>
, 31 December, page 36).

• Aurora, the ship that fired the shot that signalled the storming of the 
Winter Palace in St Petersburg in 1917, is not a battleship as we said (Hot to 
trot 
<https://www.theguardian.com/travel/ng-interactive/2017/jan/07/where-to-go-on-holiday-in-2017-the-hot-list>
, 7 January, page 2, Travel). It is a Pallada-class cruiser. Battleships and 
cruisers are both types of warship.

• We said incorrectly that Japan had run up a budget deficit of 240% (Top five 
global risks for 2017 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2017/jan/11/inequality-world-economy-wef-brexit-donald-trump-world-economic-forum-risk-report>
, 12 January, page 23). We meant that the country’s debt to GDP ratio had 
reached that figure.
