
Liverpool’s Sadio Mané scored a 10th-minute penalty to send Senegal on their 
way to a2-0 victory over Tunisia 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/live/2017/jan/15/tunisia-v-senegal-afcon-2017-live>
 and top of Group C at the Africa Cup of Nations on Sunday.

Mané scored from the spot after Aymen Abdennour had brought down Cheikhou 
Kouyaté in the penalty area, givingSenegal 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/senegal> the lead against the run of play.

Centre-half Kara Mbodji added the second on the half-hour, heading in from a 
corner as Senegal became the first team to register a win at the tournament 
after the first three matches all ended in draws. However, the victory was far 
from convincing asTunisia <https://www.theguardian.com/football/tunisia> missed 
several chances, notably in a dominant second-half performance.

In the earlier match, Leicester City’s Riyad Mahrez scored twice, one a late 
equaliser,as Algeria were held to a 2-2 draw by Zimbabwe. 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2017/jan/15/riyad-mahrez-scores-twice-algeria-zimbabwe-africa-cup-of-nations-match-report>

Defending champions Ivory Coast begin their campaign in Group C against Togo 
on Monday, while in the same group, Democratic Republic of Congo face Morocco.
