
Milos Raonic fended off a spirited comeback from the fourth seed Stanislas 
Wawrinka to send the 2014 champion tumbling out of the fourth round of the
Australian Open <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/australian-open> 6-4, 6-3, 
5-7, 4-6, 6-3 on Monday.

The 25-year-old Canadian, who had never beaten the French Open champion in 
four previous meetings, sent down 24 aces and hit 82 winners in the three-hour, 
44-minute contest to reach the quarter-finals for the second year in a row.

The Swiss Wawrinka fought back from losing the first two sets to force a

Raonic sealed the victory with a simple volley at the net to set up a 
quarter-final against France’s Gaël Monfils.
