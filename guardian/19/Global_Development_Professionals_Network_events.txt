
The Guardian’s Global Development Professionals Network regularly hosts events 
around the world to discuss and explore key humanitarian and development issues.

This list below gives details of our past and future events.

Past events

2016

Maintaining the momentum for the SDGs
This morning seminar, hosted alongside the UN general assembly, was the second 
of our annual leadership events. We looked at how successfully the global 
community is maintaining the momentum for change following the adoption of the 
SDGs. Speakers included Julia Gillard (Global Partnership for Education), 
Rebeca Grynspan (Secretaría General Iberoamericana) and Naoko Ishii (Global 
Environment Facility).
Listen to the event’s podcast here 
<https://www.theguardian.com/global-development-professionals-network/audio/2016/nov/08/maintaining-the-momentum-sdgs-global-goals-podcast>
.

Is sustainability truly compatible with access to water and sanitation? 
A lunchtime panel discussion, hosted in association with FEMSA Foundation, 
alongside SIWI World Water Week, in Stockholm, Sweden, in August 2016.
Panellists included: Jayanthi Iyengar (Xylem Inc), Sergio Campos (IADB) and 
Bill Kingdom (World Bank).
Read the write up here 
<https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2015/sep/16/global-drought-why-is-no-one-discussing-fresh-water-at-cop21>
.

Breaking down barriers to HIV/Aids response: past, present and future
An afternoon seminar and networking reception, hosted in association with 
Gilead Sciences, alongside the International Aids Conference in Durban in July 
2016. Panellists included: Philippe Douste-Blazy (Unitaid), Fareed Abdullah 
(South African Aids Council) and Sheila Dinotshe Tlou (UNAids).
Read the write-up here 
<https://www.theguardian.com/global-development-professionals-network/2016/aug/03/aids-hiv-antiretroviral-drugs-treatment-prevention>

The next five years in global development: can we usefully look forward?
Last summer, we hosted an informal meet-up in a pub in central London. Duncan 
Green (Oxfam), Harriet Sherwood (Guardian) and Teresa Dumasy(Conciliation

upcoming SDGs and Cop21 agreements, which was followed by casual drinks and 
networking.
