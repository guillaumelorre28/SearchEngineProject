
1. No list of Nola music could be complete without Pops telling you what a 
wonderful world it is.
2. Kermit Ruffins lists the many things that define New Orleans – take notes 
while listening to this one. Bashiggitdy!
3. Keep your pen handy as the bard of Nola, Allen Toussaint, explains a simple 
way to have an exceptional lunch with a shrimp po-boy and a bottle of beer.
4. New Orleans' Radiators explain – in a funky way – the art of eating 
crawfish. Suck the head!
5. If you are not familiar with the Meters, this is one of the most sampled 
bands of all of the music that created the Nola funk sound and blazed the trail 
for many to come. This cut is from 1969.
6. Twerking is now a worldwide craze but it started here in Nola in 1993 – and 
DJ Jubilee was one of the pioneers of bounce music which gave birth to twerking.
7. This band started in 1994 as Galactic Prophylactic and quickly became one 
of the country's stalwarts of the Jam Band scene. This song takes you to the 
streets of Nola.
8. The New Orleans standard in its original version from one of the early 
hit-makers, Lee Dorsey.
9. Dr John paints a great picture and sends you a personal invitation to Nola, 
and reminds you that dreams come true in New Orleans.
10. We end the list with recent Grammy winners the Re-Birth Brass Band laying 
down our basic philosophy, "DO WHATCHA WANNA"!
