
Each pair of rhyming lines contains both a definition (one or more words) and 
a mixture or reversal of the letters (beginning at the beginning or ending at 
the end of a word in the clue) of the two answers indicated by the clue numbers 
(in the correct order).

Both indications of the first answer precede both indications of the second. 
In each case either definition or letter-mixture may come first.

Every answer is in the Chambers Dictionary (2011).
