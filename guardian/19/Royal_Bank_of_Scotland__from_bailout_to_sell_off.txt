October 2007: RBS takes control of ABN Amro after a six month £48bn bid battle 
to take control of the Dutch bank

April 2008: A £12bn cash call on investors 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/apr/18/royalbankofscotlandgroup.banking>
 as RBS attempts to bolster capital as the credit crunch bites

October 2008: Stephen Hester replaces Sir Fred Goodwin as chief executive  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2008/oct/13/rbs-stephen-hester>during the 
beginning of a rescue exercise for RBS. The bailout takes three tranches and 
eventually amounts to £45bn

December 2008: government uses £15bn to buy shares at 655p

January 2009: RBS shares plunge to record low of 103p

February 2009: RBS reports record £24bn loss 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/feb/26/rbs-record-loss> for 2008

April 2009: £5bn used to buy shares at 318p

December 2009: £25bn used to buy shares at 500p as the Asset Protection Scheme 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2009/dec/07/royal-bank-of-scotland-asset-protection>
, created to insure the bank’s riskiest assets, gets underway

February 2010: RBS reports £3.6bn loss for 2009 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2010/feb/25/rbs-3bn-loss-defends-bonuses> 
and Hester warns the upcoming year will be a “year of hard slog”

February 2011: Losses stand at £1.1bn for 2010 and speculation mounts that 
Qatar plans to buy large stakes in the bailed out banks 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2011/feb/23/qatar-wants-stakes-in-uk-bailed-out-banks>

December 2011: A report into what went wrong at RBS  
<https://www.theguardian.com/global/2011/dec/12/royal-bank-of-scotland-fsa-report>
points to “multiple poor decisions” but avoids pinning the blame on any single 
individual

January 2012: Fred Goodwin stripped of the knighthood 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2012/jan/31/fred-goodwin-stripped-of-knighthood>
 he received in June 2004.


January 2012: Hester admits he has thought about quitting as he waives £1m 
bonus 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2012/jan/29/rbs-stephen-hester-waives-bonus>

February 2012: RBS pleas to be treated as a commercial business as it reports 
£2bn loss for 2012 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2012/feb/23/rbs-commercial-status-2bn-loss>

June 2012: The bank swaps 10 old shares for one new share 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/marketforceslive/2012/jun/06/rbs-consolidation-shareprice-jump>
 - so the share price ‘rises’ from 20p to 200p overnight

June 2012: An IT failure lasts for weeks and eventually leads to a £50m fine 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/nov/19/rbs-fine-computer-chaos-2012-royal-bank-scotland>
 from the Financial Conduct Authority and the Bank of England

February 2013: RBS fined £390m for rigging Libor  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/feb/06/rbs-fined-libor-rigging-scandal>

February 2013: RBS loss back above £5bn for 2012  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/feb/28/rbs-losses-chastening-year-stephen-hester>
 but Hester says “the light at the end of the tunnel” toward privatisation is 
“coming much closer”

March 2013: Outgoing governor of the Bank of England Mervyn King calls for RBS 
to be splitinto a good and bad bank 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/mar/06/split-rbs-good-bad-banks-mervyn-king>
 and describes the current structure as “nonsense”

May 2013: International Monetary Fund calls on the government to devise a 
clear strategy for the bailed out banks 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/may/22/lloyds-banking-group-investors-fresh-capital>

June 2013: Hester quits 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/jun/12/rbs-ceo-stephen-hester-quits> 
to facilitate privatisation

August 2013: Ross McEwan named as Hester’s successor  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/aug/02/rbs-ross-mcewan-stephen-hester>

November 2013: McEwan announces that that £38bn of troublesome loans will be 
put in to a bad bank 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/nov/01/royal-bank-scotland-losses-internal-bad-bank-rbs>
 and aims to embark on a “resetting” of the relationship with the Treasury

November 2013: Allegations emerge that RBS has been deliberating forcing small 
businesses to the brink 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2013/nov/25/rbs-accused-business-profits-regulators>
 to make a profit

February 2014: Loss stands at £8.2bn 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/feb/27/rbs-bonuses-loss-pay-market-rate>
 as McEwan turns focus more on to the UK

November 2014: RBS among banks fined for rigging foreign exchange markets  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/nov/12/foreign-exchange-fines-ubs-hsbc-citibank-jp-morgan-rbs-penalties-market-rigging>

February 2015: RBS announces Sir Howard Davies will replace Sir Philip Hampton 
as chairman as it reports a £3.5bn loss, itsseven consecutive year of losses 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2015/feb/26/rbs-paid-out-421m-in-bonuses-in-2014-royal-bank-scotland>

June 2015: George Osborne decides he will sell off the stake in RBS 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2015/jun/10/george-osborne-signals-rbs-sell-off-at-mansion-house-speech>
 even though the shares are worth less than was paid for them

July 2015: RBS warns of risk of further fines  
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2015/jul/30/royal-bank-of-scotland-expects-further-fines-dividend-delay>
as it reports loss for the half year

August 2015: Government begin sell-off 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2015/aug/03/uk-government-starts-royal-bank-of-scotland-sell-off-rbs>
