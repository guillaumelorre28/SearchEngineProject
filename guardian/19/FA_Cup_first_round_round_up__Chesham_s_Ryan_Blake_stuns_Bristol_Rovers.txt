
Chesham were the most notable giant-killers in the FA Cup first round on 
Sunday as the seventh-tier side pulled off a shock 1-0 victory at League Two
Bristol Rovers <https://www.theguardian.com/football/bristolrovers>.

The visitors passed up the opportunity to open the scoring in the 29th minute 
as Dave Pearce’s penalty was saved by Lee Nicholls. Ryan Blake then struck in 
the 77th minute to secure a famous win.

Meanwhile, it was an historic afternoon for sixth-tier Whitehawk as they 
reached the second round for the first time with a thrilling 5-3 home triumph 
against the National League sideLincoln 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/lincoln>.

The hosts went 2-0 up through Danny Mills and Jake Robinson, before Matt 
Rhead’s penalty reduced the deficit.

Sam Deering and Rhead exchanged efforts to leave the score at 3-2, and Dave 
Martin extended the lead with a few minutes to go.

An eventful finale then saw Lincoln’s Rhead complete his hat-trick before 
Deering netted from the spot having been fouled by Alan Power, who was sent off.

The National League North side Brackley held higher-division opposition to a 
draw courtesy of a last-minute equaliser, Curtis McDonald’s header ensuring 
their home match against League TwoNewport County 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/newport-county> ended 2-2 after they had 
been 2-0 down.

Aldershot and Braintree, both of the National League, will face replays as 
well after drawing at home 0-0 against League One Bradford City and 1-1 with 
League Two’sOxford United <https://www.theguardian.com/football/oxford-united> 
respectively. Oxford played the last 20 minutes with 10 men following Johnny 
Mullins’ red card.
