
Serena Williams <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/serena-williams> stormed 
into the quarter-finals of the US Open in record-smashing style on Monday, 
easing past Kazakhstan’s Yaroslava Shvedova 6-2, 6-3 to claim her 308th grand 
slam win.

The world No1 needed just 68 minutes on Arthur Ashe Stadium court to overwhelm 
the 52nd-ranked Shvedova and move past Roger Federer to the top of the all-time 
grand slam victories list.

so magical out here for me but 308 sounds pretty good,” Williams said about her 
landmark win, after moving a step closer to a record seventh US Open title.

It has been another dominating run into the last eight for the 34-year-old 
American, who has yet to be broken and has faced just one break point in four 
matches while never spending more than 68 minutes on court.
