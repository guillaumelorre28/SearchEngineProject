
The Guardian has won three Webby Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards>, the leading 
international awards honouring excellence on the internet, including two 
“People’s Voice awards” which are voted for by the public.

The People’s Voice “Best Use of Video or Moving Image” was awarded to A global 
guide to the First World War 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/ng-interactive/2014/jul/23/a-global-guide-to-the-first-world-war-interactive-documentary>
, an interactive giving a brief history of the first world war through a global 
lens.

Psst! Your phone is snooping on you. What you need to know and how to stop it 
<https://www.theguardian.com/technology/video/2014/jul/16/phone-snooping-how-to-stop-video>
, won the “Online Film and Video: Technology” award.

Seven Digital Deadly Sins <http://sins.nfb.ca/#/Grid>, a collaboration with 
the National Film Board of Canada, won the award under the People’s Voice 
“Websites: Activism” category.


Read the full list of winners here <http://www.webbyawards.com/winners/2015/>.
