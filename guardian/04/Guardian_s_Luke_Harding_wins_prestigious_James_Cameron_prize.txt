
The Guardian’s foreign correspondent Luke Harding 
<https://www.theguardian.com/profile/lukeharding> has won the prestigious James 
Cameron prize 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/nov/26/guardian-luke-harding-james-cameron-prize-wikileaks-ukraine-snowden?utm_source=twitterfeed&utm_medium=twitter>
 for 2014 for his work on Russia, Ukraine, Edward Snowden and WikiLeaks.

The honour is given in memory of the celebrated foreign correspondent and 
authorJames Cameron 
<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Cameron_%28journalist%29>, who died in 1985.
Harding was the Guardian’s Moscow bureau chief from 2007 until 2011, when the 
Kremlin expelled him from the country in the first case of its kind since the 
cold war. He has reported from Afghanistan, Iraq, Libya, easternUkraine 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/ukraine> and other war zones.

He collected the award at a ceremony in London on Wednesday 26 November.
