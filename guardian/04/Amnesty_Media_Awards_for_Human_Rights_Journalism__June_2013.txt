
Guardian journalists Eric Allison and Simon Hattenstone have been honoured at 
this year's Amnesty Media Awards for Human Rights Journalism, for their article 
'A true horror story: The abuse of teenage boys in a detention centre 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2012/apr/13/abuse-teenage-boys-detention-centre-crime>
.'

The piece, which featured in the Guardian Weekend magazine, won in the 
Magazines - Newspaper Supplements category.

The winners were announced at a ceremony at the British Film Institute in 
central London on Wednesday 12 June 2013. The awards recognise excellence in 
human rights reporting and acknowledge journalism's significant contribution to 
the public's awareness and understanding of human rights issues.

Click here <http://www.amnesty.org.uk/news_details.asp?NewsID=20824> for more 
information and for a full list of winners.
