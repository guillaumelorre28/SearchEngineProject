
An even earlier example of the “old-school-tie brigade in knots over Russia” (
Letters 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/2015/nov/18/old-school-tie-brigade-in-knots-over-russia>
, 19 November) is that of Arthur Ransome, who reported the Russian revolution 
for the Daily News. With theMI6 code name S76 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/2005/mar/10/russia.books>, he shared a flat 
with the Bolshevik chief of propaganda but attracted the attention of the 
establishment by his opposition to allied intervention in the Russian civil war.

On his return to London he was detained and questioned by MI5 on suspicion of 
treason. After the war he went back to Moscow as areporter for the Manchester 
Guardian 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnmeducationcentre/archive-educational-resource-may-2012>
and, once married to Trotsky’s personal secretary Evgenia Petrovna Shepelina, 
returned to England to live a double life as Guardian correspondent and author 
of Swallows and Amazons, no doubt remaining under surveillance the while.
Austen Lynch
Garstang, Lancashire
