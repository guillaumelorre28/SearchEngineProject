
Vigils are being held across the US following days of violence in which five 
police officers werekilled in Texas 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jul/08/dallas-police-shooting-gunman-kill-white-officers>
, and two black men,Alton Sterling 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/alton-sterling-shooting> and Philando 
Castile <https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/philando-castile-shooting>, were 
killed in separate police shootings.

The most recent shootings took place at an anti-violence rally in Dallas 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2016/jul/07/dallas-shooting-police-officers-protest-alton-sterling-philando-castile>
 – one of a number being held across the country – in response to the fatal 
police shootings earlier in the week.

Dallas <https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/dallas> mayor Mike Rawlings and 
police chief David Brown emphasised that the protest was peaceful until the 
shooting occurred. Brown asked people to pray for the families who lost loved 
ones, including the five police officers shot and killed in the attack: 
“Please, we need your support to be able to protect you from men like these, 
who carried out this tragic, tragic event. Pray for these families”.
