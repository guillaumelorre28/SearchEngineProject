
Exhibition of the week

William Eggleston Portraits
Powerful and haunting 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/jul/08/made-in-memphis-william-eggleston>
 images of the American south by one of the country’s greatest photographers.
• National Portrait Gallery 
<http://www.npg.org.uk/whatson/eggleston/exhibition.php>, London, 21 July-23 
October.

Also showing

Marcus Harvey
Rancid images of Britain from the artist notorious for exhibiting a portrait 
of Myra Hindley at the Royal Academy.
• Jerwood Gallery <http://www.jerwoodgallery.org/whatson/42/inselaffe>, 
Hastings, 16 July-16 October

Daydreaming with Stanley Kubrick
Mat Collishaw and Jane and Louise Wilson are among the artists paying homage 
to one of themost visually creative 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/jul/07/stanley-kubrick-and-me-designing-clockwork-orange-poster>
 of filmmakers.
• Somerset House 
<https://www.somersethouse.org.uk/visual-arts/daydreaming-with-stanley-kubrick>
, London, until 24 August

The Body Extended
Matthew Barney and Rebecca Horn feature alongside false body parts made for 
soldiers maimed and disfigured in the first world war in an exhibition about
sculpture and prosthetics 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/jul/15/lives-and-limbs-how-prosthetics-transformed-the-art-world>
.
• Henry Moore Institute 
<https://www.henry-moore.org/hmi/exhibitions/the-body-extended-sculpture-and-prosthetics>
, Leeds, until 23 October

Terence Donovan
Relive the Swinging 60s through Donovan’s iconic photographs of Twiggy and 
Terence Stamp.
• Photographers’ Gallery 
<http://thephotographersgallery.org.uk/terence-donovan-speed-of-light->, 
London, 15 July-25 September
