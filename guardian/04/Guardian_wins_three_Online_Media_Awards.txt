
The Guardian has been awarded three awards at The Drum’s Online Media Awards 
<http://www.onlinemediaawards.net/online-media-awards/online-media-awards-2016> 
which honour the best application of talent and technology online.


The Guardian’s website <http://theguardian.com> received awards for best site 
for news-led journalism and best designed site.The Counted 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/series/counted-us-police-killings> - an 
investigation revealing the true number of people killed by law enforcement in 
the US - was the winner in the best use of crowd-sourcing or citizen journalism 
category.


The Guardian was also commended in the following three categories:


Best Media Podcast - The Guardian’s Audio Long Reads 
<https://www.theguardian.com/news/series/the-audio-long-read>


Online Editorial Campaign of the Year - Keep It In The Ground 
<https://www.theguardian.com/environment/series/keep-it-in-the-ground>

Digital Writer of the Year - Alex Hern 
<https://www.theguardian.com/profile/alex-hern>

The full list of winners can be found here 
<http://www.onlinemediaawards.net/online-media-awards/online-media-awards-2016>.
