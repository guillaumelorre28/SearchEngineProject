
The Guardian have won Innovation of the Year award at the 2014 British 
Journalism Awards for itsNSA Files: Decoded 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/interactive/2013/nov/01/snowden-nsa-files-surveillance-revelations-decoded>
 interactive.

Judges said “This feature set a new standard for interactive digital 
story-telling by a UK publication. It combines video, data and old-fashioned 
text-based journalism skills to explain the significance of Edward Snowden 
files on NSA surveillance in a more approachable and dynamic way than would 
ever be possible in print.

“The Guardian has continued to own this story by finding new ways to make it 
meaningful to people.”

The award was collected at a ceremony in London. Read the full list of winners
