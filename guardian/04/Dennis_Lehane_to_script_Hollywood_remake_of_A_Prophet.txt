
Mystic River author Dennis Lehane has been hired to write the American remake 
<http://variety.com/2013/film/news/dennis-lehane-a-prophet-sony-1200772465/> of 
Jacques Audiard's award-winning crime drama A Prophet.

Lehane, several of whose novels have been adapted successfully for the screen, 
including Gone Baby Gone and Shutter Island as well as Mystic River, has been 
taken on by Sony, as according to Variety, the material was considered to "fit 
Lehane's style".

A Prophet premiered at Cannes in 2009, winning the runner-up grand prix award. 
Co-written by Audiard, Thomas Bidegain, Abdel Raouf Dafri and Nicolas 
Peufaillit, it is the epic tale of a French-Algerian teenager who falls in with 
the Corsican mafia while in jail, and eventually becomes a top-level gangster 
in his own right. The film starred Tahar Rahim in a breakout role.

Plans for a remake were announced earller this year 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2013/jun/06/a-prophet-hollywood-remake-sony>, 
under the stewardship of Fast and Furious producer Neal Moritz. No word is 
forthcoming on any potential directors or actors, but Lehane enjoys a close 
working relationship with Ben Affleck, who directed and starred in the film of 
Gone Baby Gone, and is currently working on an adaptation of Lehane's 2012 
novel Live by Night.
