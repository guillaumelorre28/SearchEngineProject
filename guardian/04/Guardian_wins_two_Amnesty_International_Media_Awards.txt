
The Guardian won two awards at the 2014 Amnesty International Media Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards> at a ceremony held at the 
BFI in London on Tuesday 11 November.

The ‘National Newspapers’ award went to Pete Pattisson’s investigation, 
Revealed: Qatar’s World Cup ‘slaves’ 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/2013/sep/25/revealed-qatars-world-cup-slaves>
 and the ‘Digital Innovation’ award went to the team behind the interactiveThe 
shirt on your back: the human cost of the Bangladeshi garment industry 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/ng-interactive/2014/apr/bangladesh-shirt-on-your-back>
.

The awards, which recognise excellence in human rights reporting and 
acknowledge journalism’s contribution to public awareness and understanding of 
human rights issues. See more about the awardshere 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2014/nov/12/guardian-amnesty-international-media-awards-human-rights-journalism>
.
