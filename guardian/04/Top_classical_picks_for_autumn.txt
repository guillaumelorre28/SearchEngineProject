
Chailly's Beethoven Cycle 
<http://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=11815>

Riccardo Chailly <http://www.riccardochailly.com/> and Lepizig's Gewandhaus 
Orchestra 
<http://www.gewandhaus.de/gwh.site,postext,history-gewandhausorchester,artikel,244.html>
 hit London for a five-concert series that will include all nine of Beethoven's 
symphonies. It is repertoire that will suit the Gewandhaus's characteristic 
central European sound perfectly; typically, Chailly is pairing the symphonies 
with contemporary works, bySteffen Schleiermacher 
<http://www.schleiermacher-leipzig.de/>, Carlo Boccadoro 
<http://www.carloboccadoro.it/>, Colin Matthews 
<http://www.colinmatthews.net/Home.html>, Bruno Mantovani 
<http://www.brunomantovani.com/home.html>, and Friedrich Cerha 
<http://www.friedrich-cerha.com/> that are receiving their first performances 
in the UK.Barbican, London EC2 (020-7638 8891), 25-26 October and 1-3 November.

Exquisite Labyrinth: the music of Pierre Boulez 
<http://ticketing.southbankcentre.co.uk/find/festivals-series/exquisite-labyrinth-the-music-of-pierre-boulez>

Now 85, Boulez is the only surviving member of the group of avant-garde 
composers who set out to revolutionise music after the second world war. 
Curated by pianistPierre-Laurent Aimard 
<http://www.harrisonparrott.com/artist/pierre-laurent-aimard>, this weekend 
surveys his career, climaxing in a performance of his masterpiecePli Selon Pli 
<http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MyGe6tD9Vfw>, conducted by the composer himself.
Southbank Centre, London SE1 (0844 875 0073), 30 September to 2 October.

Castor and Pollux 
<http://www.eno.org/see-whats-on/productions/production-page.php?itemid=1659>

ENO's new production of Castor and Pollux is the company's first venture into 
the French baroque, and the first opera by Rameau to be staged by a major 
British house for 14 years.Allan Clayton <http://www.allanclayton.com/> and 
Roderick Williams <http://www.bach-cantatas.com/Bio/Williams-Roderick.htm> 
are the mythological twins.Coliseum, London WC2 (0871 911 0200), 24 October to 
1 December.

Takács Quartet plays Bartók 
<http://ticketing.southbankcentre.co.uk/find/music/classical/tickets/takacs-quartet-55750>

After their unforgettable Beethoven cycle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2010/may/16/takacs-quartet-review>, the 
world's greatest string quartet return to the composer with whom they made 
their name, divided across two recitals.John Innes Centre 
<http://www.jic.ac.uk/corporate/index.htm>, Norwich (01603 628319), 15-16 
October; Southbank Centre, London SE1 (0844 875 0073), 18-19 October.

Minimal: Glass at 75 (Part 1) 
<http://www.glasgowconcerthalls.com/whatson/event/109649-Minimal:-Glass-at-75-%28Part-I%29-The-Smith-Quartet-The-Glass-Quartets-1-5>

These concerts mark the start of Glasgow's ambitious three-year project to 
celebrate minimalism. Composers from Andriessen and Eno to Riley and 
Stockhausen will be featured, but the first weekend concentrates onPhilip Glass 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/philip-glass>, with concerts by Bang on a Can
 <https://www.theguardian.com/music/artist/656ec54e-d45f-4ff0-a751-e825a0f8aff8>
, theSmith Quartet <http://www.smithquartet.com/> and the Red Note 
<http://www.rednoteensemble.com/Red_Note.html> and Scottish Ensemble 
<http://www.scottishensemble.co.uk/>s. Tramway and Royal Concert Hall, Glasgow 
(0141-353 8000), 29-30 October.
