
Exhibition of the week

Tracey Emin and William Blake
Two British mavericks meet as Tracey Emin’s works, including My Bed, are mixed 
with those of the visionary romantic artist and poet who wrote The Marriage of 
Heaven and Hell.
• Tate Liverpool 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/visit/tate-liverpool/display/tracey-emin-and-william-blake-focus>
, Liverpool, 16 September to September 2017.

Also showing

Wifredo Lam
Powerful paintings by the Cuban surrealist who mixed European avant garde art 
with Caribbean culture.
• Tate Modern 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/whats-on/tate-modern/exhibition/ey-exhibition-wifredo-lam>
, London, 14 Septmber to 8 January.

Michelangelo Pistoletto
Mirrors, utopianism and the legacy of Italy’s arte povera movement collide 
with the baroque splendour of one of Britain’s grandest houses.
• Blenheim Palace 
<http://www.blenheimpalace.com/attractions-and-events/events/2016/autumn/michelangelo-pistoletto-at-blenheim-palace.html>
, Oxfordshire, 15 September to 31 December.

Richard Serra
Bleak, difficult and among the best art of our time, the drawings of Richard 
Serra offer no refuge from darkness.
• Davies Street 
<http://www.gagosian.com/exhibitions/richard-serra--september-15-2016?__v%3Afile=bf316de7ee4274f0dca174fda141c769>
, London, 15 September to 17 December.

Robert Motherwell
A survey of this major American modernist who meditated on the meaning of 
abstract art.
• Bernard Jacobson Gallery 
<http://www.jacobsongallery.com/index.php?nav=exhibitiondetails&ID=157>, 
London, 15 September to 26 November.

Masterpiece of the week
 A Woman Bathing in a Stream (1654) by Rembrandt. Photograph: The National 
Gallery, London 
The nude is one of the dominant themes of western art but here Rembrandt turns 
it into something more intimate and loving – a tender part-naked portrait of 
his partner Hendrickje Stoffels.
• National Gallery 
<https://www.nationalgallery.org.uk/paintings/rembrandt-a-woman-bathing-in-a-stream-hendrickje-stoffels>
, London
