
Exhibition of the week

Guerrilla Girls
The veteran cultural activists present disturbing data on the continuing male 
dominance of art, in a pair of London shows.
• Whitechapel Gallery 
<http://www.whitechapelgallery.org/exhibitions/guerrilla-girls/>, London, 1 
October–5 March;Tate Modern 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/whats-on/tate-modern/workshop/tate-exchange/complaints-department>
, London, 4–9 October.

Also showing

Richard Serra
Mighty masterpieces in steel by today’s heir to the Abstract Expressionists.
• Gagosian Britannia Street 
<https://www.gagosian.com/artists/richard-serra/artist-exhibitions>, London 
WC1X, 1 October–25 February.

Louise Bourgeois
Late works on paper by the woman who brought surrealism screaming its 
nightmares into the 21st century.
• Hauser and Wirth Somerset 
<http://www.hauserwirth.com/exhibitions/2927/louise-bourgeois-turning-inwards/view/>
, Bruton, from 2 October–1 January.

Philippe Parreno
Latest large-scale intervention in the Tate’s Turbine Hall, by the French 
artist who co-directed Zidane: A 21st-Century Portrait.
• Tate Modern 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/whats-on/tate-modern/exhibition/hyundai-commission/hyundai-commission-philippe-parreno>
, London, 4 October–2 April.

Towards Night
Nocturnally themed art by Peter Doig, JMW Turner, William Blake and many more; 
perfect as the nights get longer.
• Towner Art Gallery 
<http://www.townereastbourne.org.uk/exhibition/towards-night/>, Eastbourne, 
until 22nd January.

Modelling herself after a portrait by Rubens 
<https://www.nationalgallery.org.uk/paintings/peter-paul-rubens-portrait-of-susanna-lunden-le-chapeau-de-paille>

attributes of 18th-century beauty – but she’s holding the palette, she’s in 
control.
