
The Guardian has been honoured with three awards at this year’s Association 
for International Broadcasting (AIB) awards for factual television, radio and 
online productions.

The AIB is a non-profit, non-government, industry association founded in 1993 
and its awards reward factual programme makers from across the world.


The Guardian’s first virtual reality experience, ‘6x9: A virtual experience of 
solitary confinement 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/ng-interactive/2016/apr/27/6x9-a-virtual-experience-of-solitary-confinement>
’ was the winner in the online category.


‘Brussels divided: Molenbeek after the terrorist attacks’ 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/video/2016/apr/01/brussels-divided-molenbeek-after-the-terrorist-attacks-video>
 came top in the short form journalism category.


‘The martial arts fighter with Down’s syndrome battling for the right to fight’
 
<https://www.theguardian.com/society/video/2015/oct/12/martial-arts-fighter-downs-syndrome-right-fight-video>
 was highly commended by judges in the short feature category.


The full list of winners can be found here
