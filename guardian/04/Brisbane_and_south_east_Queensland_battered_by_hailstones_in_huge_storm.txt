Severe thunderstorms were also forecast for parts of NSW as firefighters got 
the upper hand on several bushfires.


The Bureau of Meteorlogy warned of high winds, large hailstones and heavy 
rain, with possible flash flooding, in the Northern Rivers, Northern Tablelands 
and along the north and mid-north coast.

Towns likely to be affected included Grafton, Coffs Harbour and Armidale.

Severe weather was also expected further west over towns such as Dubbo, 
Coonabarabran, Coonamble and Gilgandra.

Dozens of bush and grass fires 
<https://www.theguardian.com/australia-news/2016/nov/07/nsw-bushfires-blaze-burns-out-of-control-in-hunter-as-conditions-worsen>
 contined to burn across NSW.

Rural Fire Service crews were working to contain blazes, including one at Port 
Stephens which had burnt more than 8,000 hectares by Tuesday afternoon.
