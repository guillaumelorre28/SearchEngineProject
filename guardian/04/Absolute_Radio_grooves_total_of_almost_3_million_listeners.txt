
Absolute Radio's total audience came within a whisker of 3 million listeners 
on the back of itsnew 60s and 70s digital stations 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2011/oct/18/absolute-radio-60s-70s?INTCMP=SRCH>
 – but there was a big drop for its noughties-themed offering.

The Absolute Radio group of stations had a total average weekly audience of 
2.98 million, with its mainAbsolute Radio 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/absolute-radio> station up 15.6% year on 
year to 1.61 million in the first three months of 2012, according to the latest 
official Rajar statistics published on Thursday.

Absolute Radio 60s debuted with 151,000, with Absolute Radio 70s launching 
with 157,000.

Absolute Radio 80s remains the broadcaster's biggest digital offering with 
857,000 listeners, up 37.3% year on year. But Absolute Radio Noughties was down 
50.6% on 2011 to just 84,000.

Among the other national commercial stations, Global Radio's Classic FM lost 
10.5% of its audience year on year – but was marginally up on the previous 
quarter – to 5.44 million.

The station, which received a special prize at the Sony Radio Academy awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2012/may/14/richard-keys-andy-gray-sony-awards?INTCMP=SRCH>
 this week, now has nearly 600,000 fewer listeners than it did in the first 
three months of 2011.

UTV <https://www.theguardian.com/media/utv> Media's TalkSport was up on the 
previous quarter but down year on year – with not much movement in either 
direction – to 3.23 million.

GMG Radio's Smooth Radio had a nationwide audience of 3.32 million listeners, 
marginally up on the previous quarter but 7.6% higher year on year. GMG Radio 
parent company Guardian Media Group also publishes MediaGuardian.

Among the national digital stations, there were double-digit year-on-year 
losses at Bauer Media's Q, which fell 26.2% to 214,000.

Global Radio's Xfm network also suffered double-digit losses, down 13.6% on 
the previous year to 809,000.

Bauer's Smash Hits was also down year on year, 9.4% to 991,000. But there was 
better news at sister station Heat, up 14.4% to 716,000, while sister station 
The Hits climbed back above the 1 million mark with 1.08 million listeners.

Jazz FM was another station with double digit year on year gains – nothing to 
do with this we are sure 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2012/feb/20/jazz-fm-gay-porn-gaffe> – up 
13.9% to 564,000, while Planet Rock had 861,000 listeners, up 8% on the 
previous year.

Global Radio's Heart remained the biggest national commercial network with 
7.48 million listeners – up 0.7% year on year – ahead of sister group Capital 
with 7.05 million, up 5% on 2011.

Bauer Radio's Kiss had a UK audience of 4.36 million, up 7.2% year on year, 
but sister network Magic was down 5.6% on 2011 with 3.61 million listeners.
