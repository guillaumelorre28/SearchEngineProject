
Ricardo Amunjera and Marc Themba are credited with being Namibia's first gay 
married couple. Thoughhomosexuality is criminalised 
<http://www.globalequality.org/component/content/article/166> in Namibia, the 
pair tied the knot in South Africa and returned to Namibia to become prominent
LGBT rights campaigners <https://www.theguardian.com/world/lgbt-rights>.

Amunjera, 31, says he believes "the best way you can help somebody is by 
telling them your story". His activism has earned him the title ofMr Gay 
Namibia 2013 
<http://www.gaystarnews.com/article/mr-gay-namibia-proudly-marries-his-husband-botswana150413>
 and is he is the process of setting local support group in his home town, 
joining a countrywide support network for LGBT. His work is not without its 
risks; another winner of the Mr Gay Namibia title,Wendelinus Hamutenya, was 
attacked as he walked home 
<http://www.advocate.com/news/daily-news/2011/12/12/mr-gay-namibia-assaulted> 
days after winning the competition. According to the rights groupOutRight 
Namibia <http://www.osisa.org/lgbti/namibia/outright-namibia>, LGBT people in 
Namibia encounter homophobia and transphobia on a daily basis.

In a film about their lives 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/video/2014/may/16/namibia-first-gay-married-couple-video>
, made by theInternational HIV/Aids Alliance <http://www.aidsalliance.org> and 
Positive Vibes <http://www.positivevibes.org/who_we_are.html>, the couple 
discuss their activism, their religious beliefs and their experience of 
conducting a loving relationship in a country where being gay is against the 
law. It also shows Amunjera fielding calls and texts, and using social media, 
to answer questions and comments from people seeking support around the country.

Amunjera and Themba will join the Guardian Africa Network for a live Q&A from 
1pm - 2pm BST on Tuesday 20 May, to answer your questions about living as an 
openly gay activist inNamibia <https://www.theguardian.com/world/namibia>. What 
would you like to ask? Post your questions in the comments below.
