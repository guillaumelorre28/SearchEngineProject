
The Guardian has been awarded best use of online video for ‘Building the Bomb 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2015/sep/21/building-the-atom-bomb-the-full-story-of-the-nevada-test-site>
’ (the story of the US’s development of nuclear weapons created in 
collaboration with ITV) at the World Association of Newspapers and News 
Publishers’ (WAN-IFRA <http://www.wan-ifra.org/>) European Digital Media Awards 
2016. Judges of the awards, which reward the best in digital innovation from 
publishers across Europe, were impressed by the way ‘Building the Bomb’ not 
only mixed visuals and text but “the great interweaving of historic and 
contemporary vision.”


The Guardian was also highly commended for its website, theguardian.com, which 
was awarded best news website in last year’s awards. . Judges described its 
content as “stunning examples of intelligent, responsive, audience-centred 
design” and singled out“The Counted” 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2015/jun/01/about-the-counted>
 for its “high impact visual journalism.”
