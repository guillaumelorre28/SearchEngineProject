
The Observer's film critic Philip French and the Guardian's theatre critic 
Michael Billington have received OBEs in theNew Years Honours List 2013 
<http://www.cabinetoffice.gov.uk/news/new-year-honours-list-2013> for their 
services to film and theatre.

Michael Billington <https://www.theguardian.com/profile/michaelbillington> has 
been the Guardian's theatre critic since 1971 and is also the longest standing 
member of the Critics' Circle. He is also the author of several books including 
'State of the Nation: British Theatre since 1945.'

Philip French <https://www.theguardian.com/profile/philipfrench?INTCMP=SRCH> 
has been The Observer's film critic for over 50 years and is only one of three 
British film critics to have sat on the jury of the Cannes film festival. Over 
his career he has also received a lifetime achievement award from the Critics' 
Circle and is an honorary member of Bafta.Click here 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2012/dec/29/michael-billington-philip-french-honours>
 for more details.
