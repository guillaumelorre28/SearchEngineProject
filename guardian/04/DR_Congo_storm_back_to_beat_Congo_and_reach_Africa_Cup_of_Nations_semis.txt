
Dieumerci Mbokani scored twice as Democratic Republic of Congo came back from 
2-0 down midway through the second half to beat Congo 4-2 in a thrillingAfrica 
Cup of Nations <https://www.theguardian.com/football/africannationscup> 
quarter-final at Estadio de Bata.

DR Congo completed a remarkable turnaround as six goals were scored in the 
second half.

Congo went 2-0 up with goals from Férébory Doré and Thievy Bifouma in a 
seven-minute spell but DR Congo came back to score through Mbokani twice, 
Jeremy Bokila and Joël Kimwaki. Mbokani’s first goal was created by Crystal 
Palace’s Yannick Bolasie.

They will meet the Ivory Coast or Algeria in Bata in Wednesday’s semi-final.
