
The Guardian's editor-in-chief Alan Rusbridger and Guardian journalist Nick 
Davies have been honoured with The Media Society Award 2012 for their 
"revelations and coverage of the phone hacking story" at a ceremony at the 
Corinthia Hotel in London.

Announcing the award, The Media Society said: "The Guardian's revelations and 
coverage of the phone hacking story has not only brought an issue of great 
importance to the attention of the public, but led to a major debate about the 
practices and role of our press."

The annual Media Society Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards>, now in their 18th year, 
honours individuals from across the media for their outstanding contribution to 
their industry and profession.
