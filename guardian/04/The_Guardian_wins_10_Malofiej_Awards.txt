
The Guardian’s interactive and visual teams have won ten awards at this year’s 
Malofiej Awards <http://www.malofiejgraphics.com/awards/> held at a ceremony in 
Pamplona, Spain <http://www.malofiejgraphics.com/awards/>.

The Malofiej Awards <https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards>, 
which are named after Argentinian cartographer Alejandro Malofiej, honour the 
best in data visualization, information graphics and visual storytelling from 
around the world.


The Guardian’s Homan square interactive 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/ng-interactive/2015/oct/19/homan-square-chicago-police-detainees>
 won gold in the features category, whilst a graphic onaffordable housing 
<https://www.theguardian.com/society/ng-interactive/2015/sep/02/unaffordable-country-where-can-you-afford-to-buy-a-house>
 took home the Miguel Urabayen Award for best online map in the competition.
