
The Guardian has been named 'Internet Hero' at the 2014 ISPA awards for its 
groundbreaking coverage of the Snowden revelations. The award, which 
acknowledged 'excellent reporting of mass surveillance programmes', was 
collected by Guardian special projects editor James Ball at a ceremony in 
London.

The ISPAs, hosted by The Internet Services Providers' Association (ISPA UK), 
are the UK's longest running internet industry awards. Read the full press 
releasehere
