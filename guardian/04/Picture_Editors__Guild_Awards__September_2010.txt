
The Guardian's Martin Argles has been named Photographer of the Year at the 
inauguralPicture Editors' Guild Awards <http://www.piced.net/index.html> for a 
photo essay 
<https://www.theguardian.com/politics/gallery/2010/may/12/gordon-brown-labourleadership>
 documenting former prime minister Gordon Brown during the 2010 election 
campaign.

The Picture Editors' Guild Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards> was set up to reward the 
very best in picture journalism.
