
Kate Hodal, the Guardian's south-east Asia correspondent, has won at the One 
World MediaAwards 2013 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards-2013>.

Kate's report 'Death Metal 
<https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2012/nov/23/tin-mining-indonesia-bangka>
', which exposed the risks of tin mining in Indonesia, won the press award, 
with judges commenting that it demonstrated "investigative journalism at its 
best."

The One World Media Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards> recognise the unique role 
of journalists and filmmakers in bridging the divide between different 
societies and raising awareness of vital development issues.

Click here <http://oneworldmedia.org.uk/awards/winners/> for more information 
about the awards and to see a full list of winners.
