 Namvula Rennie … cool and engaging 
Namvula Rennie is a singer-songwriter whose cool, engaging style reflects her 
background. She was born in Zambia to a Zambian mum and Scottish dad, and her 
songs mix African influences and soulful balladry with jazz. Rennie plays 
acoustic guitar and her classy backing band includes African musicians and 
members of the London-based jazz outfit Led Bib.

 * Mac <http://macbirmingham.co.uk/event/making-tracks-namvula/>, Birmingham, 
25 September (0121-446 3232). Then touring until 17 October 
Seckou Keita 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/may/21/seckou-keita-22-strings-review>

Born in Senegal but now based in Nottingham, Seckou Keita is an adventurous 
master of the kora. He has played flamenco, jazz-funk and African styles, and 
is best-known for his album of duets with the Welsh harpist Catrin Finch. All 
these influences can be heard in his elegant solo album22 Strings 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/may/21/seckou-keita-22-strings-review>, 
released earlier this year and now followed by a lengthy solo tour.

 * Ropetackle Arts Centre 
<http://ropetacklecentre.co.uk/events/african-night-fever/>, Shoreham-by-Sea 
(01273 464440), 26 September.Then touring <http://www.seckoukeita.com/live/> 
until 5 December

Sain Zahoor <https://www.facebook.com/SainZahoorAhmadOfficial>

Zahoor is a remarkable Sufi mystic singer from Pakistan who spent much of his 
life travelling across the country, living and performing in shrines. For the 
past 14 years, he’s also performed on the international circuit, accompanying 
himself on the three-stringed ektara and singing, as a fan once put it, songs 
of “spiritual love that make you dance”. He performs this autumn alongside the 
celebrated band Faiz Ali Faiz, which playsqawwali, or devotional music.

 * Transcender festival 
<http://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=11124>, Barbican, London 
(020-7638 8891), 27 September.Then touring 
<https://www.ents24.com/uk/tour-dates/sain-zahoor> until 5 October

 Think you hate banjo? You haven’t heard Béla Fleck & Abigail Washburn 
Béla Fleck and Abigail Washburn 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/oct/23/bela-fleck-abigail-washburn-review-banjo>

Béla Fleck is surely the finest and most adventurous banjo player on the 
planet, capable of switching from classical pieces to traditional-influenced 
songs and African styles, while his fellow banjo virtuoso and wife Abigail 
Washburn adds in Appalachian or Chinese influences. They are worth checking out 
even if you think you hate the banjo.

 * Howard Assembly Room 
<http://www.operanorth.co.uk/productions/bela-fleck-and-abigail-washburn>, 
Leeds (0844-848 2700), 13 November.Then touring <http://belafleck.com/shows/> 
until 16 November

Ewan MacColl Tributes

Ewan MacColl 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2015/jan/25/ewan-maccoll-godfather-folk-adored-and-feared>
 was born 100 years ago last January, but celebrations of his life and 
memorable songwriting continue. On 30 October, Cooking Vinyl releaseJoy of 
Living <http://cookingvinyl.com/joy-of-living-a-tribute-to-ewan-maccoll/>, a 
double album of his work performed by a cast including Martin Carthy, Jarvis 
Cocker and Martha and Rufus Wainwright. This is followed byBlood and Roses 
<http://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=17969>, a concert at the 
Barbican, in London, that features MacColl’s sons Neill and Calum and his wife, 
Peggy Seeger, along with a lineup that includes Norma Waterson, Martin and 
Eliza Carthy, and Rachel and Becky Unthank.

 * Barbican <http://www.barbican.org.uk/music/event-detail.asp?ID=17969>, 
London (020-7638 8891), 9 November

Scarborough jazz festival <http://jazz.scarboroughspa.co.uk/>


This year the excellent Scarborough jazz festival has an impressive lineup,

Snarky Puppy 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/may/08/snarky-puppy-scala-london-review>

This Brooklyn collective, led by bassist Mike League, have built up such a 
grassroots following among fans of jazz, funk and fusion that they’ve 
transcended their cult status. This impressively ambitious big-venue tour of 
the UK proves that they’ve become one of the biggest live draws in 
international jazz.

Matthew Halsall and the Gondwana Orchestra 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2014/may/29/matthew-halsall-gondwana-orchestra-when-the-world-was-one-review>

vibe of Alice Coltrane’s astral jazz for this propulsive project, featuring 
Rachel Gladwin on harp. Impressive new album, Into Forever, features a host of

Esperanza Spalding 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/2012/may/30/esperanza-spalding-review>

The bass-playing American jazz singer Esperanza Spalding has no new album and 
only one UK date on a whistle-stop, seven-nation European tour. Spalding’s 
latest project, a Kate Bush-style theatrical, multimedia song cycle of “live 
musical vignettes”, is entitled Emily’s D+Evolution and explores childhood and 
imagination. It has earned ecstatic notices in the US and arrives in London 
this November.

 * Shepherd’s Bush Empire 
<http://www.o2shepherdsbushempire.co.uk/event/108948/esperanza-spalding-emily-s-d-evolution-tickets>
, London (0844-477 2000), 2 November
