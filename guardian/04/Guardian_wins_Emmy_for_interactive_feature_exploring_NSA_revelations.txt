
The Guardian has won an Emmy for NSA Files: Decoded 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/interactive/2013/nov/01/snowden-nsa-files-surveillance-revelations-decoded>
, an interactive multimedia feature which takes users through the facts and 
implications of the NSA’s mass surveillance programme, which was uncovered by 
the Guardian in 2013.
The feature won the award in the new approaches: current news category at a 
ceremony in New York, with former Guardian US editor, Janine Gibson, collecting 
the award.
The NSA: Files Decoded project was led by the Guardian US’s former 
interactives editor and reporter Gabriel Dance, Guardian journalist Ewen 
MacAskill and producers Fielding Cage and Greg Chen. It offers audiences a 
comprehensive picture of the story, featuring video interviews and discussions 
with experts and key players, from senators to NSA employees, and innovative 
interactive features explaining the underlying data and context.
