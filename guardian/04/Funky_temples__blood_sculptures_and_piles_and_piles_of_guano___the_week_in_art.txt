
Exhibition of the week

Dinh Q Lê – The Colony
Piles and piles of poo accumulate in the Vietnamese artist’s latest work, but 
don’t worry, it’s a video piece, whichfocuses on the historic trade in guano 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/feb/02/dinh-q-le-the-colony-review-rich-messy-melange-history-technology>
 – the fertile faeces of the Peruvian booby birds, which colonise the remote 
Chincha islands off Peru’s south-west coast. Artangel’s commissionpremiered 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2016/feb/02/dinh-q-le-the-colony-review-rich-messy-melange-history-technology>
 at Birmingham’s Ikon gallery earlier this year. Now Londoners can get a whiff 
too.
• 133 Rye Lane <https://www.artangel.org.uk/project/the-colony/>, London, 25 
August-9 October.

Also showing

Jo Spence
Superb survey of the late photographer including self-portraiture dating from 
the last decade of her life. Spence illuminated the personal in the political 
and,in her final work 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/gallery/2016/feb/16/photographer-jo-spence-the-final-project>
, documented her own road to death.
• Stills gallery <http://edinburghartfestival.com/whats-on/detail/jo-spence>, 
Edinburgh, until 16 October.

Constellations
The constellations in question are the chains of influence and inspiration 
between British and American artists. The result: a true “galaxy of modern art 
history” 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2016/aug/19/five-of-the-best-art-exhibitions>
. 
• Tate Liverpool 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/visit/tate-liverpool/display/constellations>, 
Liverpool, ongoing.

Unseen: London, Paris, New York, 1930s-60s
Your last chance to see three great cities 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/gallery/2016/may/23/unseen-london-paris-new-york-in-pictures>
 of the modern world documented by three photographers: Wolfgang Suschitzky, 
Dorothy Bohm and Neil Libbert.
• Ben Uri Gallery <http://benuri.org.uk/exhibitions/unseen/>, London, until 28 
Aug
