
Exhibition of the week

George Shaw
Humbrol model aircraft paints take on the masters of oil painting as Turner 
nominee Shaw shows off the results of his National Gallery residency.
• National Gallery 
<https://www.nationalgallery.org.uk/whats-on/exhibitions/george-shaw-my-back-to-nature-11-may-2016-1000>
, London, 11 May–30 October.

Also showing

Painting With Light
The story of how photography inspired or destroyed – depending on your 
viewpoint – British art in the Victorian age.
• Tate Britain 
<http://www.tate.org.uk/whats-on/tate-britain/exhibition/painting-light>, 
London, 11 May-25 September.

The British Landscape Tradition
As summer starts (fingers crossed) here comes an artistic outing in painted 
meadows and glades, to look at British pastorals from the 18th century to the 
surrealist age.
• Pallant House Gallery 
<http://pallant.org.uk/exhibitions/forthcoming-exhibitions/delonghi-print-room/the-british-landscape-tradition-from-gain>
, Chichester, 11 May-26 June.

Bert Hardy
Striking black and white prints by this Picture Post photographer, who covered 
D-day and bore witness at Bergen-Belsen.
• Photographers’ Gallery 
<http://thephotographersgallery.org.uk/bert-hardy-personal-collection>, London, 
13 May-3 July.

Master Strokes 
Free display of Dutch and Flemish drawings from the likes of Rubens, Van Dyck 
and Rembrandt, no less. Let’s hope my daughter is allowed to sketch them.
• V&A Museum 
<https://shop.vam.ac.uk/whatson/index/view/id/1189/event/Master-Strokes--Dutch-and-Flemish-Drawings-from-the-Golden-Age/dt/2016-05-14/eType/1/free/1>
, London, 14 May-13 November.
