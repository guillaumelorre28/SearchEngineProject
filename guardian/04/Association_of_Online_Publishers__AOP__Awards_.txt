
The Guardian has won several prizes at this year's AOP Digital Publishing 
Awards <https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards>, which were 
announced at a ceremony on Thursday 6 June.

The Guardian's Audience department, in collaboration with GuardianMedia, won 
the 'Research and Insight Project' award for their planning toolAudiences not 
Platforms 
<https://www.theguardian.com/advertising/video/audiences-not-platforms-cross-platform-media-planning-with-the-guardian-video>
.

The Guardian Sport team won the 'Editorial Team 2013 - Consumer' prize for 
Olympics 2012 <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/olympics-2012>.

The Association of Online Publishers Awards (AOP) recognises the highest 
quality of work across digital publishing, looking at innovative products 
across a range of platforms so it's a fantastic achievement.

Click here <http://www.ukaop.org.uk/news/aopawardwinners4158.html> for a full 
list of winners.
