
Arlene Phillips <http://www.arlenephillips.com>, 67, was born in Manchester. 
In the 70s, she created the dance groupHot Gossip 
<http://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=hot+gossip&aq=f> and went on to 
become a world-renowned choreographer for theatre, film, pop concerts, music 
videos and adverts. Last year, she was controversially dropped from theStrictly 
Come Dancing <http://www.bbc.co.uk/strictlycomedancing/> judging panel. She has 
just published two books for children and choreographed theFlashdance 
<http://www.shaftesbury-theatre.co.uk/> musical, which runs at the Shaftesbury 
Theatre, London, until February next year.

When were you happiest?
Each time I gave birth.

What is your greatest fear?
Flying – it doesn't feel safe.

What is your earliest memory?
Trying to do step-together-step-hop when I was three years old at ballet 
class, and the teacher shouting at me for getting it wrong.

Which living person do you most admire, and why?
Carlos Acosta, for his incredible achievement in dance.

What is the trait you most deplore in yourself?
Tidying up everything before anyone has finished with it.

What is the trait you most deplore in others?
Leaving everything messy.

Property aside, what's the most expensive thing you've bought?
Both my daughters' education.

What is your most treasured possession?
An old biscuit tin with a few treasured possessions that belonged to my mother.

What do you most dislike about your appearance?
I'm short, stocky and have a big bum.

If you could go back in time, where would you go?
Hollywood, under the tutelage of Busby Berkeley 
<http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Busby_Berkeley>.

If you could bring something extinct back to life, what would you choose?
Unicorns, if only they had existed.

Who would play you in the film of your life?
Tamzin Outhwaite <http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0653666/>, because she really can 
dance.

What would be your fancy dress costume of choice?
Anything that enhances my boobs, nips in at the waist and covers my arms to 
the elbows.

What is the worst thing anyone's said to you?
"I hate you, Mummy!"

What is your guiltiest pleasure?
Getting into bed surrounded by my books, magazines, notebooks, open computer 
and watching Miss Marple.

Which living person do you most despise, and why?
He's Irish – I don't want to say more.

Who would you invite to your dream dinner party?
Trevor Nunn for his wisdom, Julian Clary for entertainment, Fred Astaire to 
tell me how he did it and Marilyn Monroe to tell me what really happened.

What is the worst job you've done?
Folding old army greatcoats covered in mothballs to send off to Kensington 
Market in the 70s.

What has been your biggest disappointment?
I'm still not ready to talk about it.

When did you last cry, and why?
Watching the news and looking at the pain in children's eyes caused by the 
flooding in Pakistan. 

What is the closest you've come to death?
When a friend encouraged me to swim out to an island by telling me it was 
closer than it actually was.

What do you consider your greatest achievement?
The odd, sad little girl from Manchester turned out to be Arlene Phillips.
