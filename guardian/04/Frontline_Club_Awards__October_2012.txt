
Guardian journalist Ghaith Abdul-Ahad has been honoured at the Frontline Club 
Awards 2012, winning first prize in the Print category for his pieceInside 
Syria: rebels and regime trapped in cycle of destruction. 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/2012/jul/24/inside-syria-rebels-regime-destruction>

The judges described Ghaith's piece as "incredibly powerful" and said, "Ghaith 
consistently takes us to the very edge, in a way that nobody else does."

The Frontline Club Awards 
<http://www.frontlineclub.com/events/2012/10/the-frontline-club-awards-2012.html>
 recognise the most outstanding journalism seen over a one year period and 
reward independence of spirit, integrity and courage over the four categories 
of print, broadcast, photojournalism and tribute.

This year's judges were: Jon Lee Anderson, The New Yorker; VII 
photojournalists Gary Knight and Seamus Murphy; Jeremy Bowen and Allan Little, 
BBC; Carlotta Gall,The New York Times; Patrick Cockburn, The Independent and 
Frontline Club founder Vaughan Smith.

Full details about the winners and the awards can be found here
