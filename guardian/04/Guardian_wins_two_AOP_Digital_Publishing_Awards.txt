
The Guardian has been honoured with two Association of Online Publishers (AOP) 
Digital Publishing Awards 
<http://www.ukaop.org/aop-news/aop-digital-publishing-awards-2016---winners-announced>
 which benchmark and champion excellence in online publishing.

The Guardian took home the best use of video award 
<http://www.ukaop.org/digital-publishing-award-2016-winners/best-use-of-video-2016>
 for its ‘dab - bit of news now and then 
<https://www.facebook.com/theguardian/videos/vl.757358927730826/531097203744714/?type=1&theater>
’ video series - the publication’s first specifically designed for Facebook 
mobile - andWe walk together 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/video/2015/sep/10/we-walk-together-a-syrian-familys-journey-to-the-heart-of-europe-video>
, which follows a Syrian family’s journey to the heart of Europe.

The Counted 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/series/counted-us-police-killings>, the 
ongoing Guardian US series which exposes and documents the extent of deadly 
force used by law enforcement in the United States, picked up the award forbest 
use of social media 
<http://www.ukaop.org/digital-publishing-award-2016-winners/best-use-of-social-media-2016>
.

A full list of winners can be found here 
<https://www.ukaop.org/news/aop-digital-publishing-awards-2016---winners-announced>
.
