
The Guardian's 'Three Little Pigs' television advert - created by Bartle Bogle 
Hegarty (BBH) and directed by Ringan Ledwidge - has won a prestigious'yellow 
pencil' D&AD design award 
<http://www.dandad.org/awards/professional/2013/categories/tvca/tv-cinema-advertising/02151/three-little-pigs>
 for TV & Cinema Advertising.

The D&AD Professional Awards 
<http://www.dandad.org/index.php/awards/professional/2013/about> celebrate and 
nurture outstanding work in design and advertising. The Yellow Pencil is 
recognised the world over as a symbol of the very highest creative achievement.

This recognition for Three Little Pigs follows on from previous accolades 
including:The Grand CLIO award 
<https://www.theguardian.com/guardian-us-press-office/guardian-three-little-pigs-ad-wins-grand-clio-2013>
,The Eurobest Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/guardian-advert-wins-at-eurobest-awards-2012>
,Business Insider's 10 Best Ads of 2012 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/guardian-advert-named-number-one-in-business-insiders-ten-best-ads-2012>
,The British Arrow Craft Awards 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards-awards-2012>, TED: Ads 
Worth Spreading 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/guardian-three-little-pigs-advert-named-in-ted-ads-worth-spreading-list-2013>
 andThe Adweek Awards 2012 
<https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/guardian-wins-at-adweek-awards-2012>
.
