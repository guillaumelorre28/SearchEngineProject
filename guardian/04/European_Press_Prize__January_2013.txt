
Paul Lewis, special projects editor 
<https://www.theguardian.com/profile/paullewis> at the Guardian, has won a 
European Press Prize for 'Reading the Riots 
<https://www.theguardian.com/uk/series/reading-the-riots>.'

His year-long research project into the causes of the 2011 summer riots, which 
was run jointly with the London School of Economics, won in the 'Innovation' 
category and was commended on its unique approach to investigative journalism.

The innovation prize is awarded to a journalist or editor who has influenced 
the future of journalism through an innovative idea.Click here 
<http://www.europeanpressprize.com/> for more information about the awards and 
to see a full list of winners.
