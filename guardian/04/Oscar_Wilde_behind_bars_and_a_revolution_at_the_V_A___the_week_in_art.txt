
Exhibition of the week

Inside: Artists and Writers in Reading Prison
Oscar Wilde’s incarceration in Reading jail after falling foul of the Marquess 
of Queensberry is remembered by artists and writers including Steve McQueen and 
Colm Tóibín at the prison itself.
• HM Prison Reading <https://www.artangel.org.uk/project/inside/>, until 30 
October.

Also showing

You Say You Want a Revolution?
The age of the Doors and the Rolling Stones’ Gimme Shelter is brought to life 
in this exhibition aboutthe revolutionary pop culture of the late 1960s 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2016/aug/21/san-francisco-exhibition-victoria-albert-revolution-silicon-valley>
.
• V&A 
<https://www.vam.ac.uk/exhibitions/you-say-you-want-a-revolution-records-and-rebels-1966-70>
, London, 10 September until 26 February.

Giuseppe Penone
This leading figure of Italy’s arte povera movement 
<https://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/jonathanjonesblog/2012/sep/03/giuseppe-penone-arte-povera-whitechapel-gallery>
 in the 1960s is still making ecologically sensitive art for the 21st century.
• Marian Goodman Gallery <http://www.mariangoodman.com/upcoming-exhibitions>, 
London, 8 September until 22 October.

Nicolas Party
Very surreal cat paintings – I kid you not – are the latest from Glasgow’s 
radical art scene.
• Modern Institute 
<https://www.themoderninstitute.com/exhibitions/2-the-modern-institute-3-airds-lane2016-09-10/5655/>
, Glasgow, 10 September until 29 October.

Marcus Harvey
Grotesque images of British history and identity make perfect art for a Brexit 
summer’s end.
• Jerwood Gallery <http://www.jerwoodgallery.org/whatson/42/inselaffe>, 
Hastings, until 16 October.
