
The Guardian has won six awards at this year’s Foreign Press Association Media 
Awards <http://www.fpalondon.net/#!media-awards/cs07> for a series of long 
reads.

The FPA Media Awards <https://www.theguardian.com/gnm-press-office/awards> 
honour excellence in journalism. Now in its sixteenth year, the awards provide 
the opportunity for British journalists to be recognised by their international 
peers.


Martin Chulov won Print and Web Feature Story of the Year for Isis: the inside 
story <https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/dec/11/-sp-isis-the-inside-story> 
and also took home the award for Journalist of the Year. This is the third time 
since 2010 that a Guardian journalist has won the Journalist of the Year award.


David Goldblatt picked up Sports Story of the Year for his long read on a 
Ugandan prison’s football league 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2015/may/28/the-prison-where-murderers-play-for-manchester-united>
. New York-based Oliver Burkeman won Science Story of the Year for his long 
read onthe mystery of consciousness 
<https://www.theguardian.com/science/2015/jan/21/-sp-why-cant-worlds-greatest-minds-solve-mystery-consciousness>
. Sam Knight picked up Financial/Economics Story of the Year for his piece on
Serco’s struggle for survival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/business/2015/jul/02/serco-rupert-soames-outsourcing-privatisation>
. Travel/Tourism Story of the Year went to Christopher de Bellaigue for his 
Lost in Translation long read.


The full list of winners can be found here 
<http://www.fpalondon.net/#!media-awards/cs07>.
