
Guardian special projects editor James Ball has been named joint winner in the 
Print category of the RSS Statistical Excellence Awards for his article on the
Guardian Datastore <https://www.theguardian.com/data>, 'The Thatcher effect: 
what changed and what stayed the same 
<https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2013/apr/12/thatcher-britain>.'

Judges praised the article for the fair, unbiased and accessible manner in 
which the article sought to examine the lasting impact of Margaret Thatcher 
using an analysis of contemporary and historical statistical data.

The RSS Statistical Excellence Awards celebrate statistical excellence in 
journalism, official statistics and in the pharmaceutical industry. Read the 
full press release announcing the winnershere 
<http://www.statslife.org.uk/news/1627-society-announces-2014-winners-of-excellence-awards>
.
