
Mark Townsend, home affairs editor for The Observer 
<http://observer.theguardian.com/>, has received a Human Trafficking Foundation 
Media Award <http://www.humantraffickingfoundation.org/htf-in-the-news/> for 
his article onsex trafficking 
<https://www.theguardian.com/law/2011/jul/02/odessa-ukraine-sex-trafficking-investigation>
.

The awards, part of the wider Anti-Slavery Day 
<http://www.antislavery.org/english/antislavery_day/> programme, recognise the 
work done by the media to raise awareness of human trafficking in the UK. 
Winners of the inaugural awards were presented with their accolades by 
Immigration Minister Damian Green MP at the House of Lords yesterday.

To read more, click here <http://www.humantraffickingfoundation.org/>.
