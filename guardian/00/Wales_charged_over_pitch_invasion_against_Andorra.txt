
Uefa has started disciplinary proceedings against the Football Association of 
Wales for the pitch invasion following Gareth Bale’s late winner againstAndorra 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/andorra> on Tuesday night.

Bale’s free-kick was greeted by Wales fans racing on to the pitch to celebrate 
the decisive goal inWales’ 2-1 Euro 2016 qualifying victory 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/2014/sep/09/andorra-wales-euro-2016-qualifier-match-report>
.

“Disciplinary proceedings have been opened following the European qualifier 
Group B match between Andorra andWales 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/wales> played on Tuesday 9 September in 
Andorra,” Uefa said in a statement. The case will be heard on 16 October. The 
FAW said it was not commenting on the investigation.

Under Uefa’s disciplinary regulations there are a wide range of punishments 
available include a warning, a fine and a points deduction.

Wales’s next two Euro 2016 qualifiers 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/euro-2016-qualifiers> are both at home in 
October, against Bosnia-Herzegovina and Cyprus.

Andorra did not escape action either, with the Andorran Football Federation 
facing action for exceeding five yellow cards in the match.
