
A United Kingdom <https://www.theguardian.com/film/a-united-kingdom>, the 
story of the real-life relationship between an African king and the English 
office worker he married in 1947, has been selected as the opening film of the 
London international film festival.

Starring David Oyelowo as Seretse Khama 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2015/may/27/david-oyelowo-rosamund-pike-african-king-marriage-british-woman-seretse-khama-ruth-williams>
, who in 1925 became king of Bechuanaland (then a British protectorate, now the 
republic of Botswana) aged four, and Rosamund Pike asRuth Williams 
<https://www.theguardian.com/news/2002/may/29/guardianobituaries>, the Londoner 
who married him despite considerable controversy at the highest political 
levels, both in apartheid South Africa and in the UK, A United Kingdom is 
directed by Amma Asante, the British director who won a string of awards for 
her previous film,Belle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/jun/15/belle-review-costume-drama-teeth-slavery>
.

After their marriage, Khama and Williams were forced to live in exile in the 
UK, but were allowed to settle in Bechuanaland in 1956, after popular demand 
culminated in the sending of a telegram to the young Queen Elizabeth II. Khama 
gave up his royal status, and was elected the first president of an independent 
Botswana in 1966. The couple had four children, before Khama died in 1980 and
his wife in 2002 
<https://www.theguardian.com/news/2002/may/29/guardianobituaries>.

The film is billed as having a European premiere in London in October 
<http://www.bfi.org.uk/lff>, implying it is likely to have its world premiere 
at Venice or Toronto film festivals months before.
