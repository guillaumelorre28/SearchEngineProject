Full-time: Wales 2-0 Andorra

Wales end their qualifying campaign in the way they started it, beating Andorra
 <https://www.theguardian.com/football/andorra>. But it’s the stuff they did in 
between that was most remarkable and explains why this final whistle will be 
followed by an era-defining party. Next stop: France, where no one will fancy 
facing this spirited and skilful team.

Preamble:

Hello, welcome, and help yourselves to a drink and some grub. For this is not 
a football match, it’s a party! Wales are going to the European Championships 
for the first time and nothing that happens in Cardiff tonight can change that! 
Unless they do something mad like give the Super Furry Animals a game and then 
get docked loads of points for fielding ineligible players, but no, Chris 
Coleman is surely on top of things on that front, and this is a newWales 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/wales>, no rotten luck, no 
self-destruction, no more nearly men: they’re off to a major tournament for the 
first time since 1958! Wales!

All the Super Furry Animals will be doing, then, is entertaining the crowd 
with some some pre-match drum and axe work. And the Welsh FA have teased the 
nation by saying there will be a “special surprise” after the game to add to 
the fun. Feel free to send your suggestions as to what that might be. As for
Andorra <https://www.theguardian.com/football/andorra>, all they are requested 
to do is refrain from injuring anyone, especially the real Prince of Wales, who 
would be an irreplaceable loss for both country and Real Madrid.

Wales were made to fight for an awkward 2-1 win on an atrocious pitch in 
Andorra way back at the start of the campaign, but wouldn’t it be nice if 
things went more smoothly tonight and they banged in a load of goals to give a 
bumper crowd more to cheer about and, indeed, improve a record of scoring just 
nine goals in nine qualifiers so far? Not that that is the most important

Wales end their qualifying campaign in the way they started it, beating Andorra
 <https://www.theguardian.com/football/andorra>. But it’s the stuff they did in 
between that was most remarkable and explains why this final whistle will be 
followed by an era-defining party. Next stop: France, where no one will fancy 
facing this spirited and skilful team.
 
And there’s the biggest roar of the night! Davies did a fair Bale impression 
by bombing down the left and pinging in a wicked low cross. Bale swung at it, 
didn’t make a great connection, but swept the ball past a rather lame dive by

Wales <https://www.theguardian.com/football/wales>, by the way, did more than 
just reach a major tournament in 1958. They made it out of their group thanks 
to a play-off win over a fine Hungary side, and only lost in the quarter-final 
1-0 to Brazil, a match for which John Charles, whose influence was equivalent 
to that of Bale on the current team, was injured. Have a gander at the goals 
from that play-off win over Hungary, featuring a marvellous volley from Ivor 
Allchurch and a rather harsh bollocking dished out by the goalkeeper afterwards.

a football match, it’s a party! Wales are going to the European Championships

get docked loads of points for fielding ineligible players, but no, Chris

All the Super Furry Animals will be doing, then, is entertaining the crowd

Andorra <https://www.theguardian.com/football/andorra>, all they are requested

Wales were made to fight for an awkward 2-1 win on an atrocious pitch in

to be pretty much how the Welsh spent their days at the end of qualifying 
campaigns. But not now. What a time to be a Welsh footballer or fan!
