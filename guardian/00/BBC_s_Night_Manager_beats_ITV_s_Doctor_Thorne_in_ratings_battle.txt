
BBC spy thriller The Night Manager easily saw off the challenge of ITV drama 
Doctor Thorne <https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/doctor-thorne>, touted 
as the new Downton Abbey, to win the battle for the 9pm Sunday night slot.

The £20m adaptation of John Le Carré’s spy thriller, which stars Tom 
Hiddleston, Olivia Colman andTom Hollander 
<https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/tom-hollander>, drew an average of 
6.2 million viewers.

Viewing was down slightly on the previous week 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2016/feb/29/channel-4-beats-itv-ratings-for-all-shows-across-whole-day>
, however the BBC1 drama still drew 27.9% share of audience between 9pm and 
10pm. It was easily enough to see off the debut of ITV’s Doctor Thorne, an 
adaptation of Anthony Trollope’s novel by Downton Abbey creator Julian 
Fellowes, which drew 3.8 million viewers.

The drama, which also stars Hollander, pulled in a 17% share of total TV 
viewing between 9pm and 10pm.

Sunday night also saw the final in the latest series of celebrity winter 
sports reality show The Jump, which has beendogged by controversy 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2016/feb/08/channel-4-launches-safety-review-jump-celebrity-casualties>
 following a string of accidents and picked up an average of 1.8 million 
viewers.

BBC2’s new drama series Thirteen, about a woman who escapes after being held 
captive in a cellar for 13 years, premiered with an average of 900,000 viewers 
and a 5.8% share of total TV audience between 10pm and 11pm.
