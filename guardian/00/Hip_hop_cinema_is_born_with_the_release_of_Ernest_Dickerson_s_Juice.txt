
Spike Lee's third film, Do the Right Thing, released in 1989, had brought the 
sensibilities of the hip-hop era to the multiplex and ushered in a new wave of 
black film-making. Hollywood began to give black directors decent budgets and 
started casting rappers as leads. AfterIce Cube 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/icecube> excelled in John Singleton's Boyz N 
the Hood and Ice-T impressed in New Jack City, Ernest Dickerson – 
Lee's long-time cinematographer – topped the lot with the superb Juice, 
starring Tupac Shakur in an eerily prophetic role.
