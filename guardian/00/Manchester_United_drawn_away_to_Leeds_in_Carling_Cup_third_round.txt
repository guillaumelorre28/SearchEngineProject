
Manchester United <https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchester-united> 
have been drawn away to old rivals Leeds in the third round of the Carling Cup. 
Sir Alex Ferguson will be looking to avenge the 1-0 FA Cup defeat to the same 
side two years ago at Old Trafford when the Championship team were in League 
One.

Brighton, following their fine start to life in the Championship, were drawn 
at home toLiverpool <https://www.theguardian.com/football/liverpool>.

Holders Birmingham, entering the competition at this stage because of their 
involvement in the Europa League, begin the defence of the trophy they won 
against Arsenal with a trip to face the FA Cup winnersManchester City 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/manchestercity>.

There are four all-Premier League ties with Chelsea at home to Fulham, Aston 
Villa <https://www.theguardian.com/football/aston-villa> at home to Bolton, 
Stoke taking on Tottenham and Everton welcoming West Brom.

Shrewsbury's reward for their second-round defeat of Swansea is a trip to 
Arsenal, whileNottingham Forest 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/nottinghamforest> will hope to upset 
Newcastle at the City Ground.

Rochdale, winners against QPR, will visit either Aldershot or Carlisle 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/carlisle> who have yet to play their 
second-round tie as a consequence of the riots which caused numerous 
first-round matches to be put back.
