
Amanda Holden's efforts to become a midwife drew more than 4 million people to 
ITV1 on Monday 14 December.

Out of My Depth, which followed the actor and Britain's Got Talent judge as 
she trained to join the team of a hospital delivery room, was watched by 4.1 
million, an 18% share of the audience between 8pm and 9pm, according to 
unofficial overnight ratings.

It was not enough to beat BBC1's David Attenborough series Life, which ended 
its 10-part run by looking at primates, drawing 5 million viewers and a 22% 
share.

The second part of Man on Earth, Channel 4's new Tony Robinson series about 
climate change, had 1.3 million viewers and 6%, with another 160,000 tuning in 
an hour later on digital catchup service Channel 4 +1.

BBC2's art series School of Saatchi ended its four-part run with 900,000 
viewers and a 4% share at the same time.

Channel Five's movie repeat Bad Santa attracted 1.6 million viewers and an 8% 
share between 9pm and 11.05pm.

BBC2's sitcom Miranda finished its first series with 2.6 million viewers and 
an 11% share between 8.30pm and 9pm. Miranda has been commissioned for a second 
series.

BBC1's Panorama, on the Royal Mail, picked up 3.4 million viewers and a 14% 
share in the same half-hour slot.

The early part of the evening was dominated by the soaps, starting with ITV1's 
Emmerdale, which drew 7.1 million and 35% at 7pm.

At 7.30pm, Coronation Street pulled in 9.4 million and 42%, the night's 
biggest audience, with 8.6 million and 35% tuning in for the 8.30pm instalment.

At 8pm, BBC1's EastEnders picked up 8.7 million viewers and a 37% share.

Christmas at River Cottage, featuring Hugh Fearnley-Whittingstall, brought 1.8 
million and a 7% share to Channel 4, with another 333,000 on Channel 4 +1.

On multichannel, the opening parts of Move like Michael Jackson, BBC3's hunt 
for dancers to perform at the late star's memorial service, pulled in 637,000 
and 646,000 viewers, a 2.8% and 3.3% share respectively, in two hour-long parts 
starting at 8.30pm and 9.30pm.
