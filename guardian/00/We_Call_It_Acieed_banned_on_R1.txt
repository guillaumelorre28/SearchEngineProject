
Even though the distinctive chorus of D-Mob's hit ("Acieeed! Acieeed! 
Acieeed!") seemed to be praising the virtues of LSD, it was very much the 
E panic kicking in at the tabloids with a rush of headlines like "SPACED OUT! 
11,000 youngsters go drug-crazy at Britain's biggest-ever Acid party" or "£12 
TRIP TO AN EVIL NIGHT OF ECSTASY" that pushed the BBC to remove it from their 
playlist. Not before Caron Keating got to introduce it on Top of the Pops with 
an enthusiastic "acieeed!" of her own though.
