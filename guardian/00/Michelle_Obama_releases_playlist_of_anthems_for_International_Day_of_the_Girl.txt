
Michelle Obama <https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/michelleobama> was 
grooving to the tunes of Beyoncé, Demi Lovato and Esperanza Spalding as she 
celebrated the International Day of the Girl on Sunday.

The first lady tweeted Sunday a link to a “girl power” music playlist 
<https://player.spotify.com/user/thewhitehouse/playlist/3R2DGCLfGdVqqqN6rdQYr5> 
on the streaming music platform Spotify. The United Nations has designated 11 
October to promote awareness of gender inequality around the world.

Obama’s playlist includes pop hits like Alicia Keys’ Girl on Fire and Bang 
Bang by Jessie J, Ariana Grande and Nicki Minaj. The first lady noted 
sisterhood with songs by girl groups, including TLC’s No Scrubs and Survivor by 
Destiny’s Child. The playlist also included soulful tunes byAretha Franklin 
<https://www.theguardian.com/music/aretha-franklin>, Diana Ross and Billie 
Holiday.

One song on the list, Confident by Demi Lovato, carries a warning of explicit 
lyrics <http://www.azlyrics.com/lyrics/demilovato/confident.html>.

Obama was promoting her Let Girls Learn campaign, which aims to expand access 
to education 
<https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/video/2015/jun/16/michelle-obama-seeks-inspire-girls-east-london-school-video>
 for more than 62 million girls around the world and encourages American girls 
to take advantage of their opportunities.

Barack Obama released Spotify playlists while vacationing on Martha’s Vineyard 
over the summer. His daytime and evening playlists included tracks by Justin 
Timberlake, Stevie Wonder, Bob Dylan, Mary J Blige, Frank Sinatra and Lauryn 
Hill.
