
Michael Haneke's Oscar contender Amour 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/147440/amour> was the big winner at the 
London Critics' Circle film awards last night, taking three of the top prizes, 
including film of the year.

The Palme D'Or winner, which is nominated for five Academy awards, also won 
screenplay of the year for Haneke's French-language script about an elderly 
couple dealing with the aftermath of a debilitating stroke.Emmanuelle Riva 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/nov/08/emmanuelle-riva-amour-michael-haneke>
 was named actress of the year for her lead portrayal.

Another big winner at the Mayfair Hotel in London was Paul Thomas Anderson's 
The Master <https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/146341/master>, which has 
had a relatively disappointing awards season so far. The period drama took best 
actor for Joaquin Phoenix and best supporting actor for Philip Seymour Hoffman, 
both of whom are also nominated for Oscars. Anne Hathaway won best supporting 
actress for her Golden Globe-winning turn as Fantine inTom Hooper's Les 
Miserables <https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/150107/miserables>, which is 
up for eight Academy awards. Meanwhile, Helena Bonham Carter, who also appears 
in the musical, was honoured with the Dilys Powell award for excellence in film.

Elsewhere, Ang Lee took the prize for director of the year for his film Life 
of Pi <https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/149778/life-of-pi>, based on Yann 
Martel's Booker prize-winning novel. The fantasy adventure, which is nominated 
for 11 Oscars, also won a technical award for its use of 3D special effects.
Jacques Audiard's Rust and Bone 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/146658/rust-and-bone> won the prize for 
best foreign-language film.

Winners in the British film categories included Alice Lowe and Steve Oram 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/video/2012/nov/28/sightseers-alice-lowe-steve-oram-video>
, who were named breakthrough British film-makers for their screenwriting work 
onBen Wheatley's comedy horror Sightseers 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/nov/29/sightseers-review>, while Toby 
Jones was named British actor of the year for psychological thrillerBerberian 
Sound Studio 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/aug/30/berberian-sound-studio-review>. 
Andrea Riseborough took British actress of the year for herportrayal of an IRA 
informant in Shadow Dancer 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/aug/23/shadow-dancer-review>, and Tom 
Holland 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/2012/dec/23/young-actor-tom-holland-16> 
was named young British performer of the year for his turn in tsunami drama The 
Impossible.

Meanwhile, Michael Haneke's film carried off the main prizes at the Lumiere 
awards 
<http://www.screendaily.com/news/amour-continues-winning-streak-at-french-lumire-awards/5050814.article>
, the French equivalent of the Golden Globes, voted for by non-French critics. 
Amour won best film, while actors Riva and Jean-Louis Trintignant won best 
actress and best actor. However, Haneke was beaten to best director by Jacques 
Audiard for Rust and Bone, which also took best screenplay.
