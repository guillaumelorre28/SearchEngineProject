
Ant and Dec <https://www.theguardian.com/tv-and-radio/ant-and-dec> made more 
than £7m last year from presenting hit shows including Britain’s Got Talent and 
I’m a Celebrity … Get Me Out of Here last year.

The duo, whose £10m a year “golden handcuffs” deal with ITV is up for renewal 
this year 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2013/nov/22/ant-and-dec-sign-itv-deal>, made 
£7.07m from the three companies through which they funnel their TV business 
activities.

Declan Donnelly <https://www.theguardian.com/media/declandonnelly> made £3.38m 
in the year to the end of June, taking his total shareholder funds at his 
company Deecourt to £12.4m, according to Companies House filings.

He also made just under £3m in investments and benefitted from the disposal of 
an unnamed asset that made him £1.76m.

His presenting partner Anthony McPartlin 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/anthonymcpartlin> made £3.3m according to 
the financial filing for his company Teecourt, taking his total shareholder 
funds to £12.3m.

They are also joint directors of Hurley Promotions, which made £376,000 in the 
year to the end of October.
