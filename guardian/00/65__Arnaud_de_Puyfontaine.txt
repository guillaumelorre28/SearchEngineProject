Arnaud de Puyfontaine <https://www.theguardian.com/media/arnaud-de-puyfontaine>
 oversees US publisher Hearst's UK interests, spanning 22 titles including 
Elle, Men's Health, Harper's Bazaar and Esquire.

His responsibilities grew last year with the publishing giant's $651m purchase 
<http://www.nytimes.com/2011/02/01/business/media/01lagardere.html> of the 
Hachette Filipacchi magazine division of the French media company Lagardere.

Since the merger, Hearst – which before the Hachette Filipacchi deal operated 
under the National Magazine Company umbrella in the UK 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/2007/jul/09/mediatop1002007.mondaymediasection58>
 – has shut some titles (She, Cosmopolitan Bride) sold others (You & Your 
Wedding, Prima Baby & Pregnancy) and launched new ones, with theUK debut of 
Men's Health spin-off, Women's Health 
<https://www.theguardian.com/media/mediamonkeyblog/2012/feb/03/womens-health-magazine-launch?INTCMP=SRCH>
.

UK chief executive since 2009, de Puyfontaine previously worked at Italian 
media group Mondadori, Emap France and Le Figaro. He was appointed as executive 
vice-president of Hearst Magazines International last year.

"He's trying to 'out Coleridge' [Condé Nast UK boss] Nicholas Coleridge," said 
our panel. He hasn't managed it in this year's list. Maybe next time.
