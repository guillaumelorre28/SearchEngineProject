The experienced Sunderland defender O’Shea, his country’s third-most capped 
player, and theWest Bromwich Albion 
<https://www.theguardian.com/football/westbrom> midfielder McClean had been 
doubts for Saturday’s World Cup qualifier in Austria because of hamstring and 
back injuries respectively, and the presence of both among the party which left 
Dublin on Thursday afternoon came as a relief to O’Neill.

However, only 27 of the 28 squad members made the trip as the Leeds midfielder 
Eunan O’Kane returned to his club with a groin problem.

A Football Association of Ireland statement said: “The travelling squad to 
Vienna consisted of 27 players in total, including Dundalk duo Andy Boyle and 
Daryl Horgan.”
