
Clever politicians had sought to control rave culture with a nudge, starting 
with Luton MP Graham Bright's Private Member's Bill, which led to the 
fines-based Entertainment Act 1990. But after Castlemorton , ravers joined CND, 
miners and travellers as nonconformists targeted by the Tory government. So the 
new Criminal Justice Act strengthened trespass law and even defined as illegal 
gatherings of more than 20 people listening to "repetitive beats".
