
A film crew working on director Alfonso Cuarón’s latest movie say they have 
been assaulted and robbed while filming on a street in downtownMexico 
<https://www.theguardian.com/world/mexico> City. 


In a letter reported by Deadline 
<http://deadline.com/2016/11/alfonso-cuaron-movie-fight-crew-mexico-city-police-1201847622/>
, the production team said they were working with official permits from the 
city’s film commission when they were assaulted. Cuarón was not on the set at 
the time.

“It was not a ‘simple fight’ like the authorities have reported,” the letter 
read. “[I]t was assault and there were people injured: two women were hit; five 
of our crew members were taken to the hospital; and phones, wallets and 
jewellery were stolen.

“After we filed the complaint, the response of the district was immediate and 
positive. We hope that the district authorities will keep their promise and 
help compensate for the stolen items, as well as bringing those responsible to 
justice.”

According to El País, who have obtained a video 
<http://internacional.elpais.com/internacional/2016/11/02/actualidad/1478117455_982295.html>
 purporting to show the altercation, the city’s police department denied the 
crew had been robbed but the state prosecutor confirmed that some of the crew 
had been stripped of their personal belongings. The crew has filed a complaint 
with authorities.

Cuarón, who won the best director Oscar in 2014 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2014/mar/03/alfonso-cuaron-wins-best-director-oscar>
 for his film Gravity, is making his first film in Mexico in 15 years. No title 
has been announced, but Participant media says the movie is about “a year in 
the life of a middle-class family in Mexico City in the early 1970s”.
