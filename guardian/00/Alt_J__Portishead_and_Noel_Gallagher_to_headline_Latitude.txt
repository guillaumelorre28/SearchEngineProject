
Alt-J, Portishead and Noel Gallager’s High Flying Birds have been announced as 
the headliners for this year’sLatitude festival 
<https://www.theguardian.com/culture/latitudefestival>, which takes place from 
16-19 July at Henham Park in Suffolk.

A strong undercard on the main stage in the Obelisk arena sees the headliners 
joined by Manic Street Preachers, Caribou, James Blake, Laura Marling and Wild 
Beasts, among others.

The headliners for the BBC 6 Music stage, meanwhile, are Jon Hopkins, the 
Vaccines and Sbtrkt, with Django Django, Public Service Broadcasting, Catfish 
and the Bottlemen, La Roux, Young Fathers and Years & Years appearing down the 
bill.

None of the main stage headliners have topped the bill at Latitude before, 
thoughAlt-J <https://www.theguardian.com/music/alt-j> have on the Lake Stage in 
2012 and closed the show on the 6 Music Stage on the Saturday of the 2013 
festival.

There are plenty of acts to intrigue on the i Arena stage, too, with 
experimental types Adult Jazz, Norwegian pop queen Susanne Sundfør, Aussie 
psychedelicists King Gizzard and the Lizard Wizard and retro soulster Leon 
Bridges all appearing.
