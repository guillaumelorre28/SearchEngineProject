
Family drama Broken <https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/147439/broken>, 
the feature-film debut from award-winning theatre and opera director Rufus 
Norris, has been named best film of 2012 at theBritish independent film awards 
<http://www.bifa.org.uk/winners/2012>.

Starring Tim Roth  
<http://film.theguardian.com/Player/Player_Page/0,,42325,00.html>as a father 
looking after an 11-year-old daughter (newcomer Eloise Laurence) after his wife 
leaves him, the film also won best supporting actor for Rory Kinnear. The 
Guardian's Peter Bradshawcriticised the film  
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/may/18/broken-review>as "strained, 
desperately self-conscious and replete with unconvincing and unearned emotional 
crises" at Cannes in May but praised Laurence for her turn as the likeable 
Skunk.

The other big winner at the Bifas was Peter Strickland's period psychological 
thrillerBerberian Sound Studio 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/148212/berberian-sound-studio>, which 
won best director, best actor (for Toby Jones), best achievement in production 
and best technical achievement for Joakim Sundström and Stevie Haywood's sound 
design. The film is set in the Italian studios of a 70s horror film production 
and has been called "seriously weird and seriously good" byBradshaw, who also 
said 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/aug/30/berberian-sound-studio-review> it 
marks Strickland's emergence as "a key British film-maker of his generation".

Best actress went to Andrea Riseborough for her turn as an Irish republican 
terrorist turned reluctant informer in James Marsh'sShadow Dancer 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/145818/shadow-dancer>, while Olivia 
Colman won her second Bifa in as many years (best supporting actress) for her 
performance as Queen consort Elizabeth in Roger Michell's Hyde Park on Hudson. 
Last year she won best actress for her role in Paddy Considine'sTyrannosaur 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/142849/tyrannosaur>.

Elsewhere, most promising newcomer went to My Brother the Devil's James Floyd, 
while best documentary went to Bart Layton's conman exposéThe Imposter 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/movie/145816/imposter>. The Hunt, from 
Denmark's Thomas Vinterberg, was named best international independent film.
