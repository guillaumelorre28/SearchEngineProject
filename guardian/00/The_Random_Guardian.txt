
Daniel Vydra explains his experiment on the InsideGuardian blog 
<https://www.theguardian.com/help/insideguardian/2010/mar/26/random-guardian>:


"During a presentation yesterday about the future of news media with Clay 
Shirky, the idea of "a chatroulette for news" was jokingly mentioned...

This is the result: http://random-guardian.appspot.com 
<http://random-guardian.appspot.com>

...this is the product of about 3 hours of hacking...It was written in Python 
<http://www.python.org/>, pulling data from our content API 
<https://www.theguardian.com/open-platform>, and running on Google app engine 
<http://code.google.com/appengine>."

Try adding a section name at the end of the URL to see random articles from 
today on that topic such ashttp://random-guardian.appspot.com/random/media 
<http://random-guardian.appspot.com/random/media>. It's frighteningly addictive.
