
Writer and artist Alasdair Gray 
<https://www.theguardian.com/books/alasdairgray> has been seriously injured 
after a fall at his home. An ambulance was called to the 80-year-old’s house in 
the West End of Glasgow at around 2am on Thursday, and he was taken to hospital 
for treatment.

He is best known as author of the novel Lanark, but is also regarded for his 
artwork – including a series of murals.

A Police Scotland <https://www.theguardian.com/uk/scotland> spokesperson said: 
“At 1.50am this morning, police received a report of an 80-year-old man having 
been injured at a property in Glasgow. An ambulance attended and the man was 
taken to Glasgow Royal Infirmary for treatment. His injuries are not believed 
to be life threatening at this time and inquiries are continuing.”
