
James Cameron is to partner with Cirque du Soleil for a touring live arena 
show inspired by the blockbuster film Avatar. The project was announced on 
Thursday by Cameron and Daniel Lamarre, CEO of Cirque du Soleil. It is expected 
tobegin touring in late 2015 
<http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/james-cameron-cirque-du-soleil-707862> 
ahead of the release of the first of three planned sequels to Avatar in 
December 2016.

Cameron and Cirque du Soleil previously partnered on the 2012 3D documentary 
Cirque du Soleil: Worlds Away, on which the Canadian film-maker was a producer. 
"Over the years, I have discovered the extraordinary talents and imaginations 
of both the artists and the creative forces behindCirque du Soleil 
<https://www.theguardian.com/stage/cirque-du-soleil>," said Cameron, who made 
the announcement at the C2MTL Commerce and Creativity Conference in Montreal. 
"I know we share the common goal of bringing audiences to another level of 
entertainment experiences. I look forward to doing just that on this project."

Twentieth Century Fox, which made Avatar, will also work on the project. 
Chairman and CEO of the studio, Jim Gianopulos, said: "As Jim Cameron prepares 
to take us to even greater heights with three newAvatar 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/avatar> films, we're thrilled with the 
innovative experience he's developing in this groundbreaking agreement with 
Cirque du Soleil."

Cameron is currently in pre-production on three Avatar sequels 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2012/jun/27/james-cameron-three-avatar-sequels>
. Star Sam Worthington said last year that the movies will begin shooting in 
October 2014 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2013/oct/21/avatar-sequels-begin-shooting>. 
They are due to hit cinemas between December 2016 and December 2018, with 
Cameron hoping to repeat the spectacular success of thehighest-grossing film of 
all time 
<https://www.theguardian.com/film/2010/jan/26/avatar-sinks-titanic-record>. 
Released in 2009, Avatar has made $2.7bn worldwide.
