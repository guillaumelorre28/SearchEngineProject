
This year's Meltdown festival at the Southbank Centre in London is curated by 
the musician and visual artistAntony Hegarty 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2012/mar/14/antony-hegarty-meltdown-2012>. He 
first emerged from the New York underground scene with Antony and the Johnsons 
in 1998 and shot to prominence with the Mercury Prize-winning albumI am a Bird 
Now <http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/album/63373d83-a96e-355c-aa44-837db92c2193>
.

Since then, critical acclaim has followed each of his projects including the 
albumsThe Crying Light 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/album/009f05b6-06ed-360e-95ea-4378bc548957> and
Swanlights 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/album/c653c21b-4a52-4fbc-a4dc-3767f99ef660>. 
And he's collaborated with several other musicians and performance artists.

To mark his Meltdown, the Southbank Center has put together the playlist 
below, which you can listen to if you have Spotify. It features several arists 
already announced for the festival, including the ex-Cocteau TwinElizabeth 
Fraser 
<http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2012/may/01/cocteau-twins-elizabeth-fraser-meltdown>
.

Past curators of Meltdown include Nick Cave, John Peel, Laurie Anderson, Patti 
Smith, David Bowie and, most recently, Ray Davies, each of them hand-picking 
their favourite artists to play and perform.
