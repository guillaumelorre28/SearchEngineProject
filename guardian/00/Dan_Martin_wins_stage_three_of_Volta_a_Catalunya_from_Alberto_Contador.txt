
The Birmingham-born Irishman Dan Martin won stage three of the Volta a 
Catalunya on Wednesday to seize the race lead. The 29-year-old finished the 
172km stage from Girona to the summit at La Molina ahead of a stellar field, 
withAlberto Contador <https://www.theguardian.com/sport/alberto-contador> 
(Tinkoff) second and Romain Bardet (AG2R La Mondiale) third.

The Tour de France champion, Chris Froome 
<https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2015/jul/26/chris-froome-tour-de-france-second-win-legends>
 (Team Sky), was eighth, one place ahead of another Briton, Hugh Carthy (Caja 
Rural-Seguros RGA).

Martin holds a six-second overall lead from Contador, while Froome is 22 
seconds behind in seventh and his Team Sky colleague Geraint Thomas is ninth in 
the race, which finishes on Sunday.
